# Written Statement — Issued Immediately After Day One Speech

**For release:** Upon conclusion of the President’s address to the nation and the world.  
**No byline.** Issued as a statement from the White House.

---

The President’s first act in office was to offer peace — not as a reward, but as the condition. We will meet the condition. We will put down the blade. We will see the face, not the category. That was his word to the American people and to the world. It stands.

**Tomorrow** the President will take the next step: he will ask every federal employee to bind their conduct to the same standard. An ethics pledge — face not category, public interest first — will be issued to the executive branch. The President and his senior staff will sign it. We will ask the rest of the federal workforce to sign it as well. The details will be announced tomorrow.

**Today** the President is also extending an invitation.

He is asking **Elon Musk** to design a **shadow federal government**: a full blueprint of how to deliver everything the federal government currently does — every function, every mission — at a fraction of the cost. Not a commission. Not a report that sits on a shelf. A working design. A parallel government on paper: same outputs, radically lower cost. Technology, process, structure, and accountability — all of it. When it is done, Congress will receive it. Congress will then vote: adopt the shadow government, in whole or in part, or do not. The choice stays with the people’s representatives. A side effect of the exercise is that we put a serious, concrete option on the table to solve the deficit — not by cutting promises in the dark, but by showing what it would take to keep every promise at a fraction of what we spend today.

The President does not assume Mr. Musk will say yes. He is inviting him. The offer is public. The door is open.

---

*Issued immediately following the President’s address. No questions.*
