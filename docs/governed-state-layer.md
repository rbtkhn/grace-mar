# Governed State Layer

The **Governed State Layer** is **durable companion state** that affects future operation: what the companion **is**, **knows**, and **commits to**, under human sovereignty and protocol.

**Examples:**

- Approved **seed-phase** outputs at activation  
- **Record** contents after gate-approved merge (see [concept.md](concept.md))  
- Approved **change-review** outcomes  
- Durable operating, pedagogical, and safety commitments encoded in governed files  

---

## Rules

- Material changes require **visible review** when the protocol says so (gate, change review, activation sign-off).
- Prior state should be **preserved or diffable**; avoid silent overwrite of important fields.
- **Contradictions** should be explicit (multiple versions, tension notes) rather than flattened away.

**Not governed state:** raw evidence, ephemeral memory, unstaged analyst drafts, or prepared context that has not been merged through a governed path.

See [state-model.md](state-model.md), [change-review.md](change-review.md), [Identity Fork Protocol](identity-fork-protocol.md).
