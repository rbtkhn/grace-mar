# Companion Self Doctrine Memo

**Status:** canonical outward-facing framing

**Purpose:** provide one clean source text for explaining Companion Self to collaborators, investors, operators, and future product materials.

**Relationship:** Grace-Mar is the first live instance and proving ground. `companion-self` is the reusable template and public-facing product frame.

---

## Core Thesis

Companion Self is identity infrastructure for the agentic era.

As AI systems become persistent, agentic, and deeply personalized, the central question is no longer only what models can do. The central question is **who they serve, what they are allowed to treat as true, and who controls the memory they act from**. Companion Self answers that question with a governed architecture: a portable Record, a queryable Voice, and a human gate that decides what becomes durable identity.

This is not a character bot, a productivity shell, or a vendor-owned memory layer. It is a companion-owned, evidence-grounded, versioned self that can be queried, extended, and carried across tools without surrendering authorship.

---

## What Companion Self Is

Companion Self is a system for building a **documented self** that remains under human authority.

Its core claim is simple:

- identity should be structured
- important claims should be evidence-linked
- memory updates should require human approval
- the resulting Record should be portable across platforms and agents

The system is designed for a world in which many AIs may speak, recommend, plan, and act around a person. Companion Self supplies the missing substrate those systems do not natively provide: a trustworthy identity layer that the companion can inspect, govern, and export.

---

## Record, Voice, Gate

Companion Self is built on a tricameral model:

- **Mind**: the human companion, sovereign and conscious
- **Record**: the documented self, stored as structured identity plus evidence
- **Voice**: the queryable interface that speaks from the Record when asked

The gate is the constitutional boundary between staging and truth.

Agents may stage candidates. They may not merge them. The human companion, or an explicitly delegated human, decides what crosses into the Record. This is the Sovereign Merge Rule. It is not a UX preference; it is the core safety, trust, and authorship mechanism of the system.

Without the gate, personalized AI becomes soft capture: systems infer, persist, and operationalize identity without durable consent. With the gate, identity remains companion-owned.

---

## SELF, SKILLS, WORK

Companion Self distinguishes between three kinds of structured surface:

- **SELF**: who the companion is. This includes self-knowledge, self-curiosity, and self-personality.
- **SKILLS**: what the Record can evidence the companion can do. In the current architecture these Record-bound skill modules are **THINK** and **WRITE**.
- **WORK**: adjacent execution territory. Planning, making, delivery, and tool-using work may use the full power of models and systems, but it does not become Record truth automatically.

This distinction matters.

SELF is identity.
SKILLS are evidenced capability inside the Record.
WORK is execution context.

The system must not collapse these into one undifferentiated memory heap. If it does, identity becomes noisy, capability becomes inflated, and work traces start masquerading as selfhood. Companion Self keeps those layers legible so that autonomy can grow without losing authorship.

---

## Governance Over Fluency

Most AI products compete on fluency: smoother chat, faster answers, more natural tone, more initiative.

Companion Self treats **governance** as the more important primitive.

The right question is not only whether a system sounds convincing. The right question is:

- what is it allowed to know
- how did that knowledge get there
- can the human inspect or reject the update
- can the identity be moved somewhere else intact

In this model, fluency is downstream of governance. The Voice should be warm, helpful, and natural, but never at the cost of provenance, boundary discipline, or human authority.

This is why the system enforces:

- evidence linkage
- calibrated abstention outside the documented boundary
- explicit staging instead of silent memory writes
- audit trails for staged, approved, rejected, and applied changes

Companion Self is built on the belief that trust will matter more than charm in agentic systems that persist over time.

---

## Why Portability Matters

Today, most personalized AI context is trapped inside products. A platform may know the most about a learner, creator, patient, or customer, but that knowledge is not carried forward under the person's control. It is inferred, siloed, and owned elsewhere.

Companion Self treats portability as a first-order right.

The Record should be exportable, inspectable, and reusable across systems:

- new schools
- new tutors
- new agents
- new interfaces
- future archives and legacy contexts

Portability is not only convenience. It is protection against lock-in, loss of narrative control, and forced re-onboarding of identity. In an agent economy, the portable Record becomes the stable layer that many systems can read without any one system being allowed to own the self.

---

## Why This Matters Now

AI is moving from chat to long-lived agentic systems. Those systems will increasingly plan, remember, recommend, and act. If their memory layer remains vendor-owned and weakly governed, users will inherit highly capable systems with weak identity controls.

Companion Self is designed as a counter-architecture:

- personal instead of platform-owned
- evidence-grounded instead of inferred
- gated instead of auto-written
- portable instead of trapped
- durable instead of session-fragmented

This makes it relevant far beyond one use case. Education is a strong entry point because learner continuity, provenance, and trust are urgent there. But the underlying architecture generalizes to any domain where identity must persist across tools without being surrendered to them.

## Grace-Mar And The Template

Grace-Mar is the first instance: a live private tool, a proving ground, and a working Record.

Companion Self is the upstream template and product frame: the reusable architecture that can be instantiated for other companions.

The intended workflow is:

1. develop and prove structural ideas in Grace-Mar
2. separate instance-specific material from reusable architecture
3. promote the reusable layer upstream into `companion-self`

This preserves the distinction between private instrument and public template while letting real usage sharpen the architecture before it is generalized.

---

## Strategic Position

Companion Self should be understood as a trust layer for personalized AI.

It does not need to replace existing tutors, copilots, or agents. It can sit beneath them as the governed identity substrate they read from and contribute to through staging. In that sense, it is complementary infrastructure: a continuity and sovereignty layer for systems that would otherwise personalize from scattered, inferred, or locked-up context.

Its defensibility does not come primarily from interface novelty. It comes from:

- governance discipline
- longitudinal evidence quality
- clear boundaries between identity, capability, and execution
- portability
- operator workflows that make sovereignty usable, not merely theoretical

---

## Canonical Short Form

Companion Self is a companion-owned, evidence-grounded, portable identity layer for the agentic era. It separates the **Record** from the **Voice**, and places a human **gate** between staged suggestions and durable truth. `SELF` captures who the companion is, `SKILLS` captures what the Record can evidence they can do, and `WORK` remains an adjacent execution layer rather than automatic identity. The system competes on governance before fluency: provenance, approval, auditability, and portability are treated as core product features. Grace-Mar is the first live instance; `companion-self` is the reusable template that turns this architecture into a general product surface.
