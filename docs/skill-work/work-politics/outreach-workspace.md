# WAP outreach workspace

Canonical operator entrypoint for outreach inside `work-politics`.

Use this file when you want to answer:

- what offer is being tested
- who it is for
- whether the path is direct or partner-led
- what proof supports it
- how outreach outcomes are being tracked
- what objections are teaching us

---

## What outreach is for

Outreach here is a market-learning surface, not a mass-email engine.

Use it to learn:

- which offer framing creates curiosity
- which buyer segment actually cares
- which partner types can introduce us into warmer client relationships
- what proof builds trust
- where friction appears in the funnel

---

## Operator path

1. Start in `offers.md` and choose one offer to test.
2. Confirm the matching segment in `target-registry.md`.
3. Pull one or two proof lines from `proof-ledger.md`.
4. Choose the lane: direct outreach or partner-led outreach.
5. Run a small outreach batch.
6. Log outcomes in `outreach-funnel.md`.
7. Log pushback and what it means in `objection-log.md`.
8. Adjust offer, proof, segment, or partner type before the next batch.

---

## Outreach lanes

| Lane | When to use | What it optimizes for |
|------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Direct** | Warm or semi-warm conversations with likely buyers | Fast learning about buyer pain, offer fit, and language |
| **Partner-led** | When a trusted intermediary already serves likely WAP buyers | Borrowed authority, warmer intros, and faster trust |

---

## Weekly rhythm

| Step | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Offer check** | Are we still leading with operator outcomes instead of tooling? |
| **Segment check** | Are we talking to the most plausible buyer for this offer? |
| **Lane check** | Is this better run as direct outreach or through a partner who already has trust? |
| **Proof check** | Do we have one credible line that reduces trust friction? |
| **Funnel check** | Where is the biggest drop-off this week? |
| **Objection check** | What did replies teach us about fit, trust, timing, or scope? |

## Partner pitch draft

Use this when talking to a consultant, agency, or adjacent operator who already has trusted client relationships:

> I have been building a small workflow around campaign briefing, message prep, and operator visibility.  
>  
> I am not trying to sell a giant AI system. What I can do is offer a bounded diagnostic for teams where briefing, drafts, and follow-ups are getting fragmented.  
>  
> If that is useful for any of your clients, I would be happy to do a small diagnostic or sample workflow surface for them. It gives them something concrete, and it gives you another way to bring value without adding extra lift on your side.

## Guardrails

- Keep outreach bounded, truthful, and companion-led.
- Do not overclaim technical maturity.
- Do not frame WAP primarily as "AI automation."
- In the partner lane, make the partner feel safer and more useful, not responsible for a risky handoff.
- Political trust and discretion matter more than clever outbound tricks.
