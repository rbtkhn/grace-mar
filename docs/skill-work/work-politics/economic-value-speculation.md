## Speculative economic value

**1. Share of a huge and growing spend.**  
Lobbying and political consulting together are **$8B+** (consulting ~$3.3B, lobbying $5.24B). Even a tiny share—e.g. 0.01% of addressable “campaign support / research / messaging” work—is **hundreds of thousands to low millions** in revenue. The application doesn’t need to replace firms; it needs to capture a slice of campaigns (and possibly causes or PACs) that want **focused, cheap, draft-only support** without a full retainer or a big firm.

**2. Congressional races are expensive; our ask is small.**  
Competitive House races burn **millions** in ads, polling, and consultants. A single offering at **0.1 BTC** (~$6k–10k) is a **rounding error** for a campaign that’s already spending six or seven figures. The value proposition isn’t “we’re the only option”—it’s “we’re a low-friction, fixed-price option for research and message support so you can reallocate staff or avoid a bigger retainer.”

**3. Replicability across principals and cycles.**  
Same product can be sold to **other candidates** (e.g. post–Massie bifurcation: other House races, aligned positions). One successful engagement de-risks the next. If the companion runs this as a repeatable service, **10 clients at 0.1 BTC per cycle** is 1 BTC per cycle (~$60k–100k at current prices)—meaningful for a one-person operation; trivial relative to industry spend.

**4. Lobbying adjacent.**  
Lobbying spend is **$5.24B** and growing; a lot of that is “research, messaging, and strategic support” for interests, not just direct lobbying. The same bounded product (briefs, research, message discipline, no public voice) could in theory serve **advocacy clients** who want cheap, clear deliverables. That expands addressable market without changing the product.

**5. Bitcoin as wedge.**  
Clients who prefer to pay in BTC (privacy, no chargebacks, no bank friction) are a **segment**. Offering 0.1 BTC + receipt makes the application the obvious choice for that segment. If that segment grows (e.g. more campaigns or causes willing to hold/spend crypto), the wedge gets larger.

---

## Caveats

- **No guarantee of demand.** The market is big; capturing any of it depends on distribution (X, email, referrals) and trust.
- **One engagement (e.g. Massie campaign) validates the product;** it doesn’t by itself “monetize the industry.”
- **Regulation and FEC:** Campaign payments in Bitcoin may attract scrutiny; receipts and clear scope protect both sides. Companion should confirm compliance for their jurisdiction.
- **BTC volatility:** 0.1 BTC fixed price means USD-equivalent moves with spot; companion may want to revisit pricing or hedge if scaling.

---

## Bottom line

The **potential economic value** of this AI application is: a small, fixed-price, Bitcoin-paid product serving a **multi-billion-dollar** political consulting and lobbying space. The application doesn’t need to “win” the market—it needs to **take a sliver** of campaigns (and possibly advocacy clients) who want research and message support without firm overhead or opaque retainers. At 0.1 BTC per engagement, even a handful of clients per cycle can be economically significant for a one-person operation; at scale (many principals, many cycles), the upside is bounded only by the companion’s capacity and distribution, not by the size of the industry.
