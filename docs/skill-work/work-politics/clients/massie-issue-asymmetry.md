# Massie KY-4 — Issue asymmetry scan

**Purpose:** Map where Thomas Massie’s record and voice are clearly different from (a) primary opponents, (b) general opponents, and (c) a “generic Republican,” on issues that matter in KY-4, then package those gaps into doctrine-safe wedges for both the primary and the general.

**Doctrine filter:** See `massie-ky4-operator-checklist.md` — every wedge should:

- Strengthen **engineer/farmer**, **anti-waste**, **anti-surveillance/war** framing.
- Avoid **forced culture-war cosplay** that isn’t Massie.
- Collapse to **one KY-4 sentence** explainable to a neighbor.

---

Working buckets tuned to current profile; add/merge as race develops.

4. **Congressional power & process** — separation of powers, emergency powers, rules, transparency.
5. **Agency & regulatory overreach** — unelected agencies, mandates, ESG-style nudges.
6. **Food, land & homestead** — small farms, property rights, food freedom, rural infrastructure.
7. **Energy & environment (KY-4 lens)** — power bills, grid, regulation that hits local industry.
8. **Guns & self-defense** — Second Amendment, red-flag frameworks, federal overreach.
9. **Health freedom & mandates** — COVID-era positions, medical autonomy, federal dictates.
10. **Tech, money & markets** — Bitcoin/crypto, financial surveillance, banking/AML creep.
11. **Education & local control** — federal vs local say, school culture fights as they land locally.

## 3. Flagship wedges (dual-salience candidates)

These are **hypothesis-level** flagship issues that appear strong for both the primary and the general, given current documentation.

1. **Anti-stupid war (war powers / Iran)**  
   - **Why primary:** Only R on a bi-partisan war powers push; took heat for restraining a Republican president; records courage vs crowd.  
   - **Why general:** Voters of all stripes are tired of endless wars that don’t fix anything and cost lives and money; “don’t send our kids to a war Congress never voted on” is crossover-safe.  
   - **Receipts:** Iran war powers co-sponsorship; floor remarks about radicalizing new terrorists; documented criticism of bombing as distraction from Epstein transparency.

2. **Transparent, accountable government (Epstein files + process)**  
   - **Why primary:** Shows independence from leaders and donors; “I pushed to open the files when both parties were quiet.”  
   - **Why general:** Cuts across partisan lines — people who think powerful people get away with everything.  
   - **Receipts:** Epstein files push; quotes tying transparency to trust in institutions.

3. **Liberty at home (surveillance, mandates, overreach)**  
   - **Why primary:** Distinguishes Massie from tough-on-crime rhetoric that quietly expands the surveillance state.  
   - **Why general:** Pairs privacy and health freedom with a “government out of your home, your phone, and your doctor’s office” line; accessible beyond base.  
   - **Receipts:** Anti-surveillance votes; COVID-era skepticism of federal dictates.

These can be re-scored or swapped as more race-specific data comes in (ads, debate lines, local stories).

## 4. Wedge cards (draft shells)

### 4.1 Primary wedge — “The Republican who voted against dumb wars”

- **Claim (primary-friendly):** When both parties sleepwalk toward another Middle East war, Massie is the Republican who forces Congress to vote before your son or daughter is sent.  
- **Receipts:**  
  - Only Republican co-sponsor of the Iran war powers resolution with Ro Khanna.  
  - Floor remarks warning an Iran conflict would radicalize new generations of terrorists.  
- **Contrast line:** Ed Gallrein is running with people who cheer any strike Trump orders; Massie is the one who reads the fine print before we bleed and borrow.  
- **Neighbor sentence (KY-4):** “I want the guy who makes them vote before they send our kids and grandkids.”  
- **Avoid:** Anything that sounds soft on defense; always anchor in “strong, careful, constitutional” instead of “anti-military.”

### 4.2 Primary wedge — “Engineer who reads the bill, not the room”

- **Claim:** Massie is the engineer-farmer who votes the math and the Constitution, even when leadership is yelling.  
- **Receipts:**  
  - Pattern of lone or small-cluster votes against omnibus spending and emergency packages that balloon debt.  
  - Public explanations that connect line items to KY-4 waste.  
- **Contrast line:** While others wait to see how Trump or leadership lean, Massie tells you how he voted and why in one KY-4 sentence.  
- **Neighbor sentence:** “He votes the numbers, not the noise.”  
- **Avoid:** Direct attacks on base-friendly figures; keep focus on **process and outcomes**, not intra-party drama.

### 4.3 General wedge — “Keep government out of your home and phone”

- **Claim:** Massie is the one in the race who has actually voted to stop the government from reading your messages and dictating your family’s health choices.  
- **Receipts:**  
  - Anti-surveillance votes; opposition to warrantless spying.  
  - COVID-era resistance to federal mandates and one-size-fits-all rules.  
- **Contrast line:** Others talk about freedom; Massie has a voting trail where he told both parties “no” when they wanted more power over your life.  
- **Neighbor sentence:** “He’s the one who kept D.C. out of our phones and out of our doctor’s office.”  
- **Avoid:** Technical surveillance jargon; keep it at “they shouldn’t be watching you or bossing your family around.”  

### 4.4 General wedge — “Farmer who fights bureaucrats, not neighbors”

- **Claim:** Massie is the local farmer who wants fewer distant bureaucrats telling KY-4 how to work their land, run their small business, or school their kids.  
- **Receipts:**  
  - Cattle farm and rural life as lived experience.  
  - Record against federal overreach that hurts small farms and small business.  
- **Contrast line:** Opponents offer more programs from far away; Massie wants decisions closer to the people who actually live with them.  
- **Neighbor sentence:** “He’s on our side when the folks with clipboards come telling us how to live.”  
- **Avoid:** Blanket anti-government language that sounds like abandoning needed local services; focus on **who decides**.

## 5. Weekly brief & monitoring hooks

- **Weekly brief hook:** Under “Votes / record” or “Opposition,” add one bullet: “Asymmetry this week — [issue]: [one-sentence wedge + receipt].”  
- **Monitoring:** When the monitoring list flags upcoming votes on spending, war, surveillance, or mandates, pre-draft how a YES/NO would alter the asymmetry matrix before the vote.  
- **Refresh cadence:** Re-score matrix and flagship list monthly or after any major vote, Trump statement, or Gallrein move that touches a flagship bucket.
