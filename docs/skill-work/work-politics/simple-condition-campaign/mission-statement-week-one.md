# Mission Statement — Week One

**Goal:** By the end of the first week, the foundation for the Simple Condition public awareness campaign is in place. No public content is posted and no money is spent without principal approval. The week is about **readying** the assets and the list so that Week Two can move to design delivery, approval, and distribution.

**We will:**

- **Lock the message.** One-pager copy and letter-summary copy are written, reviewed, and approved by the principal. The awareness one-line library (no Iran CTA) is set and approved. These are the canonical texts for the designer and for all later use.

- **Lock the brief and the designer.** The designer brief is approved and a freelancer is hired (or the job is posted and hire is pending principal sign-off). The designer has the brief and the approved copy and a clear delivery date. No design work starts until copy and brief are approved.

- **Lock the list.** A mail list of 50–75 religious leaders or institutions is built, checked, and approved by the principal. The decision on channel is made: physical mail, email, or both. No envelopes or emails go out until the list and the channel are approved.

- **Stay within governance.** Every deliverable that touches copy, spend, or distribution is sent to the principal for approval before the next step. We do not merge, print, mail, or post until the principal says so.

**We will not:**

- Send the letter or one-pager to anyone until assets and list are approved.
- Hire or pay the designer until the principal approves the brief and the freelancer.
- Post on social media or run paid boost in Week One.

**Success looks like:** Approved copy, approved one-line library, approved designer brief, hired designer (or approved job post and hire in progress), approved mail list and channel, and a clear handoff into Week Two (design delivery → asset approval → print/mail or email → organic social and clergy outreach).
