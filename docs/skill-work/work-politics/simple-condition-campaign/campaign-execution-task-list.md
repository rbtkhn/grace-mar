# Campaign Execution: Sequential Task List

**Purpose:** Single ordered checklist to execute the Simple Condition public awareness campaign (culminating at Easter). Complete in order; stop at each **[Approval]** step and wait for principal sign-off before continuing. **Human approval required** for all public content, spend, and outreach.

**Reference:** [hire-campaign-executor](hire-campaign-executor.md), [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md), [public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy](public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy.md), [employee-instructions-1000-budget](employee-instructions-1000-budget.md).

---

## Foundation (E − 10 to E − 8 weeks)

1. Read campaign plan: public-awareness-campaign-design, campaign-culmination-easter, public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy. Confirm Easter date (E) and backward calendar with principal.
2. Confirm with principal: approver name/role, how to submit for approval (email/Slack/doc), where to store deliverables, total budget and allocations.
3. Draft one-pager copy (public) — ~300–400 words: what the Condition is, door is open, beauty and blade/seam, one subject many tongues. Source: THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION, campaign design §3.
4. Draft letter summary (2–3 pages) for religious leaders — opening, condition, seam, two propositions, children's question, ask, closing. Source: THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.
5. **[Approval]** Send one-pager and letter-summary copy to principal. Do not proceed to design until approved.
6. Finalise one-line library (awareness) — 10–20 shareable lines: door is open, peace as condition, seam, children's question, one subject many tongues. No Iran / "demand the vote." Use one-line-library-awareness.md if it exists; else create from one-line-library.
7. **[Approval]** Send one-line library to principal.
8. Write designer brief: deliverables (one-pager PDF, letter-summary PDF, 4–6 quote graphics, optionally 2–3 short videos 10–30 sec); attach approved copy and quote lines; style (clean, readable, invitational); file names.
9. **[Approval]** Send designer brief and chosen freelancer (or job post) to principal. Confirm asset budget (e.g. $250–400).
10. Hire designer; send approved brief and copy. Set deadline (e.g. 7–10 days).
11. Optional: draft Easter pulpit cut (60 sec) — one-line Easter lead into universal reading. **[Approval]** if creating.
12. Build clergy list — 50–100+ Christian clergy (Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox): names/titles, institutions, email (and/or mailing address). Spreadsheet: Name, Title, Institution, Email, Tradition, Notes.
13. Build educator list — 20–50 educators (religious ed, campus ministry, teachers who touch sacred texts or ethics). Same columns as clergy.
14. Build micro-influencer list — 20–30 (faith, peace, coordination): handles, platform, email/DM. **[Approval]** Principal approves clergy, educator, and influencer list scope and use.
15. Receive designer deliverables: one-pager PDF, letter-summary PDF, 4–6 quote graphics. Check for typos and readability.
16. **[Approval]** Send all design deliverables to principal. No public use until approved.
17. Optional: produce 2–3 short videos (10–30 sec) — quote + text or simple edit. Or commission per designer brief. **[Approval]** before public use.
18. Optional: produce 90-sec video (one idea: door + seam + children's question). **[Approval]** before public use.
19. Host one-pager and letter summary at a stable URL (e.g. GitHub Pages, website, or PDF link) for all CTAs.
20. Set up spend log and results log (spreadsheet or doc). Log any design/spend to date.

---

## Launch (E − 7 to E − 6 weeks)

21. Draft first clergy email/DM — Easter ask: "Will you read this 90-second pulpit reading to your congregation on Easter Sunday (or Holy Saturday)?" Attach universal reading and link to tradition-specific readings. **[Approval]** Principal approves clergy outreach message and list.
22. Send clergy outreach (batch 1) — to first 30–50 on list. Record: sent date, any reply ("I'll read it," decline, no response).
23. Draft first educator outreach — share one-pager and children's question; "Use this question once when you teach." **[Approval]** Principal approves educator message.
24. Send educator outreach to list. Record replies.
25. Draft first social posts (3–5) — different quote graphics; captions from one-line library; CTA "Read the letter" + link. Include "Tag someone who needs to hear this" / "Send to your pastor." **[Approval]** Principal approves posts and channels.
26. Publish first social wave — schedule or post over 3–5 days. Note which post performs best.
27. DM micro-influencers (10–20) — send 2–3 graphics + short video; "Post one if it resonates. No commitment." **[Approval]** Principal approves influencer list and message if not already done.
28. Weekly report (optional): reach, clergy replies, best-performing post. Send to principal.

---

## Spread (E − 5 to E − 4 weeks)

29. Send clergy batch 2 — same Easter ask to next 30–50 (or re-contact non-responders with one follow-up). Record confirmations ("I'll read on Easter").
30. Collate clergy confirmations — count and list (congregation name, tradition, contact) for Easter weekend. **[Approval]** Principal may request recap or messaging change.
31. Publish 90-sec video and letter-summary link (if not already live). Add to social and outreach.
32. Second wave of social — rotate hook: "Does anyone have to die for this to be true?" / "Every tradition carries beauty and a blade." New or alternate graphics. **[Approval]** Principal approves.
33. Post second wave. Include UGC prompt: "What's the seam in your tradition? Reply with one example of beauty and one of blade."
34. Optional: pitch one op-ed or podcast — "A letter for Easter: peace as the condition." **[Approval]** Principal approves pitch and outlet.
35. Optional: paid boost — use best-performing post; Meta and/or X; target interests (religion, peace, interfaith). Budget per plan (e.g. $75–150). **[Approval]** Principal approves platform, post, targeting, spend.
36. Run paid boost if approved. Log spend and results.
37. Weekly report: confirmations, engagement, spend to date. Send to principal.

---

## Holy Week (E − 1 week)

38. Draft Holy Week reminder to clergy — "Easter is this Sunday. If you're reading the Condition reading, thank you. Link again: [reading]. We'll send a short recap after." **[Approval]** Principal approves.
39. Send Holy Week reminder to all clergy who confirmed (and optionally to full list).
40. Draft Holy Week social — "This Easter, [N] congregations will hear the same message: the door is open. Peace is the condition." + Easter graphic or line. **[Approval]** Principal approves.
41. Post Holy Week social (2–3 posts across the week). Peak visibility.
42. Optional: pitch media — "Easter weekend: churches read the Condition." **[Approval]** Principal approves pitch.

---

## Easter weekend (Culmination)

43. Easter Saturday / Sunday — clergy who committed read pulpit reading (or Easter cut) at services. Executor does not run services; only coordinate and thank.
44. Easter Sunday (or Monday): post culmination asset — "Today [N] congregations heard: the door is open. One subject, many tongues. Thank you." **[Approval]** Principal approves final count and wording.
45. Send thank-you to clergy who read (short email or DM).
46. Log: number of congregations, traditions, any immediate feedback. Add to results log.

---

## Sustain (E + 1 to E + 2 weeks)

47. Collate post-Easter recap — how many read, where (region/tradition), one or two testimonies (with permission). **[Approval]** Principal approves use of testimonies and recap.
48. Publish recap (blog post, social post, or email to list): "What you did this Easter." Link to children's question and one-pager for next steps.
49. Send recap to clergy and educator list: "The Condition doesn't end at Easter. Use the children's question. Share the one-pager."
50. Final report to principal: total reach, clergy count, engagement metrics, spend vs budget, lessons learned, handoff or extend recommendations.
51. Archive deliverables and logs in agreed location. Close or extend campaign per principal.

---

## Approval quick reference

- **Copy:** Tasks 5, 7 — one-pager, letter summary, one-line library.
- **Design:** Tasks 9, 16, 17, 18 — brief, freelancer, final assets, video.
- **Lists:** Task 14 — clergy, educator, influencer lists.
- **Outreach:** Tasks 21, 23, 27 — clergy, educator, influencer messages.
- **Social:** Tasks 25, 32, 40, 44 — all posts and culmination asset.
- **Paid:** Task 35 — boost platform, post, targeting, spend.
- **Media:** Tasks 34, 42 — op-ed/podcast pitch, Easter media pitch.
- **Recap:** Task 47 — testimonies and recap content.

*If anything is unclear or a decision is needed that isn't covered, pause and ask the principal. Do not assume.*
