# Hire: Campaign Executor — Simple Condition Public Awareness (Culminating at Easter)

**Purpose:** Define the role and process for hiring someone to **execute the full public awareness campaign** — viral + deep penetration, Easter culmination — with approval gates. Use this doc to brief candidates and to draft job postings.

**Governance:** All public-facing content, spend, and outreach commitments require **principal approval** before release. The executor implements; the principal approves.

---

## 1. Role in one sentence

**Campaign Executor:** Own execution of the Simple Condition public awareness campaign from foundation through Easter weekend and sustain: assets, clergy/educator outreach, social, metrics, and coordination — all under principal approval.

---

## 2. Scope of work (what they'll execute)

| Phase | What the executor does | Key docs |
|-------|------------------------|----------|
| **Foundation** | Lock one-pager and letter-summary copy; finalise one-line library (awareness); brief and hire designer for graphics/video; build initial clergy and educator lists; optional Easter pulpit cut. | [public-awareness-campaign-design](public-awareness-campaign-design.md), [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md), [employee-instructions-1000-budget](employee-instructions-1000-budget.md) Phase 0 |
| **Launch** | Ship 4–6 quote graphics and 2–3 short videos; seed to clergy ("Will you read this on Easter?") and educators; first social wave; personal DMs to micro-influencers. | [public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy](public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy.md) §4, [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md) |
| **Spread** | 90-sec video and letter summary live; second wave of content; clergy confirmations; UGC prompts; op-ed or podcast outreach; rotate hooks weekly. | Viral-penetration §6, [social-media-playbook](social-media-playbook.md) |
| **Holy Week** | Reminder to clergy; social push ("This Easter, hundreds of congregations will hear…"); media pitch. | [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md) §5 |
| **Easter weekend (culmination)** | Coordinate: clergy read; aggregate and thank; "Easter 20XX: one message, many tongues" post or short video. | [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md) §2, §4 |
| **Sustain** | Recap (how many read, where); testimonies (with permission); tie to children's question and one-pager; handoff or extend. | [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md) §2 |

**Budget context:** A $1K budget exists for assets and distribution ([budget-plan-1000](budget-plan-1000.md)); the executor may also coordinate additional freelancers or spend if approved. All spend requires approval.

---

## 3. Deliverables (executor produces or coordinates)

- **Copy:** One-pager (public), letter summary (2–3 pages), one-line library (awareness), designer brief. *(Some may already exist in repo.)*
- **Assets:** Quote graphics (4–6), short videos (2–3, 10–30 sec), optional 90-sec video; one-pager and letter summary as PDFs. *(Designer may execute; executor briefs and approves with principal.)*
- **Lists and outreach:** Clergy list (Christian, multifaith optional); educator list; micro-influencer list. DMs and emails per [campaign-culmination-easter](campaign-culmination-easter.md) §5.
- **Social:** Posts and scheduling per [social-media-playbook](social-media-playbook.md) and viral strategy; all copy approved before post.
- **Reporting:** Weekly or biweekly: reach, engagement, clergy confirmations, spend vs budget. Final recap post-Easter: congregations read, testimonies, lessons learned.

## 4. Skills and experience

| Need | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Campaign or project execution** | Can own a multi-phase plan with deadlines and approval gates; doesn't need to be told every step. |
| **Outreach and relationship** | Comfortable reaching out to clergy, educators, influencers by email/DM; professional, concise, invitational tone. |
| **Social and content** | Can draft posts, use scheduling tools, and work with a designer brief; understands shareability and CTAs. |
| **Copy and messaging** | Can adapt existing copy (one-pager, letter summary) from source docs or edit drafts; attention to tone (invitational, not accusatory). |
| **Organization and reporting** | Tracks lists, confirmations, spend; reports clearly to principal. |
| **Comfort with approval gates** | No public post or spend without sign-off; can work asynchronously and reschedule when approval is delayed. |

**Nice to have:** Experience in faith-based or nonprofit campaigns; familiarity with religious audiences; basic design sense (to brief designers); experience with Easter or seasonal campaigns.

**Not required:** Theology degree; large budget experience; in-house team (executor can be solo with freelancers).

---

## 5. Time commitment and duration

- **Duration:** From hire through Easter weekend plus 1–2 weeks sustain (recap, handoff). Example: hire by mid-Feb 2026 → Easter Apr 5, 2026 → sustain to mid-Apr 2026.
- **Hours:** Part-time contract (e.g. 15–25 hrs/week) is typical; scale up in Holy Week and Easter weekend. Or full-time short-term (e.g. 8–10 weeks) if scope includes more outreach and content.
- **Flexibility:** Principal and executor agree on weekly cadence, approval turnaround, and communication channel (email, Slack, etc.).

---

## 6. Compensation (placeholder)

- **Option A — Fixed project fee:** One lump sum for full execution (foundation → Easter → sustain). Define scope clearly; add change-order process if scope grows.
- **Option B — Monthly or weekly rate:** Pay per week or month for the campaign period; executor tracks hours or deliverables per agreement.
- **Option C — Staged:** Fee at Foundation complete, at Launch complete, at Easter culmination, at Sustain complete.

*Principal to set actual numbers; consider local rates for campaign/communications contractors and budget (e.g. $1K is for assets/distribution, not labor).*

*Below is a condensed version you can paste into LinkedIn, Indeed, or an email. Adjust title, compensation, and "How to apply" as needed.*

**Title:** Campaign Executor — Public Awareness (Contract, Through Easter)

**Summary:** We're hiring a contractor to execute a public awareness campaign for a global message about peace, coordination, and the role of faith traditions. The campaign runs from foundation (assets, lists, copy) through a coordinated **Easter weekend culmination** (clergy read a short message to congregations) and a short sustain phase. You'll own execution: coordinating design, outreach to clergy and educators, social content, and reporting — all under principal approval. No public post or spend without sign-off. Remote; part-time (e.g. 15–25 hrs/week) for approximately 8–10 weeks.

**Responsibilities:**
- Produce or finalise copy (one-pager, 2–3 page letter summary, one-line library) and designer brief; coordinate designer for quote graphics and short videos.
- Build and maintain clergy and educator lists; send outreach emails/DMs (invitation to read a 90-second pulpit reading on Easter weekend).
- Draft and schedule social content (posts, graphics) per campaign playbook; all content approved before posting.
- Track confirmations, engagement, and spend; report weekly or biweekly to principal.
- Execute Holy Week reminder and Easter weekend coordination; produce post-Easter recap (how many congregations, testimonies if any).

**Qualifications:**
- Experience executing a time-bound campaign or project with clear phases and deliverables.
- Comfort with outreach (email/DM to clergy, educators, or similar) and with drafting social/content; professional, invitational tone.
- Organized; can track lists, deadlines, and approvals; clear reporting.
- Comfortable working with approval gates (no public release without principal sign-off).

**Preferred:** Experience in faith-based, nonprofit, or awareness campaigns; familiarity with religious audiences; basic design briefing.
