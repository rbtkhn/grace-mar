# Public Awareness: Viral + Deep Penetration Strategy

**Purpose:** Make the Simple Condition concept **go viral** (maximum spread, shareability, network activation) and **penetrate deep** (repeat exposure, layered understanding, community ownership, lasting recall). Complements [public-awareness-campaign-design.md](public-awareness-campaign-design.md); this doc is the virality and depth playbook. **Human approval required** before any public ship.

---

## 1. Why this concept can go viral

| Driver | How the concept fits |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Hope + agency** | "The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut." — One line that reframes despair into agency. Shareable because it feels true and empowering. |
| **Clarity** | "Every tradition carries beauty and a blade. Make the seam visible." — Names something people recognize but hadn't named. "I never thought of it that way" = share. |
| **One question** | "Does anyone have to die for this to be true?" — Memorable, portable, applicable to any tradition or text. People ask it, quote it, tag others. |
| **Identity** | "I'm the kind of person who sees the seam." / "My faith teaches this." — Shareable as identity signal without requiring doctrine. |
| **Low barrier** | No sign-up, no donation, no policy expertise. Read one line. Share one line. Ask one question. Depth is optional. |
| **One subject, many tongues** | The campaign itself can mirror the message: same idea in Catholic, Baptist, Jewish, secular, educator, AI-ethics language. "Say it in your own language" becomes a participation prompt. |

**Viral risk to manage:** The concept is invitational, not tribal. Avoid framing that triggers "us vs them" or "your tradition is the problem." Virality should feel like invitation and clarity, not attack.

---

## 2. Depth: what "penetrate deep" means

- **Repeat exposure** — The same person encounters the idea in multiple forms (one-line → graphic → 30-sec video → one-pager → pulpit → essay). Each layer deepens without repeating the same text.
- **Layered understanding** — Surface: "The door is open." Middle: "Peace is the condition, not the reward." Deep: The full letter, the seam, the two propositions, the children's question, one subject many tongues.
- **Community ownership** — Clergy read it from the pulpit. Educators use the children's question in class. Influencers and ordinary users say "my tradition teaches this" or "I'm asking my pastor to read this." The idea is not only consumed but **used** and **passed on** in their own voice.
- **Ritual / habit** — The children's question becomes a repeatable practice: "When you read a sacred text or hear a justification, ask: does anyone have to die for this to be true?" Habit = depth.
- **Multi-channel embedding** — Theology (pulpit, religious press), secular (coordination, op-ed), education (classroom), social (memes, video), art (quote graphics, short film). Same concept, many tongues — penetration across worlds.

---

## 3. Content ladder (surface → deep)

Design content so that **every layer is shareable** and **each layer pulls to the next**. No dead ends.

| Layer | Format | Example | CTA to next layer |
|-------|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **L1: One line** | Text, quote graphic, 10-sec video | "The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut." | "Learn more" → L2 or L3 |
| **L2: One idea** | 30-sec video, carousel (3–5 slides), short post | "Peace isn't the reward. Peace is the condition. Here's what that means in one sentence." | "Read the one-pager" / "Ask your clergy to read this" → L4 |
| **L3: Three ideas** | 90-sec video, one-pager (single page) | Door + seam + children's question. | "Read the letter summary" / "Share with your tradition" → L5 |
| **L4: Full summary** | 2–3 page letter summary, 3–5 min read | Executive summary of the Condition: two propositions, seam, ask, children's question. | "Read the full letter" / "Send to your religious leader" → L5 |
| **L5: Full letter** | Full [THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION](../../../civilization-memory/essays/THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.md) or pamphlet | For clergy, scholars, committed readers. | "Use the pulpit reading" / "Teach the children's question" |

**Rule:** Every asset at L1–L4 ends with a single, clear next step. No "follow us" without "and do this."

## 4. Viral mechanics: what to build and how to seed

### 4.1 Maximum-shareability assets (ship first)

| Asset | Why it spreads | Format |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| **"The door is open"** | One line, hope + agency, no theology required | Quote graphic (multiple designs: minimal, faith, secular). 10-sec video (text on screen or spoken). |
| **"Does anyone have to die for this to be true?"** | One question, applicable everywhere, challenge/prompt | Quote graphic. Short video: "Ask it. When the answer is no, that's the beauty. When it's yes, that's the blade." |
| **"Every tradition carries beauty and a blade"** | Names the seam; identity-friendly ("my tradition has both") | Quote graphic. Carousel: "Beauty = X. Blade = Y. The seam is the line. Make it visible." |
| **"One subject, many tongues"** | Coordination without creed; shareable across traditions | Quote graphic. 30-sec: "We don't need one creed. We need one thing we all point to." |

**Volume:** 4–6 quote graphics (same lines, 2–3 visual styles for A/B). 2–3 short videos (10–30 sec). All with consistent watermark or handle and one link (e.g. simplecondition.org or link-in-bio to one-pager).

### 4.2 Share prompts (baked into copy or post)

- **"Tag someone who needs to hear this."**
- **"Send this to your pastor / rabbi / imam / priest."**
- **"What's the seam in your tradition? Reply with one example of beauty and one of blade."**
- **"Ask in your congregation: Does anyone have to die for this to be true? When the answer is no, that's the beauty. Keep it."**
- **"Say it in your own language. The subject is the same."** — Invites UGC: people post their tradition's version.

### 4.3 Seeding strategy (who gets it first)

| Node | What they get | What we ask |
|------|----------------|-------------|
| **Clergy (multifaith)** | Pulpit reading (universal or tradition-specific); 2–3 page letter summary; one graphic | "Read this. If it resonates, read it to your congregation once. No other commitment." |
| **Educators** | One-pager with the children's question; how to use it in class | "Use this question once when you teach sacred text or conflict. Report back if you're willing." |
| **Micro-influencers (faith, peace, coordination)** | 3–5 graphics + 30-sec video; one-line library | "Post one. Tag us. No long commitment." |
| **Journalists / op-ed** | Full letter or summary; pitch: "A letter the world's religious leaders need to read" | "Excerpt it, review it, or run an op-ed. We're not selling anything." |
| **Communities (parish, synagogue, mosque, campus)** | One-pager + one graphic; "Discuss in your group" prompt | "Print this. Discuss once. Share what you did (optional)." |

**Principle:** Give each node **one thing to do** and **one thing to share**. Personal ask (DM, email) beats broadcast.

## 5. Depth tactics: repetition and ownership

### 5.1 Same message, many tongues (campaign as proof of concept)

- Publish **tradition-specific** versions: Catholic, Baptist, Jewish, secular, educator, AI-ethics. Same structure, different vocabulary. "One subject, many tongues" is demonstrated by the campaign itself.
- **Translations:** Arabic, Spanish, Hebrew, etc. Same one-liners and one-pager. Deepens penetration in multilingual communities and signals seriousness.

### 5.2 Repetition without fatigue

- **Rotate the hook:** Week 1: "The door is open." Week 2: "Does anyone have to die for this to be true?" Week 3: "Every tradition carries beauty and a blade." Week 4: "One subject, many tongues." Same underlying idea, fresh entry each time.
- **New formats, same message:** Podcast clip, op-ed, pulpit, classroom, graphic, tweet. One user might see the door line in a graphic, later in a video, later from their pastor. Depth through repetition across format, not repetition of the same post.

### 5.3 Ownership and UGC

- **"My tradition teaches this."** — Invite people to post one sentence from their tradition that aligns (e.g. "Love your enemy," "Beat swords into plowshares," "Pikuach nefesh"). Aggregate into a "many tongues" thread or reel.
- **"I asked my clergy to read this."** — Simple testimonial: "I sent the pulpit reading to my pastor. She read it. Here's what happened." (With permission.)
- **"I used the children's question in class."** — Educator short-form: "I used 'Does anyone have to die for this to be true?' when we studied X. One student said Y." Collect and reshare (with permission).

### 5.4 Ritual: the children's question as habit

- **Prompt for formation:** "Every time you hear a sacred text or a justification for force, ask: does anyone have to die for this to be true? When the answer is no, that's the beauty. When it's yes, that's the blade."
- **Curriculum tie-in:** One-pager for teachers: use the question when teaching sacred texts, history of conflict, or ethics. Repeat use = depth in the next generation.

## 6. Phasing: viral spike + sustained penetration

*For a campaign that **culminates at Easter**, see [campaign-culmination-easter.md](campaign-culmination-easter.md): backward calendar from Easter Sunday, clergy ask, Easter-specific assets.*

| Phase | Goal | Tactics |
|-------|------|---------|
| **Launch (week 1–2)** | Viral spike: one line and one question everywhere | Ship 4–6 graphics + 2–3 short videos. Seed to clergy, educators, 20–30 influencers. Personal DMs. "Tag someone" / "Send to your pastor" in every post. One hashtag: #TheSimpleCondition or #OneSubjectManyTongues. |
| **Spread (week 3–6)** | Depth: layers and repetition | 90-sec video. One-pager live. Letter summary to clergy and journalists. Rotate hooks weekly. First UGC round: "My tradition teaches this" / "I sent this to my pastor." First educator reports. |
| **Embed (week 7–12)** | Ownership and habit | Tradition-specific pulpit readings and one-pagers. Translations if ready. Op-eds and podcast pickups. "I used the children's question" educator stories. Second wave of graphics (new designs, same lines). |
| **Sustain (ongoing)** | Long-tail and application pull | Tie to current events: "The Condition speaks to this." When an application (e.g. Iran, another conflict, AI ethics) launches, audiences already know the frame. New content: new quotes, new voices, new formats. |

## 7. Metrics: viral + deep

| Metric | Viral (spread) | Deep (penetration) |
|--------|----------------|--------------------|
| **Reach** | Impressions, shares, reposts, quote-tweets | Click-through to one-pager, letter summary, full letter |
| **Engagement** | Likes, comments, "tag someone" replies | Clergy sign-on ("I read this"); educator sign-on ("I used the question"); UGC posts |
| **Repetition** | — | Same user sees message in 2+ formats (track via link clicks, email opens, or survey) |
| **Ownership** | — | Testimonials, "I sent to my pastor," "my tradition teaches this," classroom use reports |
| **Recognition** | — | "The Simple Condition" / "the seam" / "the children's question" named in third-party content (op-eds, sermons, social) |

**North star:** Recognition + ownership. The concept is viral when it's shared; it penetrates deep when it's **used** and **spoken in the user's own voice**.

---

## 8. Summary

To make the concept go **viral**: lead with one line and one question; create 4–6 shareable assets (graphics, 10–30 sec video); seed to clergy, educators, and micro-influencers with one clear ask each; bake in share prompts ("tag someone," "send to your pastor," "what's the seam in your tradition?"). To **penetrate deep**: build a content ladder (one line → one idea → one-pager → letter); repeat the message across formats and weeks without repeating the same post; invite ownership (UGC, "my tradition," "I asked my clergy," "I used the question in class"); turn the children's question into a repeatable habit; mirror "one subject, many tongues" with tradition-specific and translated content. Phasing: launch (viral spike) → spread (depth, layers) → embed (ownership, habit) → sustain (tie to events, application pull). Success = recognition of the idea and people using it in their own voice. All public ship requires human approval.
