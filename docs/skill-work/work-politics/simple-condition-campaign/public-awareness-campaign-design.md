# Public Awareness Campaign: The Simple Condition

**Purpose:** Design for a public awareness campaign that makes the Simple Condition (the letter, the seam, peace as condition, one subject many tongues) known to broad audiences — religious and secular — without tying it to a single policy or conflict. **Human approval required** before any public ship. Per [civ-mem-draft-protocol.md](../civ-mem-draft-protocol.md) and [consulting-charter.md](../consulting-charter.md).

**Relationship to Iran campaign:** The Iran campaign is one *application* of the Condition (demand the vote, see the face, end the war). This campaign is about the *idea itself* — so that when specific applications (Iran, other conflicts, AI ethics, interfaith coordination) are launched, audiences have already encountered the framework.

---

## 1. Campaign goal

- **Primary:** Increase awareness of the Simple Condition so that (a) religious leaders and communities encounter the letter and the seam; (b) secular audiences encounter the coordination/seam framing; (c) "one subject, many tongues" and the children's question become recognizable.
- **Secondary:** Create a shared reference — the Condition — that diverse traditions and institutions can point to when they want to coordinate without doctrinal agreement. Build demand for the letter to be read, taught, and discussed.
- **Not:** Converting anyone to a creed; advocating one tradition over another; or substituting for direct action on specific issues (e.g. Iran). Awareness of the idea supports those applications; it does not replace them.

| Audience | What they need | Preferred channels |

| **General public (curious)** | One-line hooks; "the door is open"; "every tradition carries beauty and a blade." Meme-ready, low barrier. | Social, short video, graphics, quotes in mainstream press |
| **AI / tech / policy** | AI-ethics derivation (condition-first, seam visibility); "one project, shared subject." | [AI-ETHICS-FROM-THE-CONDITION](../../../civilization-memory/essays/AI-ETHICS-FROM-THE-CONDITION.md), talks, policy-adjacent forums |

## 3. Core messages (by audience)

**Universal (all audiences):**
- The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut.
- Every tradition carries beauty and a blade. Making the seam visible is the solution.
- One subject, many tongues — we don't need one creed to coordinate; we need one thing we all point to.

**Religious:**
- Peace may be the condition for what your tradition says you're waiting for — not the consequence.
- Say it plainly: killing delays what all of you say you await. Say it in your own language.
- The children's question: "Does anyone have to die for this to be true?" When the answer is no, that's the beauty. When it's yes, that's the blade.

**Secular:**
- Coordination doesn't require agreement on God. It requires one shared reference — the condition, the seam — that many value systems can name in their own terms.
- Identification of the seam is the solution: not imposition, but making the line between benefit and harm visible so people can choose.

**AI / tech:**
- The same framework that applies to religious traditions applies to AI: condition-first deployment, seam visibility, no sacred footnote. See [AI Ethics from the Condition](../../../civilization-memory/essays/AI-ETHICS-FROM-THE-CONDITION.md).

## 4. Channels and tactics

| Channel | Tactic | Owner / note |
|---------|--------|--------------|
| **Letter distribution** | Send the full letter (or a 2–3 page executive summary) to a curated list of religious leaders and institutions (multifaith). No ask beyond "read and consider." | Operator / trusted partners; respect institutional protocols |
| **Pulpit / congregation** | [Pulpit reading (universal)](pulpit-reading-universal.md); [Viral faith-communities kit](viral-faith-communities-kit.md). "My faith teaches X; we want peace." | Clergy; provide ready-to-use scripts and graphics |

| **Op-eds / essays** | Place excerpts or op-eds in religious press (e.g. Sojourners, Commonweal, Islamic Monthly, Jewish outlets) and secular (e.g. coordination, ethics, peace). One subject many tongues; AI-ethics piece for tech outlets. | Writer / operator; pitch as "a letter the world's religious leaders need to read" or "one subject, many tongues" |
| **Academic / interfaith** | Propose panels or talks at conferences (religious studies, peace and conflict, coordination). Frame: the seam, the condition, coordination without creed. | Operator / scholars |
| **Education** | One-pager for teachers: the children's question; "does anyone have to die for this to be true?" as a prompt for reading sacred texts or discussing conflict. No theology required. | Curriculum partner or operator |
| **Book / long-form** | If the letter is published as a short book or pamphlet, campaign drives "read the letter" and "share with your clergy." | Publisher / operator |
| **Translations** | Letter (or key sections) in Arabic, Hebrew, Spanish, Mandarin, etc. Same message, many tongues — embodies the thesis. | Translator network; quality control |

## 5. Phased rollout

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **0. Foundation** | 2–4 weeks | Finalize one-pager (letter summary); 5–10 one-liners for social; 1–2 quote graphics. Identify 20–50 initial religious-leader or institution contacts; 5–10 secular outlets or influencers. |
| **1. Seed** | 4–6 weeks | Send letter (or summary) to initial list; publish 1–2 op-eds or essays; post 3–5 social assets; share pulpit reading and faith-communities kit with 10–20 clergy. Measure: opens, shares, replies, pickups. |
| **2. Amplify** | 6–12 weeks | Expand distribution; second wave of op-eds; teacher one-pager if partner ready; short video; coordinate with any application campaign (e.g. Iran) so "the Condition" is named when the application is. |
| **3. Sustain** | Ongoing | New translations; new excerpts; tie to current events (when a conflict or coordination moment arises, "the Condition speaks to this"). Keep the letter and the seam in circulation. |

## 7. Success metrics

- **Reach:** Letter or summary sent to N religious leaders/institutions; M op-eds or essays placed; social impressions and shares.
- **Engagement:** Clergy using pulpit reading; faith communities posting "my faith teaches X"; educators using the children's question.
- **Recognition:** "The Simple Condition" or "one subject, many tongues" or "the seam" becomes a recognizable reference in target circles (religious, coordination, education).
- **Application pull:** When an application campaign (e.g. Iran, another conflict, AI ethics) launches, audiences say "that's the Condition" — awareness has preceded the application.

---

## 8. Governance and risk

- **Human approval:** All public-facing copy, graphics, and distribution decisions require operator/principal approval. Drafts may be machine-generated.
- **No appropriation:** The letter is offered; it is not claimed by any single institution or tradition. Campaign should not imply "we own the Condition" — rather "we are making it available."
- **Tone:** Invitational, not accusatory. "We are asking you to read and consider" — not "you have failed." Respect for religious and secular audiences alike.
- **Backfire:** If the letter is perceived as attacking religion or as a single tradition's proselytism, pause and recalibrate. Messaging should be "every tradition carries both; make the seam visible" — not "your tradition is the problem."

---

## 9. Summary

A public awareness campaign for the Simple Condition aims to make the letter, the seam, and "one subject, many tongues" known to religious leaders, faith communities, secular coordination-minded audiences, educators, and the general public. Channels: direct distribution of the letter, pulpit and social (using existing kits where possible), op-eds, education one-pagers, and optional translation and video. Phases: foundation → seed → amplify → sustain. Success = recognition of the idea and demand for it, so that application campaigns (Iran, AI ethics, others) can build on an already-familiar framework. All public ship requires human approval; tone is invitational and respectful.
