# Viral Faith Communities Kit — Virtue + Friendship

Social-media formats for faith communities to share in a way that **shows the virtue of their own tradition** and **expresses desire for friendship**, not guilt or critique. Shareable = identity-positive. Human approval required before distribution.

---

## Design principle

**Virtue:** "My faith teaches X" — one line that names what their tradition holds dear (mercy, sacredness of life, peace, seeing the face).  
**Friendship:** "We want Y" — we want peace, we want to see your face, we want to meet you, we're not here to fight.

Outcome: Sharing this makes the sharer look good (proud of their tradition, open to others). That drives virality.

---

We want friendship — not war.

## Format 2: Carousel (3 slides)

**Slide 1 — Virtue**  
"My [faith / tradition] teaches _____."  
*Pre-fill by tradition or leave blank for user.*

**Slide 2 — Friendship**  
"We don't want to fight. We want to see your face. We want peace."  
*Or:* "We want friendship — not targets. We want your name, not your category."

**Slide 3 — Invitation**  
"Whatever your tradition — if you want the same, say it. Share it. Demand the vote."  
*Hashtags:* #SeeTheFace #DemandTheVote #ChooseTheSeam

**Design note:** Same visual style each slide (font, palette). Slide 1 = one line. Slide 2 = one line. Slide 3 = CTA + hashtags. Minimal; high contrast for stories/feed.

## Format 3: Story / short video (15–30 sec)

**Script (voiceover or text-on-screen):**
- "My faith teaches that every life is sacred."
- "We're not here to fight. We're here for friendship."
- "See the face. Demand the vote. End the war."
- [Optional] "Whatever you believe — if you want peace, say it."

**Visual:** Speaker face, or simple text on solid/soft background. No graphic violence. Warm, open tone.

## Format 4: "I'm [faith]. I want _____." (fill-in tweet/post)

**Template:**  
"I'm [Christian / Muslim / Jewish / Buddhist / Hindu / Sikh / a person of faith]. My tradition teaches [one line]. I want friendship — not war. I want to see your face. #SeeTheFace #DemandTheVote"

**Example:**  
"I'm Christian. My tradition teaches that every person is made in the image of God. I want friendship — not war. I want to see your face. #SeeTheFace #DemandTheVote"

**Viral hook:** Others reply with their tradition + same structure. Thread or quote-tweet = "I'm Muslim. My tradition teaches…" "I'm Jewish. My tradition teaches…" Builds a coalition of virtue + friendship.

## Why this goes viral

1. **Pride, not guilt** — "My faith teaches X" = I'm proud of my tradition. Shareable.
2. **Friendship as desire** — "We want friendship" = I'm open, I'm not the enemy. Relatable.
3. **Identity-safe** — Each tradition gets its own line; no one tradition is centred.
4. **Participatory** — Fill-in and "I'm X, I want Y" invite others to post their version.
5. **Clear ask** — See the face, demand the vote. One action, one hashtag.

## Localisation

- Translate the two-line template (virtue + friendship) into Arabic, Farsi, Hebrew, Spanish, Mandarin, etc.
- Keep hashtags in English for cross-community findability, or add local equivalents (#أرى_الوجه, etc.) for regional spread.
- Tradition-specific lines can be translated so clergy and influencers post in the language of their community.
