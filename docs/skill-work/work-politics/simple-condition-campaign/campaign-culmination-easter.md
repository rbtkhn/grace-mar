# Campaign Culmination: Easter

**Purpose:** Plan the public awareness campaign so it **culminates at Easter** — maximum coordinated impact on Easter weekend (Holy Week through Easter Sunday), with phases backward-planned from that date. **Human approval required** before any public ship.

**Easter (moveable):** e.g. April 5, 2026; March 28, 2027. Adjust all dates below by year. This plan uses **Easter Sunday = E** and counts backward in weeks.

---

## 1. Why Easter as culmination

- **Christian majority in US** — Largest single bloc of the target audience (clergy, congregations) is in church on Easter; highest attendance of the year. One coordinated ask: "Read this on Easter weekend."
- **Thematic fit** — Resurrection, new beginning, "the door is open," peace as the condition for what we await. The message can be framed as consonant with Easter hope without appropriating the day: peace as condition, not reward; we can be the ones who open the door.
- **Single focal moment** — A single weekend when clergy who have been seeded can read the pulpit reading (or a short Easter-specific version), social pushes peak, and media can run "churches worldwide read the same message."
- **Multifaith note** — Easter is Christian. Jewish, Muslim, and other traditions are not asked to "culminate" on Easter; for them, the culmination is the same *message* (peace as condition, seam, one subject many tongues) and the same *assets*, with their own high-visibility moments (e.g. Ramadan, High Holidays) as optional parallel peaks, or simply continuous rollout. The *campaign calendar* peaks at Easter; the *message* is for all.

## 2. Backward calendar from Easter Sunday (E)

| Phase | Window (before E) | Goal | Key actions |
|-------|-------------------|------|-------------|
| **Foundation** | E minus 8–10 weeks | Assets and list ready | Lock one-pager, 4–6 quote graphics, 2–3 short videos. Finalise Easter-specific pulpit cut (see §4). Build clergy list (Christian: Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox) and educator list. |
| **Launch** | E minus 6–7 weeks | Viral spike; first awareness | Ship graphics and videos. Seed to clergy: "We're building toward Easter. Will you read this to your congregation on Easter weekend?" Seed to educators and influencers. First "door is open" and "children's question" wave. |
| **Spread** | E minus 4–5 weeks | Depth; repetition; commitment | 90-sec video and letter summary live. Second wave of graphics (rotate hook). Clergy confirm: "I'll read on Easter." Educator and UGC: "My tradition teaches this." Op-eds or podcast: "A letter for Easter." |
| **Holy Week** | E minus 1 week | Final push; confirmations | Reminder to clergy: "Easter reading attached. Thank you." Social: "This Easter, hundreds of congregations will hear the same message: the door is open. Peace is the condition." Media: pitch "Easter weekend: churches read the Condition." |
| **Culmination** | **E weekend (Holy Saturday – Easter Sunday)** | Coordinated peak | **Easter Sunday (and optionally Holy Saturday vigil):** Clergy who committed read the pulpit reading (or Easter cut). Social: "Today, [X] congregations heard: the door is open. We're the ones holding it shut." Aggregate and thank. One coordinated post or short video: "Easter 20XX: one message, many tongues." |
| **Sustain** | E plus 1–2 weeks | Capture and extend | Collate: how many read; where; one or two testimonies (with permission). "What you did this Easter" recap. Tie to next step: "The Condition doesn't end at Easter. Use the children's question. Share the one-pager." |

## 4. Easter-specific assets (optional)

- **Easter pulpit cut** — Shortened pulpit reading (60 sec) that opens with one line tying to Easter: e.g. "This Easter we celebrate that the door was opened. The Simple Condition asks: we can be the ones who keep it open. Every tradition carries beauty and a blade…" Then merge into the universal reading from "Our traditions teach that every human life is sacred." Optional; some clergy may prefer the full universal or tradition-specific reading with no Easter lead.
- **Easter social line** — "This Easter: the door is open. We're the ones holding it shut. Peace is the condition." For graphics and posts during Holy Week and Easter Sunday.
- **Easter recap asset** — "On Easter 20XX, [N] congregations in [X] traditions heard the same message: the door is open. One subject, many tongues." For use in E+1 week.

---

## 5. Clergy ask (Easter-specific)

**Email / DM to clergy (Launch and again in Spread):**

> We're running a short campaign that culminates on Easter weekend. The message is simple: peace is the condition, not the reward; every tradition carries beauty and a blade; we can make the seam visible and choose the beauty. We're asking Christian clergy to read a 90-second pulpit reading to their congregation on Easter Sunday (or Holy Saturday vigil). No other commitment. Attached: [universal reading] and [Catholic / Baptist / etc. tradition-specific reading]. If you're willing, reply "I'll read it" and we'll add you to the count. Thank you.

**Holy Week reminder:**

> Easter is this Sunday. If you're reading the Condition reading, thank you. Here's the link again: [reading]. After Easter we'll send a short recap. Peace.

## 6. Multifaith and secular (no Easter dependency)

- **Jewish, Muslim, other traditions** — Same assets and message; no requirement to tie to Easter. Invite them to use the pulpit reading (or tradition-specific version) on a high-visibility day of their choice (e.g. Ramadan, High Holidays) or any weekend. The "culmination" calendar is Christian-focused; the *campaign* is for everyone.
- **Secular** — Coordination and seam messaging continue as in [public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy.md](public-awareness-viral-penetration-strategy.md). No Easter tie-in required. Optional: one secular line during Holy Week: "This weekend many churches will hear a message about peace as condition. You don't have to be religious to share it: the door is open. We're the ones holding it shut."

---

## 7. Success at culmination

- **Clergy:** N congregations (target: set by list size and conversion) read the pulpit reading on Easter Sunday or Holy Saturday.
- **Social:** Peak impressions and shares on Easter weekend; "Easter 20XX: one message, many tongues" post or video.
- **Media:** At least one pickup (religious press, mainstream) framing "churches read the Condition on Easter."
- **Recognition:** The phrase "the door is open" or "the Simple Condition" is associated with Easter weekend for participants; sets up sustain phase (children's question, one-pager, application campaigns).

---

## 8. Summary

Plan the campaign so that **Easter Sunday (and optionally Holy Saturday)** is the culmination: backward-plan from E with Foundation (E−10 to E−8), Launch (E−7 to E−6), Spread (E−5 to E−4), Holy Week reminder (E−1), Culmination (E weekend), Sustain (E+1–2). Christian clergy are asked to read the pulpit reading on Easter; Easter-specific short assets (60-sec Easter cut, Easter social line, recap) are optional. Multifaith and secular audiences get the same message and assets without Easter dependency. Success = coordinated peak on Easter weekend plus recap and extension into sustain. All public ship requires human approval.
