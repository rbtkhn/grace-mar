## The executive branch has policy briefs and recommendations. It rarely has a **tool** that only surfaces options and perspectives and leaves the decision to you — no silent logging, no single "answer."

We deploy an **assistant brain** that does exactly that. **We never give you one answer.** Get the full field in one place — no extra meetings to gather every perspective. Pre-decision checkpoint: high-quality decision point with full context; then you direct what happens next.

**What it is:** A persistent orientation layer the principal or staff can query in the flow of work. Optionally you can state your instinct first ("Where are you leaning?"); the system then shows the full field and where your read sits relative to the perspectives. You get **structured options** through **fixed perspectives** — not one recommendation. Tensions between perspectives are **preserved, not resolved**; when two perspectives conflict we label it ("Tension: A and C conflict here") and **do not resolve**. The assistant never writes to your record unless you explicitly say "save" or "record." You decide; the system serves.

**Guarantees:** Your session is sovereign. No silent logging. No training on your data. No retention unless you say "record." Nothing is written to any long-term or institutional record unless you explicitly invoke relay.

**Polyphonic cognition:** The product is built on **polyphonic cognition** — multiple voices or perspectives held at once, without collapsing into a single resolution. The implementation uses fixed slots (e.g. A = legitimacy/orientation, B = structure/constraints, C = liability/mechanism, D = optional civ-mem lens). So you always see the same three (or four) angles; no relabeling, no hidden perspective. Duty of competence: the option set includes the full range, including at least one accommodation or reversal option (e.g. "don't escalate," "delay," "reframe").

**What it is not:** Not a recommender. Not a writer to your record by default. Not a replacement for NSC, cabinet, or staff. It is an **additional layer**: options and perspectives in the present, so the principal can orient with the full field in view.

**Optional dimension:** If the institution has ingested the civ-mem corpus (purpose of history, condition, seam, one subject many tongues), the assistant can include that as **one perspective among others** — "through the coordination lens: is the seam visible? One subject, many tongues." The content is not secret; the **system** that applies it is the product.

**Method:** **Polyphonic cognition protocol** — when to query (before call/decision), read A/B/C and tensions, relay only if you say "record." Staff training: [cheat-sheet-polyphonic-cognition-protocol.md](cheat-sheet-polyphonic-cognition-protocol.md).

**Pilot:** We propose a **90-day pilot** in one office: deploy the assistant (or a minimal version), train users on the protocol, deliver the cheat sheet. Fixed fee. Success = renewal or expansion. One principal, full field. No committee. You decide. The principal remains sovereign; the assistant brain serves.

**Research alignment:** The design aligns with prescriptive AI research on auditing human judgment in high-stakes environments: the system **tests** the principal's judgment against the full field of options rather than replacing it (see e.g. "Auditing Human Decision-Making in High-Stakes Environments via Prescriptive AI," arXiv 2512.04480).
