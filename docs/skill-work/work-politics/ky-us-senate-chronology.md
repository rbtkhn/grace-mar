# Kentucky — United States Senate chronology (work-politics source)

**Status:** Draft reference. **Not** Record truth. Use for WORK framing and the **jurisdiction-campaign-history** Cursor skill (Design A: `office: senate` or `stack`).

Kentucky elects **two** U.S. senators: **Class 2** and **Class 3** (staggered six-year terms, with specials and appointments in between). The same person may hold a seat in **non-consecutive** periods (e.g. John Sherman Cooper in Class 2).

## Campaign / framing hygiene

- **Statewide vs House:** Senate messaging is **statewide**; do not imply a single CD “owns” a senator’s story without saying so.
- **Appointments and specials:** Short tenures (Stanfill, Humphreys, Withers, etc.) still matter for “who filled the seat when” narratives—cite the official roster, do not invent succession.
- **Same name, different era:** Party labels change meaning over centuries; pre–Civil War “Democratic-Republican” is not today’s Democratic Party.
