# cici-ai work lanes

These lanes split the `cici-ai` operator surface into three durable work territories. They are WORK-layer coordination surfaces inside grace-mar; they are not Cici's Record and do not replace governed state in any Cici instance repository.

## Operating arc

The lanes should be read as a monthly operating sequence:

1. **Telegram intake** - invite, explain, ask for one concrete artifact, and preserve chat evidence.
2. **Core setup / governance** - keep the Cici repo, prompts, policies, and safety boundary coherent enough that beginners can copy the path safely.
3. **Progress proof** - turn member replies and repo artifacts into evidence-labeled status, task completion, and coaching decisions.

In short: **Telegram creates motion; Core keeps the path safe; Progress proves what actually happened.**

| Sequence | Lane | Purpose | Primary questions |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | [cici-ai-telegram](cici-ai-telegram/README.md) | Telegram team operations, posts, norms, applicant intake, and community communication. | What should be posted? Who needs a reply? What needs to be recorded from chat? |
| 2 | [cici-ai-core](cici-ai-core/README.md) | Cici/OB1 instance architecture, prompts, governed-state, repo migration, safety, and technical implementation. | What should change in the repo? What needs proposal/approval? What is safe to automate? |
| 3 | [cici-ai-progress](cici-ai-progress/README.md) | Member progress, applicant table, task completion, proof packets, scholarships, and cohort metrics. | Who has done what? What is the next task? What evidence supports the status? |

## Monthly rhythm

| Week | Emphasis | Concrete output |
|---|---|---|
| Week 1 | Intake clarity | Telegram asks for one artifact at a time; first applicant replies become evidence pointers. |
| Week 2 | Setup coherence | Core lane resolves repo identity, setup prompts, and safety instructions that beginners encounter first. |
| Week 3 | Progress proof | Dashboard separates self-report, visible GitHub evidence, first-task proof, and scholarship-readiness review. |
| Week 4 | Review and reset | Weekly review notes summarize what worked, what confused people, and what the next cohort ask should be. |

## Operating rule

Use the lane that owns the next concrete action. If an item touches more than one lane, write it in the lane of immediate action and cross-link the other lane rather than duplicating status.

Do not optimize all lanes equally every week. Choose the lane that is blocking the arc:

- If people are silent or confused, start with **Telegram**.
- If instructions or repo identity are unsafe, start with **Core**.
- If activity exists but cannot be trusted or counted, start with **Progress**.

## Boundary

Lane files may summarize, stage, and route. They must not silently rewrite Cici governed-state, applicant claims, payment commitments, or identity facts.
