# BrewMind + business companion — five verbal questions (field)

> **Purpose:** **Face-to-face** visits: Xavier carries a **printed pamphlet** and asks **exactly these five questions** out loud. **Relationship and trust first** — light signal, not a formal survey.  
> **Territory:** [work-cici](README.md) — WORK artifact.  
> **Pair with:** [brewmind-philippines-onboarding-guide.md](brewmind-philippines-onboarding-guide.md) (bundle hub) · [brewmind-business-plan.md](brewmind-business-plan.md) · [brewmind-visual-style.md](brewmind-visual-style.md) (pamphlet / print).

---

## Operator notes

- **Time:** Aim for **5–8 minutes** of real conversation; let them talk.  
- **Pamphlet:** Leave it **after** question 5 (or when natural) — one clear takeaway they can read alone.  
- **Data:** Jot **only** what helps follow-up (name, role, one line on interest). No scoring sheet in the moment.  
- **Privacy:** If you save contact details, say it’s for **follow-up only** unless they agree otherwise (Philippines DPA if you store personally identifiable data).

---

## The five questions (ask in order)

**1.** “What’s **one thing that eats your time every week** — the same questions, training new people, or something you wish always happened the same way?”

*(Listen. Acknowledge. Don’t pitch yet.)*

**2.** “**Hypothetically** — if you had a **private assistant** that only answered from info **you** approved — menu, hours, allergens, house rules, tone — would that feel **useful here**, or **not really a priority** right now?”

*(If confused, one line: “Not posting on your social for you — more like a smart FAQ your staff could trust, under your control.”)*

**3.** “We’re piloting **BrewMind**: customers **scan a QR** on a cup or table card, get a **short daily AI tip** while they’re in a place like this. Does that sound like something you’d **want to explore later**, or **probably not your lane**?”

*(No pressure — you’re testing fit, not closing.)*

**4.** “**What would we need to get right** — for **you** — before you’d even want a longer conversation about either of those?”

*(This is the relationship question: fees, branding, effort, trust, mall rules — let them vent or dream.)*

**5.** “Can I **leave this pamphlet** with you? And **who’s the right person** to stay in touch with if we follow up in a couple of weeks — or would you rather **one email** and we leave it there?”

*(Get permission and a name; business card exchange if it feels natural.)*
