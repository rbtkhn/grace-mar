# work-build-ai offers

Canonical offer surface for the `work-build-ai` business layer.

Use this file to keep the territory commercially legible:

- lead with client outcomes, not architecture jargon
- keep the first engagement bounded
- sell diagnosis before large implementation
- treat portability, provenance, and human control as the wedge

---

## Current offers

| Offer | Audience | Problem | Outcome | Delivery shape | Entry ask | Notes |
|------|----------|---------|---------|----------------|-----------|-------|
| **AI system diagnostic** | AI-native teams; consultancies; runtime-heavy operators | They have agent or automation sprawl but cannot clearly see lock-in, provenance, or governance risk | One memo showing current-state risks, architectural gaps, and the best next implementation path | Diagnostic memo + architecture summary | Share one active workflow or runtime stack | Best first offer. Easiest to explain and easiest to scope. |
| **Portable identity and memory architecture pass** | Teams using more than one runtime or planning to | Identity, memory, and continuity live implicitly inside one vendor/runtime | A clear `record` vs `runtime` separation and a proposed export / handback contract | Architecture pass + proposed contract | Review current memory and runtime setup | Best follow-on after a diagnostic. |
| **Human-gated workflow implementation sprint** | Teams that already know one workflow needs governance and provenance | Workflows are useful but too opaque, too auto-merged, or too hard to audit | One bounded workflow implemented with approval boundaries, provenance expectations, and review visibility | Fixed-scope sprint | Agree one concrete workflow surface first | Do not offer as the first engagement unless the pain is already clear. |

---

## Default business framing

| Layer | Guidance |
|------|----------|
| **Lead with** | portability, human control, auditability, and operational clarity |
| **Do not lead with** | "AI agents," "workflows," or "tooling" without tying them to buyer pain |
| **First ask** | a diagnostic or architecture pass, not a giant buildout |
| **Proof style** | use operational before/after lines from [proof-ledger.md](proof-ledger.md) |

---

## Guardrail

Do not claim that the territory is already a mature firm. These offers define the business direction, not a fictional sales track record.
