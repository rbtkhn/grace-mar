# Cici pilot — low-cost propagation checklist (operator WORK)

> **Audience:** Operator + Xavier. **Purpose:** Practical **organic / near-zero-budget** ways to reach **many at-bats** for **10 real pilot users** in the **Neural Sandbox** / [Cici](https://github.com/Xavier-x01/Cici) cohort (Philippines context). **Pairs with:** [cici-community-mission-operator.md](cici-community-mission-operator.md) (mission + drop-off). **Not:** paid campaign playbook; not Record.

**“Mass propagation” here** means **high reach per hour and per peso**, not TV-scale ads. Trust-heavy invites (fork + own Supabase) favor **referrals, local groups, and borrowed lists** over cold reach.

---

## 1. Referral / “bring one person” loop (highest ROI at $0)

- Ask **after a small win** (e.g. fork created), not only at signup — product-led growth practice is to prompt invites **in context** ([referral / viral loop overview](https://thegrowthterminal.com/blog/viral-loops-that-actually-work-the-math-behind-referral-growth/); [referral mechanics](https://getathenic.com/blog/viral-coefficient-referral-program-optimization)).
- **Double-sided** framing often outperforms one-sided: both people get something non-monetary (shoutout, “cohort buddy,” 10 min help).
- **Copy:** Keep a **two-sentence forwardable blurb** in the pin or welcome (operator can maintain one canonical block).

---

## 2. Facebook **Groups** (not Pages) — PH-native organic reach

- Group posts often get **stronger organic reach than Pages** because conversation is prioritized ([Facebook group marketing — reach angle](https://socialrails.com/blog/facebook-group-marketing-strategy); [groups vs broadcast framing](https://www.posteverywhere.ai/blog/facebook-groups-for-business)).
- **Technique:** Join **2–4** relevant PH groups (city, students, freelancers, MSME). **Contribute** first; then **one** value post + soft invite — follow each group’s promo rules.
- **Hyperlocal** (city / campus) usually beats “Philippines-wide” for trust.

---

## 3. Telegram organic habits

- Clear **pinned welcome**, steady **short** posts (wins, stuck threads), **polls** / prompts so the group looks alive ([Telegram organic growth themes](https://telegramgrowthstudio.com/blog/telegram-organic-growth-strategies.html); [group-oriented habits](https://vocal.media/lifehack/how-to-grow-your-telegram-group-organically)).
- **Cross-links:** `t.me` invite in **bios** (TikTok, Instagram, X), email footer, personal site — same pattern as cross-promotion in [Telegram growth notes](https://stillviral.com/how-to-grow-on-telegram/).

---

## 4. Borrowed audiences (zero ad spend)

- **One forward** from a trusted host: student org, coworking, church/youth, small business cluster — ask for **permission to share** one paragraph, not a formal partnership.
- **Admin swap:** Non-competing community admins **mutual mention** (your cohort ↔ their group) when audiences overlap.

---

## 5. Short video (optional experiment)

- **60s** “I’m learning Claude + my own repo; free PH group — link in bio.” Low cost if **consistent**; treat as **optional** until organic baseline works — cross-link pattern aligns with [Telegram growth discussion](https://stillviral.com/how-to-grow-on-telegram/).

---

## 6. WhatsApp / Messenger / Viber (PH reality)

- Many cohorts discover offers in **Messenger** or **WhatsApp** first. **Permission-based** forward to **one or two** groups; avoid spam — burning trust is expensive.

---

## 7. Reddit / forums (rules-heavy)

- **Help first**, link only where rules allow (weekly threads, profile). Drive-by link drops **hurt** trust and get removed.

---

## 8. Physical anchor (almost $0)

- **QR** on one flyer: library / coworking / campus board → **one landing line** + Telegram invite. Good for **a few qualified** joins if the QR matches a **clear** promise.

---

## Defer until there is proof

- **Paid Meta/TikTok** — only tiny tests after organic works.
- **Influencer deals** — only **micro**, **aligned**, **low** or **free** (e.g. one story share).

---

## Metrics (same spine as mission doc)

Track **stages**, not vanity impressions: **joined → GitHub username → fork → Supabase project → first working session** (define “working” minimally). Measure **where people stall**, not raw headcount.
