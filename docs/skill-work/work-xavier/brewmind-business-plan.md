## 1. Executive summary

**BrewMind** pairs **specialty coffee culture** with **approachable AI literacy** (“sip, learn, earn”). This plan assumes **USD ~10k seed** and recommends a **network-first** go-to-market in **Cebu City**: enroll **independent cafés and similar venues** in a BrewMind **program** (branded cups, QR, vouchers, light incentives) instead of operating a dedicated BrewMind-owned shop first. Cebu is the **density pilot**; a repeatable **partner playbook** becomes the export for other cities.

**12-month proof targets (illustrative):** 8–12 active partner venues in 2–3 geographic clusters; meaningful **QR scan → signup** conversion; **paid memberships** and/or **B2B workshop** revenue; partner **reorder rate** on branded materials.

---

## 2. Strategic choice: network vs owned shop

| Dimension | **Partner network (recommended for seed)** | **Owned micro-café (capex path)** |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Capital | Low — kits, digital, BD, legal, small field budget | High — rent deposit, build-out, equipment, inventory |
| Control | Weaker — brand and experience vary by venue | Strong — full ops control |
| Speed | Parallel pilots across many nodes | Single point of failure until break-even |
| Core skill | BD, partner success, light franchise-style ops | Barista ops, lease, F&B compliance |

**Recommendation:** Use seed for **program + distribution**. Defer owned shop until metrics justify capex or a strategic flagship location.

## 3. Concept and positioning

**One line:** BrewMind is the **learning brand and incentives layer**; partner venues are the **physical nodes**.

**Customer promise:** Great coffee context + **short daily AI micro-lessons**, points/vouchers, optional **membership** tracks (aligned with public site: Brew Basics → Mind Builder → Earn Elite).

**Optional long-term moat (not day-one):** Help Elite / corporate customers **stand up a personal companion-self-style workspace** (governed Record, gate, Voice) with **stewardship and onboarding** — dependency through **habit and service**, with **clear portability** (export, their repo, their merge authority). See grace-mar [conceptual framework](../../conceptual-framework.md) for triad and gate language.

## 4. Target customers

- **B2C:** Students, young pros, freelancers, remote workers (habit + community).
- **Venue partners:** Independent cafés first; chains often block third-party branding — validate contractually.
- **B2B (month 4+):** Half-day **Tier 1–style** workshops (from AI Learning Plan doc): BPO, schools, SMEs — revenue on top of network distribution.

---

## 5. Partner program (pilot design)

**Partner kit (v1):** Branded **cups / sleeves**, **table tent**, **one-line staff pitch** (“Scan for today’s free AI tip”), optional **QR on receipt**.

**Digital:** QR → mobile landing: waitlist, lesson of the day, membership upsell. Prefer **web-first**; native app only after repeat use is proven.

**Incentives:** e.g. first-drink upgrade, pastry, or unique **voucher** — redeemed through a simple system; partner reimbursement on a **weekly or monthly** cadence. Design against **fraud** (screenshots, copied codes): prefer **single-use codes**, staff-confirmed redemption, or device-bound flows where feasible.

**Cebu pilot shape:** 8–12 partners in **2–3 clusters** (e.g. IT Park, university-adjacent strips, remote-worker pockets) so consumers encounter BrewMind **more than once**.

**Pilot length:** ~90 days with explicit **scale / kill** rules (e.g. reorder rate on cups, scans per venue per week).

## 6. Economics and partner settlement (pick 1–2 for clarity)

- **Monthly marketing / placement fee** per venue (simplest ops; harder sell).
- **Wholesale margin** on branded SKUs (cups, bundles) sold through partner.
- **Rev-share** on BrewMind-attributed upsells (often **operationally heavy** at tiny scale — defer or pilot with one POS-friendly partner).
- **Sponsored cohorts:** one employer funds **N** redemptions across the network.

## 7. Marketing (low budget)

- Waitlist and **opening / pilot** narrative on existing site.
- One **consistent** short-form format (e.g. 60s lesson clip filmed at partner venues).
- Student org or one **Chamber / coworking** tie-in (aligns with AI Learning Plan partner thinking).

## 8. Legal, privacy, admin (Philippines — verify locally)

Business registration, **BIR**, food-adjacent rules if vouchers tie to F&B, barangay or mall rules for signage, **Data Privacy Act** compliance for emails and lesson analytics. Budget **accounting + legal** line in seed.

### How the spend differs (one view)

- **Owned shop:** Dollars buy the **right to operate one node** — lease, equipment, inventory. Wrong rent choice ties up most of $10k before the first validated cohort.
- **Network plan:** Dollars buy **reusable IP, physical brand surfaces (cups/QR), variable incentive float, BD, and legal** — many partner attempts and **attribution data** instead of one fixed site. **Flagship** real estate can wait for revenue or round 2.

---

## 11. Risks and mitigations

- **Partner churn / passive placement** — Win clear: foot traffic, basket lift, or fee; audit displays monthly.
- **Brand inconsistency** — Kits, photos, short training; drop partners who won’t comply.
- **Voucher fraud** — Single-use, staff validation, caps.
- **Overbuilding software** — Notion/PDF lesson track + spreadsheet ops until volume warrants automation.

---

## 12. Success definition (seed stage)

Not “own Cebu AI.” **Proof:** attributed engagement, **paid conversion**, partner **reorders**, and **one** repeatable B2B SKU — then raise **round 2** for app, staff, or flagship real estate.
