**30 March 2026** — **Resignations and Appointments:** The Holy Father appointed **Archbishop Paolo Rudelli**, titular of **Mesembria**, until then **apostolic nuncio in Colombia**, as **Substitute for General Affairs of the Secretariat of State**; **Archbishop Edgar Peña Parra**, until then Substitute, as **apostolic nuncio in Italy and the Republic of San Marino**; **Archbishop Petar Rajič**, until then nuncio in Italy and San Marino, as **Prefect of the Papal Household** (same bulletin lists further **Pontifical Commission** / **Dicastery** member appointments). **Primary:** [Holy See Press Office — 30.03.2026](https://press.vatican.va/content/salastampa/en/bollettino/pubblico/2026/03/30/260330c.html).

## Strategy hook

**Hypothesis:** **Substitute** turnover is a **pure administrative-layer** signal: who runs the pipe between pope and **nunciature** network — relevant to timing of diplomatic notes, audience cadence, and crisis routing, and **orthogonal** to homiletic **mind–soul** lines unless the pope explicitly ties them together. **Rudelli**’s move from **Colombia** back to the **Section for General Affairs** he once served suggests **Leo XIV** wants a known operator inside **General Affairs** — consistent with the **[thesis stub](../2026-04-03-modern-rome-papacy-thesis-stub.md)** **Leo** talent-stack / Curia reconfiguration read (**hypothesis**).

## Falsifier / counter-read

**Counter-read:** Substitute changes can be routine succession or rotation with no strategic shift; work-strategy outcomes may stay invisible for months. **Peña Parra** to the **Italy** nunciature may matter more for **Italy–Holy See** interface than **Rudelli**’s title alone.

**From thesis falsifiers:** Administrative crisis headlines (finance, abuse law, personnel) can drown reform narrative — if this appointment is read only as scandal churn, the “talent-stack” hypothesis **overfits**.
