### Verbatim

Guest voice, Monday 27 Apr 2026. Crooke: most of what is said publicly is propaganda; the Islamabad Kushner–Witkoff–Araghchi meeting, as told by Trump, is a “fantasy.” Iran’s ten points already stated the bid; re-entering a JCPOA-style arrangement is not neutral—it is a return to a cage of U.S. bases, sanctions, IAEA, and UN pressure. The tactical shift, as he reads it, is to defer the nuclear file until the war, Hormuz, blockade, seizures, and the Lebanon/Hezbollah file are in play. Qalibaf’s “high cards” against a U.S. blockade: Bab al-Mandab, Red Sea, pipelines, versus a porous or reluctant U.S. naval cordon; which clock is shorter, Hormuz tolls damaging the West or blockade strangling Iran? He rejects a simple “Iran is about to be broke on a set calendar” story, citing evasive tanker runs, high state take, a large “oil at sea” buffer, and the claim that a civilizational revisionist state does not simply yield to a baseball-bat coercer; no 430kg trophy hand to Washington. Hormuz matters not only for crude but for helium, gallium, aluminum, and pump-line goods that fail “slightly worse, then all at once.”

Trump is the first major obstacle in Crooke’s reading: long-standing need to outdo Obama on a “better” JCPOA, with the only unambiguous “win” photo being a uranium trophy; a Vance-shaped enrichment-and-monitoring package can still be rejected as “just Obama’s deal.” A Barnes-circulated White House health/temper narrative appears; reporting that Caine and (mostly) the principals balked at further mass electric/civilian infrastructure attack as a war crime rather than a valid military line; forks including Isfahan, Kent’s Carter echo, more bombing without eliminating missiles, and midterm clock. Gulf capitals balk at tolls; Crooke defends a toll as prosaic and, in his non-lawyer reading, consistent with UNCLOS in a strait that lies in Iranian and Omani waters. IRI public mood, including younger mobilized columns, hardens against “talks while the houses burn.”

Israel is the second barricade: messianic, settlement-aligned, and eschatological pressures (Gershom Scholem invoked), Katz in apocalyptic register, Netanyahu under domestic heat for “failed” Iran/Hezbollah storylines, Smotrich on needing a “big war” to finish a plan—versus an American interest in ending a war. Von der Leyen’s “values” and regime-change posturing as making sanctions unliftable; in Crooke’s phrasing, von der Leyen is aligned in part with the Israel-shaped containment of Iran. On attrition and the larger war: time favors Iran; Europe is a few weeks from a cascade of consumer pain; the strategic layer is the petro-dollar, Gulf “excess savings” to Wall Street since 1973, the destruction of reliable physical versus paper oil pricing, and a stated Iranian interest in severing a Microsoft-Amazon-dominant, dollar, and IAEA umbrella over relations.
