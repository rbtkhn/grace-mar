### Verbatim

Guest voice, Friday 10 Apr 2026. Mearsheimer: apparent “confusion” is overstated. Read a pair of Trump posts the same day as a single story of desperation to stop a war that could plausibly tip the world into a 1930s-scale depression. Morning: genocidal-tinged coercive rhetoric as an intra-day gambit to avoid having to go up a ladder of escalation (which he has long argued the U.S. would lose on). Evening: ceasefire and negotiation on the basis of Iran’s ten points — i.e. the U.S.–Israel maximalist package is not what Washington will take into talks; the only off-ramp he sees is, in his words, a form of conceding the bargaining frame to Tehran.

On Diesen’s “both ways” and Hegseth: no durable ceasefire while Hormuz is closed in sequence with Israeli operations in Lebanon vis-à-vis Hezbollah; any victory lap is “lipstick on a pig” relative to the four unmet pre-war demands, Iran’s Hormuz and missile position versus late February, and the Houthis plus Bab al-Mandab as a second chokepoint story.

The strong-man-to-war-president narrative, MAGA and evangelical fractures (Carlson, Owens, Jones, under-50 evangelicals), and broader alliance damage: unilateralist wrecking of institutions, allies, law; Europe blamed for inability to “break” Hormuz; “rogue elephant” distance for third countries; Vietnam point as defeat without ending raw material power but damaging projection, alliance trust, and East Asia pivot. Russia benefits from energy and UAF weapons diversion; China benefits from rebalanced U.S. presence. Gulf end-state may include not returning to prior baselines.

NATO: Europe as the likely Trump scapegoat; transatlantic partnership effectively gone; “meaningful NATO” in doubt by 2029; Vance successor scenario as uncertain buffer. Ukraine: if Iran is not over, U.S. focus and inventories do not ride to the rescue; Russian oil “needed” for the macro; Trump blames Europe for two fronts in his typical pattern; one tweet (host notes) can combine blame and Greenland distraction. Multipolar: since 2017; China and Russia are not the main “material new power” in his telling — lost influence and projection to U.S. policy failure are.
