### Chronicle

Mearsheimer rejects the **purely internal** Western media frame on the **late-2025 / January 2026** Iran protests. He lays out a **four-step** U.S.–Israel “tag team” playbook: **(1)** long-running **sanctions** / “maximum pressure” to deepen economic pain and incentivize revolt; **(2)** **late December 2025** onward, fomenting **violent** escalation (claims of **Mossad** on the ground, **CIA** alongside, **local agitators**, **Starlink** pre-positioned for comms if Tehran cuts networks), with **Trump**, **Pompeo**, and a **Mossad Farsi** message cited as cheerleading / coordination signals; **(3)** **Western media** aligning with the **peaceful protestors vs brutal regime** story and outsider innocence; **(4)** **U.S.** (and possibly **Israeli**) military **primed** to strike once unrest hits **critical mass** to **finish** the government and **fragment** the country.

**Failure mode** in his telling: the government **quashed** protests **decisively**, especially by **neutralizing Starlink**, so the **military coup de grace** could not follow—**Netanyahu** and **Trump** understood that, in his summary. He then **re-reads** the **June 2025 “12-Day war”** as **not** an Israeli/U.S. victory: **Israel** allegedly **wanted** the end more (missile **inventory** strain; Iran **improving** missile pressure); ties to reporting that **Netanyahu** asked **Trump** **not** to bomb **14 January 2026** over **counterstrike** exposure. On the **22 June 2025** nuclear-facilities strike, he contrasts **Trump’s** “**obliterated**” claim with a **DIA** assessment of **months** of setback only, and notes **little public** damage accounting on **enrichment** and **60%** uranium stock—raising **deterrent** incentives for Tehran.
