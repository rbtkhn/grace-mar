## 1. What this surface is

This surface is the thin bridge that converts civilizational and historical material into reusable strategy-grade objects for work-strategy.

It exists to make civilization_memory useful inside Grace-Mar without importing the full upstream corpus into local daily operations. It is not a mirror of civilization_memory, not a Record surface, and not an identity surface. Its role is practical: help the operator move from large historical or civilizational patterns to cleaner judgment, cleaner framing, and better decision support.

This surface should remain thin, selective, and reusable. It should prefer mechanisms over narrative summary, and case families over large prose blocks.

## 2. What this surface is not

This surface is not:

- a duplicate of civilization_memory
- a replacement for the History Notebook
- a replacement for the Strategy Notebook
- a place to store full essays
- a place to convert historical material into SELF or EVIDENCE truth
- a place to bypass gate discipline

This surface exists to improve WORK judgment, not to enlarge the Record.

## 3. Boundary rules

### 3.1 WORK-only
Everything here is WORK-only unless separately promoted through the proper review path.

### 3.2 No automatic Record effects
Nothing written here updates SELF, EVIDENCE, or any canonical identity surface.

### 3.3 No upstream mirroring
Do not copy large upstream files here. Only retain thin local abstractions that are repeatedly useful for strategy work.

### 3.4 Mechanism over ornament
Prefer mechanism language, fit conditions, mismatch conditions, and falsifiers over rhetorical flourish.

### 3.5 Reusability threshold
A pattern belongs here only if it is likely to be reused across multiple events, briefs, notes, or decision points.

## 4. How to use this surface

Use this surface when one or more of the following is true:

- a current event appears to rhyme with a recurring historical or civilizational pattern
- a strategy note needs a cleaner mechanism rather than a loose analogy
- a watch or decision point would benefit from case-backed framing
- the operator keeps reaching for the same historical references repeatedly
- a tri-frame judgment would improve from stable civilizational context

Typical flow:

1. Event, brief, transcript, or question enters work-strategy.
2. Check whether an existing case family or mechanism already applies.
3. If yes, cite the relevant family and record fit / mismatch / falsifier notes.
4. If no, create a resonance note or analogy audit rather than forcing a bad parallel.
5. If the pattern recurs, consider promotion into the local case index.
6. If it materially shapes judgment, link it into an active watch, strategy note, or decision point.

## 7. Active civilizational lenses

These are the default lenses for local strategy work. They are not exhaustive, but they should be preferred when evaluating new events or signals.

### 7.1 Legitimacy and continuity
How authority is justified, sustained, weakened, ritualized, or transferred.

Key questions:
- Is the system drawing strength from legitimacy, fear, inertia, or habit?
- Is continuity institutional, symbolic, spiritual, legal, or merely performative?
- Is the center preserving legitimacy or spending it?

### 7.2 Power geometry
How power is distributed across center, periphery, rivals, clients, buffers, and intermediaries.

Key questions:
- Who is central, who is peripheral, and who is exposed?
- What is the geometry of dependence?
- Which actor appears stronger than it really is because the map is being misread?

### 7.3 Material base and liabilities
The relation between claims of power and the actual material substrate that supports them.

Key questions:
- What funds the order?
- What can no longer be afforded?
- Which liabilities are hidden by prestige, debt, coercion, or temporary windfalls?

### 7.4 Institutional resilience and decay
Whether institutions are adaptive, rigid, hollow, parasitic, or merely ceremonial.

Key questions:
- Are institutions solving problems or preserving appearances?
- Is the structure durable, brittle, or zombie-like?
- Where has form detached from function?

### 7.5 Elite cohesion and fragmentation
Whether elite behavior supports strategic coherence or accelerates breakdown.

Key questions:
- Are elites coordinated or factionalized?
- Are they ruling, extracting, signaling, or panicking?
- Is dissent managed through integration or suppression?

### 7.6 Chokepoints, corridors, and exposure
Whether flows of trade, information, force, or legitimacy pass through vulnerable bottlenecks.

Key questions:
- What corridor or chokepoint matters most?
- Is movement externally open or internally governable?
- Does the actor control the node, merely transit through it, or depend on hostile guardianship?

### 7.7 Timing, sequencing, and exhaustion
Whether the order of moves matters more than the moves themselves.

Key questions:
- Is this actor early, late, trapped, or stalling?
- Is urgency a sign of strength or of diminishing options?
- Who benefits from delay and who benefits from compression?

### 7.8 Narrative authority
How stories, symbols, memory, and legitimacy interact with state behavior.

Key questions:
- Who defines the public meaning of events?
- What story is being preserved at high cost?
- Is narrative serving power, or compensating for the erosion of power?

## 8. Reusable case families

These are not full case essays. They are reusable strategic families meant to help the operator classify events faster and more carefully.

### 8.1 Imperial overextension
A center accumulates burdens faster than it can convert them into durable control.

Use when:
- commitments outpace capacity
- signaling substitutes for consolidation
- prestige obligations expand faster than material support

Common misuse:
- assuming every ambitious power is overextended
- confusing temporary strain with structural exhaustion

### 8.2 Legitimacy without performance
An order retains prestige, ritual, or symbolic standing after its practical competence declines.

Use when:
- public deference outlasts real effectiveness
- institutions remain obeyed while confidence erodes
- symbolism covers administrative decay

Common misuse:
- mistaking symbolic continuity for actual strategic durability

### 8.3 Administrative hollowing
The shell of governance remains intact while operational competence deteriorates.

Use when:
- procedures multiply but outcomes worsen
- the bureaucracy protects itself rather than the mission
- throughput declines while formal control expands

Common misuse:
- reducing all bureaucratic failure to corruption instead of structural overload

### 8.4 Chokepoint coercion
A strategic node is used to extract concessions, impose discipline, or raise costs on rivals.

Use when:
- transit, supply, finance, data, or geography becomes leverage
- coercion works through bottlenecks more than battlefield dominance

Common misuse:
- assuming chokepoint control equals complete system control

### 8.5 Peripheral drift
The edge of a system begins to loosen before the center fully perceives the danger.

Use when:
- allies hedge quietly
- local autonomy grows under formal loyalty
- compliance becomes selective rather than open rebellion

Common misuse:
- overreading every hedge as defection

### 8.6 Elite fragmentation under stress
A ruling layer becomes unable to maintain one coherent strategy.

Use when:
- narratives conflict across organs of power
- factions optimize for intra-elite position rather than state interest
- short-term blame management replaces long-term planning

Common misuse:
- mistaking noisy pluralism for breakdown

### 8.7 Delayed adaptation
An order sees the need to change but cannot change at the speed required.

Use when:
- reform is visible but too slow
- new realities are acknowledged rhetorically but resisted institutionally
- delay itself becomes the doctrine

Common misuse:
- assuming delay is always irrational rather than sometimes strategically useful

### 8.8 Prestige trap
Actors continue costly commitments because retreat would damage status or narrative authority.

Use when:
- withdrawal is materially sensible but politically intolerable
- symbolic stakes outweigh practical benefits
- leaders fear domestic or civilizational humiliation

Common misuse:
- confusing every reputational concern with a true prestige trap

### 8.9 Corridor vulnerability
A system depends on routes it does not fully secure or politically govern.

Use when:
- logistics, trade, energy, or political communication relies on exposed passageways
- internal circulation is weak or externally filterable

Common misuse:
- talking about movement in generic terms without distinguishing internal governability from external openness

### 8.10 Ritualized continuity
An order preserves forms, titles, ceremonies, and language as a stabilizer during stress.

Use when:
- continuity itself is a strategic asset
- ritual manages fear, succession, or legitimacy deficits
- symbolic order delays visible breakdown

Common misuse:
- dismissing ritual as merely decorative

### 8.11 Extraction without renewal
A center continues pulling value from its system while reinvestment, renewal, or integration declines.

Use when:
- taxation, rents, or tribute remain high while infrastructure, trust, or cohesion erodes
- the center consumes more than it regenerates

Common misuse:
- assuming all extraction is decadent rather than sometimes necessary for stabilization

### 8.12 Narrative substitution
Public storytelling expands as direct control, performance, or legitimacy weakens.

Use when:
- narrative management grows more intense as outcomes worsen
- information discipline compensates for weakening administrative or material control

Common misuse:
- treating narrative as unreal rather than as a real battlefield with real effects

## 9. Case-family usage rule

A case family should be used only when the operator can state all three:

- why this fits
- why this may not fit
- what would falsify the parallel

If those three cannot be stated clearly, use a resonance note instead of a stronger case-family claim.

## 10. Required local reasoning fields

Whenever this surface is invoked inside a strategy note, current-events analysis, or decision point, include the following mini-fields where practical:

- Case family or mechanism:
- Why this fits:
- Why this may not fit:
- Falsifier:
- Confidence: low | medium | high
- Use type: illustrative | mechanism | strong parallel

These fields exist to reduce hand-wavy analogy drift.

## 11. Relation to other work-strategy artifacts

### 11.1 Strategy Notebook
The Strategy Notebook is the primary daily judgment surface. This surface supplies reusable civilizational mechanisms that strategy writing may cite.

### 11.2 History Notebook
The History Notebook is the compressed local historical layer. It is the preferred place for richer operator-authored historical reasoning. This surface should point toward History Notebook chapters when repeated use justifies them.

### 11.3 Case index
The local case index should hold the most reusable strategy-grade cases. This surface describes the families and mechanisms that make those cases useful.

### 11.4 Current-events analysis
Current-events analysis should check this surface and the local case index before reaching for ad hoc historical analogy.

### 11.5 Decision points
Decision points may use this surface to frame strategic context, but recommendations remain operator judgment and WORK-only.

## 12. Creation rule for new entries

A new case family, mechanism, or civilizational dynamic may be added here only if at least one of the following is true:

- it has been reused across multiple strategy notes
- it materially improved a current-events analysis
- it shaped a decision point or watch in a non-trivial way
- it consistently reduces vague analogy and improves mechanism clarity
- it is likely to recur in a major work-strategy lane

If the pattern is interesting but not yet reusable, keep it in a resonance note or the History Notebook instead.

## 13. Review protocol

When updating this surface:

1. Prefer local case-index and notebook evidence first.
2. Check whether the proposed entry duplicates an existing family under different words.
3. Preserve mismatch and falsifier logic.
4. Keep entries thin.
5. Do not convert this into an essay collection.
6. If upstream civilization_memory review materially changes the pattern, note that in the upstream receipt.
7. If the content starts behaving like a stable doctrine note, consider promoting it into a more appropriate local artifact.

## 14. Minimal maintenance standard

This file is healthy when:

- it remains thin
- it remains useful
- it reduces analogical sloppiness
- it improves daily strategy writing
- it supports cleaner decision framing
- it does not become a hidden mirror of upstream civilization_memory

If it becomes large, repetitive, or essay-like, compress it.
