**Structural lens — summary (Pape-weighted):**  
Oil, gas, and chokepoint risk redistribute **who can impose costs** on the global economy. Even if “new world power” is debated, **Hormuz-sensitive** states face **strategic vulnerability** that changes **bargaining power** among U.S., Iran, Gulf exporters, and Asian consumers. **NATO** strain is a **separate axis** (Atlantic political–military cohesion) — do not collapse into the energy story without evidence.

**Operational / diplomatic lens — summary (Postol-weighted):**  
If **HEU + centrifuge + conversion** latency is **short**, **nuclear first-use** calculus is not purely about **today’s deployed arsenal** but about **weeks-to-response** after a **red-line breach**. Diplomatically, that pushes toward **crisis communication**, **de-escalation**, and **avoiding assumptions** that bombing “ends” the nuclear file.

**Institutional / domestic lens — summary (both):**  
U.S. **public tolerance** for entanglement and **aid** to allies; Israeli **domestic and diaspora** opinion under **prolonged war + economic stress**; Iranian **regime survival** narrative. Institutional actors (military, intel, energy agencies) may **disagree on timelines** — that disagreement is itself a political fact.

## Pape ↔ Postol — convergence and tension

**Convergence (why pair them):**

- Both push against a **“pause bombing → snap back to status quo ante”** story: Pape on **energy and alignment**, Postol on **latency and retaliation physics**.
- Both imply **high tail risk** if decision-makers treat the conflict as **contained** or **one-sided**.

**Productive tensions (do not flatten):**

- **Power vs latency:** Pape emphasizes **market/state leverage** and **coalition fracture**; Postol emphasizes **absolute worst-case** **kinetic–nuclear** tail. **Gulf “irrelevance”** is **not** the same as **nuclear breakout** — different mechanisms, different timelines.
- **Evidence bar:** Pape’s **NATO obituary** and **Iran as peer** framing will draw **pushback**; Postol’s **kg counts, tunnel stockpiles, and “&lt; a week”** need **external verification**. Campaign copy should **not** merge their strongest lines without **separate citations**.
- **Tone:** Postol’s segment includes **explicit mass-civilian scenario construction** (retaliatory framing). **Opposition** use needs **ethics and legal** review; **principal** use needs **values fit** — not raw paste.
