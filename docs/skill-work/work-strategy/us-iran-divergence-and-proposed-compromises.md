## 1. Lebanon and the resistance axis

**Divergence**

The Iranian side maintains that the ceasefire must encompass Lebanon and associated resistance actors as an inseparable component of the agreement. The United States holds that Lebanon falls outside the scope of the ceasefire framework and constitutes a separate matter. Recent kinetic activities in Lebanon have intensified concerns regarding compliance.

**Proposed compromise**

Establish a parallel de-escalation track for Lebanon under separate but coordinated mechanisms, with mutual commitments to proportionate restraint during the Islamabad negotiations. A neutral monitoring group (possibly involving Pakistan and one European observer) could verify compliance and report to both delegations on a weekly basis.

## 2. Uranium enrichment

**Divergence**

The Iranian proposal includes formal acceptance of enrichment activities on its territory together with retention of existing pre-processed nuclear material. The United States maintains its firm position that no enrichment shall be permitted. Negotiations are proceeding on the basis of a condensed working text, distinct from the publicly circulated version.

**Proposed compromise**

Implement a phased, time-bound enrichment limitation agreement under enhanced IAEA monitoring. Iran would cap enrichment at a low percentage (e.g., 5%) for civilian purposes during an initial verification period, with reciprocal sanctions relief tied to verified milestones. Existing stockpiles above the agreed threshold would be placed under international custody for gradual down-blending or removal.

## 3. Strait of Hormuz

**Divergence**

Both sides affirm the principle of safe, monitored, and non-discriminatory commercial passage through the Strait of Hormuz. At present, transiting vessels are required to coordinate with Iranian naval authorities via designated routes. Continued limitations carry direct implications for regional energy security.

**Proposed compromise**

Establish a joint or multilateral maritime coordination mechanism (involving Iran, the United States, and a neutral third party such as Oman or the International Maritime Organization) to ensure predictable, non-discriminatory passage. Weekly throughput targets and independent verification of vessel queues would be published, with mutual commitments to refrain from interference in designated commercial lanes.
