# HN knot: Corridors, exchange, legibility, aftermath of conquest

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-corridor-governance-legibility`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-18`

## Strategic question

How do **corridors** (trade, migration, tribute) create governable flows — and how does the drive for **legibility** reshape conquered spaces in ways that outlast the conqueror?

## Mechanism label

Corridor rents vs legibility violence

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Silk-road-adjacent, steppe corridor, and imperial Chinese exchange regimes — assembled comparatively in `hn-i-v1-18`.

## Mechanism statement

Corridors concentrate wealth and risk; states tax and protect them when they can **see** flows. Legibility projects (censuses, standard measures, forced settlement) raise revenue and control but often trigger backlash; aftermath of conquest frequently **locks in** a new visibility regime.

## Contrast or limit case

High exchange with low legibility — smuggling and elite arbitrage hollow the center’s story.

## Scope / failure condition

When corridors close (war, climate, plague), legibility investments become sunk costs without flows to tax.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-18` — Corridors need **governance that matches how value actually moves**; legibility is a tool, not a virtue by itself.
