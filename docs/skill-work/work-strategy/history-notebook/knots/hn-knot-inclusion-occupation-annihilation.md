# HN knot: Inclusion, occupation, annihilation

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-inclusion-occupation-annihilation`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-07`

## Strategic question

Under what conditions do empires **include** conquered peoples as stakeholders, **occupy** without integration, or move toward **annihilation** — and what strategic price attaches to each?

## Mechanism label

Inclusion ladder vs security panic

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Ancient empires with contrasting frontier policies — comparative treatment in `hn-i-v1-07`.

## Mechanism statement

Inclusion raises revenue and manpower but dilutes elite coherence; occupation without stakes breeds permanent garrison cost; annihilation removes future rivals but poisons legitimacy and neighboring behavior. Choices are often path-dependent on **fear of revolt** and **fiscal need**.

## Contrast or limit case

Cosmetic inclusion (titles without stakes) — breeds cynicism faster than honest occupation.

## Scope / failure condition

When categories blur (who is “inside”), policy oscillates and frontier violence spikes.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-07` — Frontier policy is a **three-way trade** among inclusion cost, occupation drag, and annihilation blowback.
