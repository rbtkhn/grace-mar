# HN knot: Collapse, vacancy, succession

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-collapse-vacancy-succession`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-20`

## Strategic question

When imperial orders **collapse**, what determines whether **vacancy** is filled by orderly succession, prolonged fragmentation, or external takeover?

## Mechanism label

Vacancy competition and succession scarcity

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Ancient endgames where tax, army, and legitimacy fail together — comparative framing in `hn-i-v1-20`.

## Mechanism statement

Collapse is rarely a single battle; it is the simultaneous failure of revenue, coercion, and story. **Vacancy** invites rivals with different time horizons — local strongmen, religious movements, neighboring empires. Succession quality (institutional or dynastic) decides whether the post-collapse map stabilizes.

## Contrast or limit case

“Collapse” without vacancy — old elite rebrands under new flag; continuity disguised as break.

## Scope / failure condition

When multiple claimants have equal legitimacy and similar force, wars of succession extend the vacuum.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-20` — Collapse is **simultaneous failure** of money, swords, and story; what follows is a **succession market**.
