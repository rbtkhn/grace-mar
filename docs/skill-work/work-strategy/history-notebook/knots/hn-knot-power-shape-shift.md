# HN knot: When power changes shape

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-power-shape-shift`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-03`

## Strategic question

How do regimes recognize when the **geometry** of power (who can say no, who funds whom, who fights) has shifted — before crisis forces recognition?

## Mechanism label

Configuration change vs surface continuity

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Transitions where old titles survive but real veto players move — ancient and early-imperial comparanda in chapter.

## Mechanism statement

Power can change shape while vocabulary stays stable: armies, priests, merchants, or regions **swap effective veto** without a formal revolution. Strategic failure often tracks misreading **who actually commands** loyalty and logistics.

## Contrast or limit case

Pure personality politics mistaken for structure — attributing shifts to individuals when the map of intermediaries has already moved.

## Scope / failure condition

When fiscal and coercive flows are opaque, “shape” claims become unfalsifiable — analysis drifts into court gossip.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-03` — Track **veto and logistics**, not only titles, when assessing whether power has changed shape.
