# HN knot: Administration, law, and the long run

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-administration-law-long-run`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-04`

## Strategic question

Why do some imperial administrations outlast charismatic founders — and why do legal and fiscal routines matter more than battle narratives for persistence?

## Mechanism label

Fiscal-legal skeleton vs heroic surplus

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Roman, Persian, Chinese administrative traditions — comparative spine for `hn-i-v1-04`.

## Mechanism statement

Long runs favor **repeatable** extraction and adjudication: cadastres, courts, weights and measures, and succession rules that limit civil war. Charisma fills gaps early; bureaucracy carries weight when charisma fails.

## Contrast or limit case

Over-formalization without local knowledge — law on paper that cannot be enforced without consent or coercion at the edge.

## Scope / failure condition

When revenue and recruitment disconnect from capital’s legal story, administration becomes theater.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-04` — Persistence is often **administrative reproducibility**, not repeated genius.
