# HN knot: Expansion ceilings, glory, and consolidation

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-expansion-ceiling-glory`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-05`

## Strategic question

Where does expansion stop being payoff and become structural drag — and how do empires confuse **glory** with sustainable frontier economics?

## Mechanism label

Glory frontier vs consolidation frontier

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Mediterranean, steppe-adjacent, and continental empires — cases assembled comparatively in `hn-i-v1-05`.

## Mechanism statement

Early expansion often yields tribute and status; beyond a ceiling, new holdings consume garrison, loyalty, and attention faster than they return revenue. **Glory** incentives bias leaders toward overshooting the economic optimum.

## Contrast or limit case

Defensive consolidation mistaken for weakness — stopping where logistics already scream.

## Scope / failure condition

When strategic culture rewards **distance** over **density**, ceilings appear as “mysterious” reversals.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-05` — Expansion has a **logistics-and-loyalty ceiling**; glory moves it later than prudence would.
