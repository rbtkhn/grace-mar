# HN knot: Parity, buffers, exhaustion, third shock

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-parity-buffers-third-shock`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-19`

## Strategic question

How do great powers use **parity** games and **buffer** zones to postpone collapse — and why does a **third shock** often arrive when buffers are gone and attention is spent?

## Mechanism label

Buffer exhaustion and parity traps

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Persian arc emphasis; comparative ancient cases in `hn-i-v1-19` per [VOL-I-PROBLEM-CHAPTERS.md](../research/VOL-I-PROBLEM-CHAPTERS.md) registers.

## Mechanism statement

Buffers buy time by outsourcing friction to intermediaries; parity strategies avoid decisive contests until they cannot. Exhaustion accumulates invisibly — fiscal, demographic, elite cohesion — so the **third shock** hits a system that already spent its slack.

## Contrast or limit case

False buffers (nominal allies with divergent interests) — shock arrives earlier than models predict.

## Scope / failure condition

When every neighbor is simultaneously in exhaustion, buffer diplomacy has no material to work with.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-19` — Buffers and parity are **temporal loans**; they do not remove underlying exhaustion.
