# HN knot: Legitimacy after conquest

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-legitimacy-after-conquest`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-01`

## Strategic question

How do conquerors secure durable rule when prewar legitimacy structures are broken?

## Mechanism label

Legitimacy recycling vs substrate erasure

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Persian, Roman, Chinese, Indian-adjacent cases — evidence developed in chapter prose, not duplicated here.

## Mechanism statement

Conquest clears room for force-first rule, but long runs require recognizable justice, ritual, or law that **subjects can narrate**. Mechanisms include adopting local cults, preserving elite bargains, or imposing uniform law; each path trades speed of control against later revolt risk.

## Contrast or limit case

Pure extraction without symbolic continuity tends to maximize early revenue and later rebellion; hybrid forms often outlast naked garrisons.

## Scope / failure condition

Fails when conquered populations retain no plausible story about **why** the new order is tolerable — legitimacy becomes only fear.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-01` — Legitimacy after conquest turns on whether the new order offers a **narratable** bargain, not only victory.
