# HN knot: Sea roads and circulation empires

**Knot id:** `hn-knot-sea-roads-circulation`  
**Primary chapter targets:** `hn-i-v1-06`

## Strategic question

When does maritime connectivity create **circulation empires** (taxable flow, naval hinge) versus vulnerable supply lines that others can sever?

## Mechanism label

Circulation advantage vs chokepoint exposure

## Source civilizations (comparative)

Ancient Mediterranean and Indian Ocean comparanda — Anglo maritime parallels reserved for cross-volume bridges per [STYLE-GUIDE.md](../STYLE-GUIDE.md).

## Mechanism statement

Sea roads concentrate wealth in ports and fund specialized navies; they also create **dependence** on a few straits and seasonal windows. Empires that master circulation often underweight continental depth until a land rival learns to interdict or outwait.

## Contrast or limit case

Pure continental empires that dismiss sea lanes — then lose fiscal oxygen when circulation shifts.

## Scope / failure condition

When pirates, rivals, or monsoon failure break the schedule, “thalassocracy” becomes a balance-sheet liability.

## Lesson line (History resonance)

`hn-i-v1-06` — Sea power is **circulation economics**; hinges are also **chokepoints**.
