### 1. Immediate and Permanent Cessation of Hostilities + Phased Military Withdrawal

**Consolidated commitment (U.S.-framed)**

All parties immediately cease active hostilities and refrain from the threat or use of force except in strict self-defense. Foreign military forces will be withdrawn in agreed phases to positions beyond 200 km from Iran’s borders.

**Legal anchors and transparency**

UN Charter Articles 2(4), 51. All milestones will be publicly documented and shared with Congress for full oversight and audit.

**Historical precedents — modern and pre-1900**

1987 INF Treaty (Reagan): Verifiable, phased reductions with oversight prevented arms race escalation.

Treaty of Paris (1783): Ended the Revolutionary War with clear territorial boundaries and commitments.

**Historical precedents — ancient and medieval**

Thirty Years’ Peace (445 BCE): Agreement between Athens and Sparta that imposed verifiable limits on military expansion and established a period of non-aggression, aiming for long-term stability in the Greek world.

### 2. Safe and Unimpeded Transit through the Strait of Hormuz + Enhanced Cooperative Regime

**Consolidated commitment (U.S.-framed)**

Immediate reopening of the Strait for all shipping. Iran and Oman will manage it jointly with a 3-year moratorium on transit fees (to protect global energy prices), followed by 50/50 sharing.

**Legal anchors and transparency**

Customary international law (UNCLOS Part III, Articles 38 & 44). Real-time transit data will be publicly reported and shared with Congress.

**Historical precedents — modern and pre-1900**

1987 INF Treaty (Reagan): Demonstrated strength through verifiable commitments while protecting broader interests.

Monroe Doctrine (1823): Clearly declared U.S. opposition to external interference and supported freedom of navigation.

**Historical precedents — ancient and medieval**

Magna Carta (1215): Established limits on arbitrary royal power and protections for commerce and movement, laying early foundations for rule-based order and accountable governance that reduced unchecked conflict.

### 3. Exclusively Peaceful Nuclear Program with Full Verification + Stockpile Removal

**Consolidated commitment (U.S.-framed)**

Iran will legally reconfirm its NPT commitment via parliamentary vote and sell its ~460 kg of 60% enriched uranium to the U.S. under IAEA supervision. The U.S. will provide compensation for damage to Iran’s declared peaceful nuclear sites.

**Legal anchors and transparency**

NPT Articles I–IV and UNSC Resolution 2231. Every transfer and verification step will be fully documented and reported to Congress.

**Historical precedents — modern and pre-1900**

1987 INF Treaty (Reagan): Destroyed entire classes of missiles with on-site verification.

Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo (1848): Ended the Mexican-American War with clear, verifiable territorial and security commitments.

**Historical precedents — ancient and medieval**

Thirty Years’ Peace (445 BCE): Featured mutual verification-like clauses and arms-related limits between rival powers, attempting to create a stable balance through documented agreements.

### 4. Phased Termination of Nuclear-Related Sanctions + Legal Implementation

**Consolidated commitment (U.S.-framed)**

Phased sanctions relief granted only after verified compliance. All sanctions relief must be formally approved by both the UN Security Council and the U.S. Congress. Frozen assets released according to law.

**Legal anchors and transparency**

UNSC Resolution 2231 + explicit Congressional role. All compliance data and relief decisions will be publicly documented and submitted to Congress.

**Historical precedents — modern and pre-1900**

1987 INF Treaty ratification process: Required Senate approval and ongoing oversight.

Jay Treaty (1794): Resolved post-Revolutionary disputes through public debate and congressional scrutiny.

**Historical precedents — ancient and medieval**

Magna Carta (1215): Required the king to abide by written commitments and submit to oversight mechanisms, establishing the principle that even powerful actors must follow agreed rules and face accountability.

### 5. Regional De-escalation, Non-Interference, and Broader Political Settlement

**Consolidated commitment (U.S.-framed)**

All parties commit to non-interference and peaceful dispute resolution. The full agreement will receive UN Security Council endorsement. Any broader regional issues will be recorded transparently with differing views preserved.

**Legal anchors and transparency**

UN Charter Articles 2(4), 2(7), Chapter VI. All interpretations and compliance updates will be publicly documented and shared with Congress.

**Historical precedents — modern and pre-1900**

Camp David Accords (1978): Public framework stabilized a volatile region through clear commitments.

Monroe Doctrine (1823): Established a transparent U.S. policy of regional non-interference while clearly defining American red lines.

**Historical precedents — ancient and medieval**

Peace of Westphalia (1648): Ended decades of devastating religious wars by establishing principles of state sovereignty and non-interference in internal affairs, creating a more stable European order based on mutual recognition.
