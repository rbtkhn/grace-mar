# Promotion Ladder

Status: Active
Scope: WORK-only
Lane: work-strategy
Purpose: Standard path for promoting civilizational, historical, and strategy-relevant material into reusable work-strategy artifacts
Owner: Operator
Last updated: 2026-04-14

## 1. What this file is

This file defines the standard promotion path for material inside work-strategy.

Its purpose is to prevent loose analogy, scattered insight, and one-off cleverness from entering strategy work in inconsistent ways. It gives the operator a repeatable way to move from an event, pattern, or case hit toward cleaner, more reusable strategy objects.

This ladder is not about certainty. It is about structure, reuse, and disciplined judgment.

## 2. What this file is not

This file is not:

- a Record promotion system
- a SELF or EVIDENCE update path
- a claim that higher stages are more true
- a requirement that every input must climb the full ladder
- a replacement for judgment

Promotion means increased local usefulness inside work-strategy, not canonical truth.

## 3. Boundary rules

### 3.1 WORK-only
Every stage here is WORK-only unless separately reviewed through the appropriate process.

### 3.2 No automatic Record effects
Nothing promoted through this ladder updates SELF, EVIDENCE, or identity-facing surfaces.

### 3.3 Additive, not substitutive
A later-stage artifact should not erase uncertainty, mismatch, or falsifier notes from earlier stages.

### 3.4 Compression is allowed
Material may skip upward only when compression preserves the important structure. Not every useful insight needs every intermediate artifact.

### 3.5 Demotion is allowed
If a promoted artifact proves weak, ornamental, or misleading, it may be demoted, compressed, merged, or retired.

## 4. Core rule

A promotion is justified only when the next artifact makes the material more reusable, more testable, or more decision-relevant than it was before.

If the next stage does not improve practical judgment, do not promote.

## 5. Ladder overview

The default ladder is:

1. Case hit
2. Resonance note
3. Analogy audit
4. Watch support
5. Decision point
6. Doctrine note
7. Optional gate candidate

This is the default path, not a mandatory sequence.

## 6. Stage definitions

### Stage 1 — Case hit

A case hit occurs when a live event, transcript, brief, question, or note appears to match a reusable case or mechanism already known to the operator.

Typical inputs:
- current-events analysis
- strategy notebook entry
- transcript review
- history resonance prompt
- direct operator question

Required fields:
- candidate case or mechanism
- why it might fit
- why it might not fit
- initial confidence

Promotion condition:
- the case reference improves framing enough to deserve a local artifact rather than remaining a passing thought

Do not promote if:
- the fit is mostly aesthetic
- the case is being used to decorate rather than clarify
- the operator cannot say what would falsify it

### Stage 2 — Resonance note

A resonance note is a light local note that captures a possible pattern without overstating the strength of the parallel.

Use when:
- something rhymes with a known pattern
- the fit feels real but still weak or partial
- a case does not yet justify stronger use

Required fields:
- signal or event
- candidate mechanism
- strongest fit
- strongest mismatch
- provisional falsifier
- next review trigger

Promotion condition:
- the resonance keeps recurring
- the note materially improves later analysis
- the mechanism becomes clearer through repeated use

Do not promote if:
- the note remains vague
- later use adds no clarity
- recurrence is imagined rather than observed

### Stage 3 — Analogy audit

An analogy audit is the point where a candidate parallel is tested rather than merely noticed.

Use when:
- historical analogy may materially affect judgment
- the operator needs to know whether a comparison is strong, weak, illustrative, or misleading
- a strategy note or brief risks becoming hand-wavy

Required fields:
- proposed parallel
- fit conditions
- mismatch conditions
- falsifier
- use type: illustrative | mechanism | strong parallel
- confidence

Promotion condition:
- the audit survives scrutiny and still adds value
- the mechanism becomes stable enough to support a watch, note, or decision frame

Do not promote if:
- mismatch overwhelms fit
- the analogy is memorable but not useful
- the audit shows the comparison should remain merely illustrative

### Stage 4 — Watch support

Watch support occurs when the audited pattern meaningfully sharpens an active watch.

Use when:
- a watch benefits from historical or civilizational framing
- the case improves signal interpretation
- the case clarifies why a watch matters or what would change its status

Required fields:
- linked watch
- linked case or mechanism
- what this changes in interpretation
- disconfirming condition
- review cadence

Promotion condition:
- the case-mechanism meaningfully changes what the operator monitors, prioritizes, or expects

Do not promote if:
- the watch behaves the same with or without the historical layer
- the pattern adds flavor but no decision value

### Stage 5 — Decision point

A decision point is reached when a civilizational or historical pattern materially shapes option framing, timing, or risk assessment.

Use when:
- the operator must choose among options
- the mechanism affects sequencing, restraint, escalation, or prioritization
- historical framing changes the recommendation or the ranking of risks

Required fields:
- linked case or mechanism
- why it matters for this decision
- which option it strengthens or weakens
- mismatch and falsifier
- why not the next-best option

Promotion condition:
- the material helps generate a reusable strategic judgment beyond the immediate decision

Do not promote if:
- the case only decorates the writeup
- the recommendation would be unchanged without it

### Stage 6 — Doctrine note

A doctrine note captures a reusable strategic lesson that has survived enough local use to deserve standing status inside work-strategy.

Use when:
- the mechanism has recurred across multiple notes or decisions
- the operator keeps restating the same lesson
- a stable principle is emerging from repeated analysis

Required fields:
- doctrine statement
- source cases or case families
- scope conditions
- mismatch conditions
- falsifier
- limits of applicability

Promotion condition:
- the note is reusable across contexts and improves future judgment

Do not promote if:
- the lesson is still too tied to one event
- the note sounds grander than it is
- scope and limits are not yet clear

### Stage 7 — Optional gate candidate

A gate candidate is only relevant if some downstream surface outside ordinary work-strategy needs formal review.

Use when:
- the material may matter beyond local work use
- a doctrine or recurring pattern may need more formal handling
- a separate governance path requires explicit staging

Required fields:
- what is being proposed
- why local work use is no longer sufficient
- what uncertainty remains
- what review is being requested

Promotion condition:
- there is a real reason to stage it beyond work-strategy

Do not promote if:
- the material is still adequately handled as WORK-only
- the push upward is driven by enthusiasm rather than need

## 7. Shortcut rules

Not every input needs every stage.

Allowed shortcuts:

- Case hit → analogy audit
- Case hit → watch support
- Resonance note → decision point
- Analogy audit → doctrine note

A shortcut is allowed only when:
- the missing intermediate steps would add little value
- mismatch and falsifier logic are still preserved
- the promoted artifact remains legible and disciplined

## 8. Demotion and compression rules

Any artifact may be demoted or compressed when:

- it is rarely reused
- its mechanism is better covered elsewhere
- it adds rhetoric more than judgment
- its fit conditions turned out to be weak
- it became redundant with a better local object

Demotion targets:
- doctrine note → decision point note
- decision point support → watch support
- watch support → analogy audit
- analogy audit → resonance note
- resonance note → deletion or merge

Demotion is a quality move, not a failure.

## 9. Minimum reasoning standard at every stage

At every stage above Case hit, the operator should be able to state:

- what mechanism is being claimed
- why this helps judgment
- why it may not fit
- what would falsify the use

If those cannot be stated, stop promotion.

## 11. Example flows

### Flow A — modest pattern
Event → case hit → resonance note → no further promotion

Use when:
- the pattern is interesting
- the fit is partial
- reuse has not yet been earned

### Flow B — analysis-strengthening pattern
Event → case hit → analogy audit → watch support

Use when:
- the parallel improves monitoring
- the decision relevance is indirect
- the watch becomes sharper

### Flow C — decision-shaping pattern
Event → case hit → analogy audit → decision point

Use when:
- the mechanism changes how options are ranked
- timing, risk, or sequencing is affected

### Flow D — repeated reusable lesson
Multiple events → repeated case hits → analogy audits → doctrine note

Use when:
- a stable lesson emerges across different contexts

## 12. Minimal maintenance standard

This ladder is healthy when:

- it reduces loose analogy
- it improves reuse
- it clarifies when a pattern matters
- it preserves mismatch and falsifier logic
- it stays lighter than a full canonical promotion system

This ladder is unhealthy when:

- everything gets promoted
- nothing gets demoted
- stages become ceremonial
- promotion is driven by rhetorical excitement rather than practical value

## 13. Operator prompt

When unsure whether to promote, ask:

Does the next artifact make this pattern more reusable, more testable, or more decision-relevant than it is right now?
