## Preamble — Islamabad Framework (roadmap + rhetoric)

**Roadmap, not treaty.** The **Islamabad Framework** names this artifact: a **roadmap** toward a future binding settlement, not a treaty in force. It is meant to **keep momentum** after a truce, **surface pitfalls** early (hybrid pressure, sequencing traps, legitimacy and forum gaps), and channel energy into **negotiable, verifiable** steps rather than maximalist collapse.

**Public rhetoric blend (Pope Leo XIV — secondary sources, papal X accounts mirror these themes):** Reporting in early April 2026 quoted Leo XIV welcoming a Middle East truce as a **“sign of living hope”** after **“great tension,”** and insisting that **“only through a return to negotiation can the war come to an end”** ([EWTN News, 2026-04-08](https://www.ewtnnews.com/vatican/pope-leo-xiv-welcomes-ceasefire-in-iran-as-sign-of-living-hope)). Vatican News summarized his appeal that **stability and peace are not achieved through mutual threats or the use of weapons**, which **sow destruction, suffering, and death**, **but only through reasonable, sincere, and responsible dialogue**; he also called to **pray for peace, work for peace**, seek **solutions without weapons**, and resist rising **hatred** ([Vatican News, 2026-03-01](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/pope/news/2026-03/pope-leo-xiv-work-for-peace-and-seek-solutions-without-weapons.html)). The same news cycle relayed his judgment that **attacks on civilian infrastructure** are **contrary to international law** and manifest **hatred, division, and destruction**, with an invitation to citizens—including contact with **Congress** and other authorities—to urge leaders to **work for peace** ([EWTN News, 2026-04-08](https://www.ewtnnews.com/vatican/pope-leo-xiv-welcomes-ceasefire-in-iran-as-sign-of-living-hope)). *These quotations are **messaging spill** for operator drafting; they are **not** a claim that the Holy See has reviewed or endorsed this document. For **wide public distribution**, align wording with **Vatican primary text** (audiences, bulletins) and keep this disclaimer visible.*

**Operator weave:** **Living hope** after a pause maps to **Phase A** thin tranches and visible wins; **only negotiation ends the war** maps to sustained **Islamabad-track** dialogue and UNSC-facing closure; **reasonable, sincere, responsible dialogue** maps to transparency, annexed metrics, and non-humiliating sequencing; **without weapons** pairs with **cessation** and with **parallel** easing of pressure channels where the annexes say so; **civilian infrastructure / international law** reinforces **Charter** and **humanitarian** guardrails in Point 1; **Congress / authorities** aligns with **Annex A** reporting and domestic accountability. Use this weave in speeches and cover letters, not as substitute for treaty precision.

## Preamble — plan text (from Islamabad intake)

This 5-point plan merges the core elements of Iran’s revised 10-point plan and the U.S. revised 15-point plan into a single, streamlined framework. It eliminates duplication, resolves the main tensions (e.g., immediate vs. phased measures, rights vs. verification), and frames every commitment strictly as mutual guarantees of compliance with existing international law and treaties — primarily the UN Charter, NPT, UNSC Resolution 2231 (2015) endorsing the JCPOA, and customary rules on straits. No new obligations or concessions are invented; the focus is on verifiable implementation of what the law already requires.

**Synthesis note:** This document **layers** that Islamabad spine with **Annex A** (U.S.-framed operational specifics, optional for talks), **Annex B** (Jiang-inspired optimizations from operator session — often **phase-B**), and **verbatim** stress-test commentary. Read **Annex B** as **implementation architecture**, not as agreed text among parties.

### Point 1 — Immediate and permanent cessation of hostilities

#### Plan commitment

All parties immediately cease active hostilities and refrain from the threat or use of force against any state’s territorial integrity or political independence, except in strict conformity with the right of self-defense. This applies across all fronts, including Iran proper, Lebanon, and regional proxies.

#### Legal basis & reconciliation (plan)

- **UN Charter**, Articles 2(4), 2(3), and 51.  
- **Reconciliation:** Combines Iran’s demand for a permanent end to all wars (including allies) with the U.S. call for an immediate ceasefire. Disputes over scope (e.g., Lebanon) are resolved by Charter rules on self-defense only when an armed attack occurs.

#### Annex A — U.S.-framed operational specifics (optional brackets)

- Add **phased military withdrawal** of foreign forces to positions **beyond 200 km** from Iran’s borders, in agreed phases (from U.S.-framed framework).
- **Transparency:** publicly document milestones and report to **U.S. Congress** for oversight and audit (U.S. domestic anchor).

#### Annex B — Optimized enhancements (Jiang-inspired; phase-B heavy)

- Define **hostilities** to include **non-kinetic** warfare where feasible: cyber, economic pressure, information operations, and **proxy** activities — with a **negotiated definition** (high political load).
- Require **Israel** and **GCC** buy-in to the same definition of “hostilities” for any durable settlement (guarantor politics).

#### Annotated commentary (Jiang — corpus-conditioned)

- **Hybrid war vs. paper ceasefire:** In **Geo-Strategy #13** (*US-Iran War Incoming*) and **#19** (*When Eschatologies Converge*), Jiang frames ongoing pressure as **bombs / propaganda / money** and much of modern war as **unseen** (cyber, economic strangulation, leadership decapitation, legitimacy erosion). A Charter-based ceasefire may **not bind** what he treats as the **actual** campaign if those channels continue.  
- **“Ceasefire as narrative”:** **Geo-Strategy #15** (*The Messianic Calling*) argues that ceasefires can **shape how the war is fought and told** while parties remain **committed to war**. He would ask whether Point 1 is **operational** or **narrative**.  
- **Proxies and Lebanon:** **Geo-Strategy #4** (*Saudi Trump Card*) and **#8** (*The Iran Trap*) stress **proxy structures** and **regional rivalry**; **Interview #8** (Nima) foregrounds **Greater Israel** and regional escalation logic. **Article 51** does not by itself settle **who counts as attacker** or **proxy attribution** — the hard politics remain.  
- **Verdict skew:** **Skeptical** that Point 1 alone **stabilizes** the conflict without explicit **verification of non-kinetic** pressure and **Saudi/Israeli/GCC** buy-in to the same definition of “hostilities.”

### Point 2 — Safe and unimpeded transit through the Strait of Hormuz

#### Plan commitment

Iran and all parties guarantee unimpeded transit passage for international shipping and aircraft through the Strait of Hormuz, without suspension, hampering, or discriminatory measures. Iran may apply standard international maritime regulations for safety and traffic separation.

#### Legal basis & reconciliation (plan)

- **Customary international law** reflected in **UNCLOS Part III** (Articles 38 and 44); consistent with **IMO** practices (accepted by non-parties in practice).  
- **Reconciliation:** Satisfies U.S. demand for immediate reopening while preserving Iran’s operational role and right to reasonable regulation — as customary law already provides.

#### Annex A — U.S.-framed operational specifics (optional brackets)

- **Joint management:** Iran and **Oman** manage the strait jointly.
- **Fees:** **3-year moratorium** on transit fees (global energy price protection), then **50/50** sharing between Iran and Oman.
- **Transparency:** **real-time transit data** publicly reported and shared with **U.S. Congress**.

#### Annex B — Optimized enhancements (Jiang-inspired)

- **Neutral third-party** incident-response and **monitoring** mechanism to reduce cheating risk and sudden closure (trust and violations).

#### Annotated commentary (Jiang — corpus-conditioned)

- **Strongest structural fit:** Jiang repeatedly treats **Hormuz** as a **global chokepoint** and **systemic risk** — e.g. **Game Theory #9** (*The US-Iran War*), **#16** (*Pax Judaica Rising*), **#18** (*Trump World Order*), **Geo-Strategy #13**, **Interviews #5** (Lascaris: Hormuz as economic “nuclear option”), **#7** (Breaking Points), **#8** (Nima), **#11** (Tucker), **#13** (PBD: **Hormuz power-sharing** as a **low-probability best case**).  
- **Enforcement question:** He would still probe **incident response**, **third-party monitoring**, and whether **GCC/Israel** accept Iran’s **safety regulation** without reading it as **discriminatory closure**.  
- **Verdict skew:** **Most aligned** point with his public arc — economically **load-bearing** and cognate to his **“shared Hormuz”** settlement intuition — provided **implementation annexes** address **trust and violations**.

### Point 3 — Exclusively peaceful nuclear program with full verification

#### Plan commitment

Iran reaffirms its inalienable right to peaceful nuclear energy while committing that its program will never seek or develop nuclear weapons. All parties support full IAEA safeguards, monitoring, and verification at declared and undeclared sites, consistent with prior JCPOA-level measures.

#### Legal basis & reconciliation (plan)

- **NPT** Articles I, II, and IV; **IAEA Safeguards Agreement** (INFCIRC/214); **UNSC Resolution 2231** (2015) and its Annex on verification.  
- **Reconciliation:** Iran’s enrichment rights + U.S. insistence on rollback/limits and monitoring are both honored under the NPT’s balance. Stockpiles and facilities return to verified JCPOA-compliant levels through IAEA processes.

#### Annex A — U.S.-framed operational specifics (optional brackets)

- Iran **reconfirms NPT** via **parliamentary vote**.
- **Stockpile:** sell **~460 kg** of **60%** enriched uranium to the **United States** under **IAEA** supervision.
- **Compensation:** U.S. provides compensation for damage to Iran’s **declared peaceful** nuclear sites.
- **Transparency:** every transfer and verification step documented and reported to **Congress**.

#### Annex B — Optimized enhancements (Jiang-inspired)

- **Parallelism:** nuclear verification runs **in parallel** with de-escalation on **all pressure channels** (avoid one-sided sequential concessions) — requires **annexed metrics** in real negotiation.

#### Annotated commentary (Jiang — corpus-conditioned)

- **JCPOA as lived history, not abstract law:** **Geo-Strategy #4** and **#8** foreground **JCPOA exit**, **Jerusalem embassy**, **Abraham Accords**, **Soleimani** — i.e. the **political** break with the JCPOA-era bargain. Jiang would treat Point 3 as **legally familiar** but **credibility-heavy**: why should Tehran accept **JCPOA-shaped limits** without **great-power or succession-stable guarantees**?  
- **War/nuclear coupling:** **Game Theory #11** (*Law of Escalation*) separates **ground invasion**, **nuclear use**, and **later symbolic-religious escalation** — he would want clarity on **sequence**: is verification **parallel to** or **conditional on** cessation of **all** pressure?  
- **Verdict skew:** **Conceptually consistent** with his nuclear-file discussions, but he would classify success as **guarantor politics + timing**, not **drafting elegance**.

### Point 4 — Phased termination of nuclear-related sanctions

#### Plan commitment

The United States and others commit to the phased or conditional termination of nuclear-related primary and secondary sanctions upon verified Iranian compliance with safeguards and non-proliferation obligations. Frozen assets are released in accordance with international legal rulings.

#### Legal basis & reconciliation (plan)

- **UNSC Resolution 2231** (2015), paragraphs on sanctions termination and snap-back mechanisms; **ICJ** principles on asset freezes (as framed in plan).  
- **Reconciliation:** Bridges Iran’s call for full lifting with the U.S. preference for conditional/phased relief — tied to IAEA verification timelines already in Resolution 2231.

#### Annex A — U.S.-framed operational specifics (optional brackets)

- Relief only after **verified compliance**.
- **Dual approval:** **UN Security Council** and **U.S. Congress** must formally approve relief steps.
- **Assets:** frozen assets released **according to U.S. law** where applicable.
- **Transparency:** compliance data and relief decisions **publicly documented** and submitted to **Congress**.

#### Annex B — Optimized enhancements (Jiang-inspired)

- **Snap-back governance:** safeguards against political **snap-backs** — e.g. **automatic review triggers** and **independent economic impact assessments** (institutional design TBD).

#### Annotated commentary (Jiang — corpus-conditioned)

- **Sanctions as war:** **Geo-Strategy #3** (*How Empire Is Destroying America*) and **#13** tie **financial/strangulation** to **imperial strategy**; **Interview #4** (Haiphong) contrasts **dollars** with Iranian **terrain/faith/decentralization**. Jiang would read sanctions relief as **the economic half** of a ceasefire — not a separate “reward.”  
- **U.S. domestic & allied drag:** **Interview #7** and **#11** discuss **petrodollar**, **GCC**, and **regime-change constituencies**. He would doubt **phased lift** holds if **political entrepreneurs** can snap the ladder back.  
- **Verdict skew:** **Directionally right** for his model (relief must be **real** and **sequenced with verification**), but **implementation risk** is **political**, not **legal**.

### Point 5 — Regional de-escalation, non-interference, and international oversight

#### Plan commitment

All parties refrain from unlawful intervention or support for actions inconsistent with the prohibition on force; commit to peaceful dispute resolution; and agree that the comprehensive settlement shall receive binding UN Security Council endorsement for monitoring and legitimacy.

#### Legal basis & reconciliation (plan)

- **UN Charter** Articles 2(4), 2(7), and Chapter VI (pacific settlement); precedent of **UNSC Resolution 2231** (2015).  
- **Reconciliation:** Addresses Iran’s proxy/allies protection and U.S. missile/proxy limits by returning to Charter non-interference rules. UNSC endorsement provides the neutral international mechanism both sides have referenced.

#### Annex A — U.S.-framed operational specifics (optional brackets)

- Broader regional issues **recorded transparently** with **differing views preserved** (dispute log).
- **Transparency:** interpretations and compliance updates publicly documented and shared with **Congress**.

#### Annex B — Optimized enhancements (Jiang-inspired; phase-B heavy)

- **Regional guarantor / consultative mechanism:** key **GCC** states, **Israel**, and **P5 observers** — addresses power dynamics **beyond** Charter text alone.

#### Annotated commentary (Jiang — corpus-conditioned)

- **Hardest fit with his driver map:** **Geo-Strategy #2** (*Christian Zionism*) and **#14** / **#19** / **Interview #8** / **#11** embed **Israeli** and **messianic/eschatological** drivers that **Charter language alone** does not capture; **Geo-Strategy #4** stresses **Saudi–Iran** rivalry as a **motor** of conflict.  
- **UNSC realism:** Jiang’s interviews (notably **PBD #13**) favor a **great-power concert** frame (**US–Russia–China–Iran**) over assuming **SC neutrality** in a **polarized** council — **vetoes** and **bloc politics** matter.  
- **Non-interference vs. “regime change” policy:** **Geo-Strategy #13** and **#19** describe **regime change** as **social breakdown** strategy; Point 5 must **explicitly interdict** the **full hybrid toolkit** or he would predict **backdoor erosion**.  
- **Verdict skew:** **Necessary** as **declaratory** glue, **insufficient** without **regional guarantors** (including **GCC capitals**) and **P5** choreography he discusses elsewhere — closer to his **multilateral settlement** instinct than Points 1 or 5 alone suggest, but **not substitutable** for **power guarantees**.

## Why this plan works as a bridge (plan text) — plus Jiang overlay

### Plan “bridge” claims (abridged)

- Concise; legally cites only pre-existing instruments; balanced incentives; practical for Islamabad talks on implementation details.  
- Could be presented as a neutral **Islamabad Framework** co-sponsored by **Pakistan** and endorsed in principle by **Trump** and **Iranian leadership**.

### Jiang-style overlay (corpus-conditioned)

| Plan claim | Jiang-lens response |
|------------|---------------------|
| **Legally airtight / no new obligations** | He often treats **law** as **lagging** **kinetic and hybrid** reality in this war (**Geo-13**, **Geo-19**). **Airtight on paper ≠ stable in the game.** |
| **Balanced incentives** | **Hormuz + sanctions pathway** matches his **economic spine**; **proxy/eschatology** drivers mean **“balance”** must include **Israel/Saudi** and **domestic US** politics, not only Tehran–DC. |
| **UNSC legitimacy** | He would **stress P5 fractures** and ask **who guarantees** compliance when the SC **cannot** act uniformly — converging with **great-power table** talk (**Interview #13**) more than with **SC as neutral machine**. |
| **Pakistan mediation** | **Plausible channel** (Mercouris transcript elsewhere notes **Islamabad** diplomacy), but Jiang’s **PBD** framing was skeptical of **bilateral US–Iran** deals without **Russia/China** **locking** Iran — **extend analogy** to **mediator + guarantors**. |

### Phase A vs phase B (operator judgment)

- **Phase A (thin tranche):** Islamabad **spine** + minimal operational clarity (e.g. Hormuz reopening, kinetic pause, talks mandate) — higher **near-term negotiability**, lower **stability** against hybrid erosion.  
- **Phase B:** **Annex B** items (non-kinetic definitions, third-party Hormuz monitoring, parallel nuclear/pressure sequencing, snap-back governance, guarantor forum) — higher **structural stability**, heavier **political** cost to agree.
