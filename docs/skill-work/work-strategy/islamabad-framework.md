# The Islamabad Framework

*April 2026 · Draft for discussion*

## Preamble

This document is a **roadmap toward a treaty**, not a treaty. It weaves Iranian and American peace ideas into one path: end hostilities, keep the Strait of Hormuz open for lawful traffic, keep Iran’s nuclear program verifiably peaceful, lift nuclear-related sanctions as compliance is verified, and ground the package in the United Nations Charter, the Non-Proliferation Treaty, and the law of the sea. It is offered in the spirit of dialogue for peace that Pope Leo XIV has urged for the region; the Holy See has not reviewed this text.

**1 — End hostilities.** All parties stop fighting and threats of force except what international law allows for genuine self-defense, across Iran, Lebanon, and related fronts. Foreign forces withdraw in agreed phases and distances so the threat of attack recedes in practice, not only on paper. Attacks on civilians and civilian infrastructure must cease. The aim is a durable halt to war, not a pause for rearmament.

**2 — Keep Hormuz open.** The Strait of Hormuz stays open to shipping and overflight, without discrimination among lawful users. Iran may apply normal safety rules for traffic in its waters. Iran and Oman jointly steward passage and related arrangements, including an initial period without transit fees so energy markets can stabilize, followed by transparent, shared rules for any fees. If the parties agree, neutral monitors can help assure the world that closure cannot return overnight.

**3 — Peaceful nuclear program, verified.** Iran’s nuclear activities remain peaceful only. Inspectors have the access needed to verify that commitment. Sensitive materials and capabilities are reduced or transferred under supervision so a weapons path stays closed; where lawful and negotiated, compensation can address harm to peaceful nuclear assets. Verification should advance together with wider security calm, not as a one-sided opening move.

**4 — Sanctions and assets in step with compliance.** Nuclear-related sanctions ease in phases as inspectors confirm compliance. Frozen assets are released under applicable law. Where legislatures and the United Nations have a role, their processes are respected and reported publicly so citizens can hold governments accountable. Any “snap-back” of sanctions should be narrow and evidence-based so trust is not destroyed by automatic collapse.

**5 — Non-interference, peaceful settlement, United Nations backing.** States renounce unlawful interference in one another’s affairs and settle disputes by peaceful means. Where views differ, the record should be honest. The full package should seek endorsement by the United Nations Security Council for monitoring and legitimacy. Parallel regional consultations—including among Gulf states, Israel, and other concerned powers—may be needed so political realities match the words on paper.
