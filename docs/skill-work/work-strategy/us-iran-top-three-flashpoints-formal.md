## 1. Lebanon and associated regional actors

The parties hold differing understandings regarding the scope of restraint in territories considered linked to the broader conflict. The United States maintains that operations in Lebanon fall outside the framework of the ceasefire agreement. The central institutional issue remains whether effective mechanisms exist to coordinate or sequence kinetic activities in Lebanon during the Islamabad negotiation period. Recent military actions in Lebanon have heightened concerns over compliance and good faith.

## 2. Uranium enrichment activities

The Iranian side has proposed acceptance of enrichment activities together with retention of existing pre-processed nuclear material. The United States maintains its position that no enrichment shall be permitted on Iranian territory. Discussions are proceeding on the basis of a condensed working text distinct from the publicly circulated version. Any viable framework must establish clear provisions for phased verification, reciprocal measures, initial freeze obligations, third-party monitoring arrangements (including an IAEA mechanism), and defined timelines.

## 3. Strait of Hormuz passage

Both sides affirm the principle of monitored, non-discriminatory commercial passage through the Strait of Hormuz. At present, vessels are required to coordinate transit with Iranian naval authorities via designated routes. The practical status of passage is to be assessed through verifiable weekly throughput data and vessel queue metrics drawn from independent maritime reporting sources, rather than unilateral declarations of openness or restriction. Continued limitations in this area directly impact regional energy stability and the durability of the ceasefire arrangement.
