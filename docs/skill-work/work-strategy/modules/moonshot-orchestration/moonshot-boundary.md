# Moonshot Boundary

Status: WORK-only boundary  
Record status: Not Record truth  
Authority: Human operator + normal gate process  

## Purpose

This file defines the operating boundary for the Moonshot Orchestration module.

The module may observe, classify, map, and propose. It may not merge, authorize, execute, or claim capability without evidence.

## Immutable boundaries

1. WORK may not directly alter SELF.
2. WORK may not directly alter SELF-LIBRARY.
3. WORK may not directly alter SKILLS.
4. WORK may not directly alter EVIDENCE.
5. WORK may not bypass the recursion-gate or Approval Inbox.
6. WORK may not create canonical `SKILL-0018`.
7. WORK may not autonomously join, deploy to, or coordinate external infrastructure.
8. WORK may not represent external affiliation or authorization.
9. WORK may not convert speculative moonshot language into capability claims.
10. WORK may not promote inspiration into doctrine without evidence.

## Augmentable practices

The following may evolve through later review:

1. dashboard fields
2. contribution-map structure
3. source-tier examples
4. experiment-framing templates
5. review cadence
6. connections to work-dev, work-strategy, history-notebook, or CIV-MEM-derived materials

Any substantive change should remain WORK-only unless separately staged for gate review.

## External infrastructure rule

External infrastructure includes, but is not limited to:

- hosted agents
- AI runtimes
- memory stores
- robotics systems
- compute clusters
- orbital or satellite infrastructure
- corporate APIs
- public collaboration platforms
- autonomous deployment targets

The Moonshot Orchestration module may analyze or model such infrastructure. It may not authorize participation, deploy code, issue commitments, or represent the operator externally.

## Record promotion rule

Successful moonshot work may produce candidate updates for SELF-LIBRARY, SKILLS, or EVIDENCE, but only through the normal proposal, review, and merge process.

No dashboard row, contribution map, or source note is automatically promotable.

## Hype-control rule

Tier 3 and Tier 4 material may inspire a watch item.

Tier 3 and Tier 4 material may not justify:

1. a capability claim
2. an execution proposal
3. a Record update
4. a public-facing assertion
5. an external commitment

A serious experiment proposal requires at least one Tier 1 source or strong Tier 2 source.

## Human authority rule

The human operator retains authority over:

1. what counts as relevant
2. what becomes actionable
3. what gets staged
4. what gets executed
5. what gets promoted
6. what gets ignored

The module may recommend. It may not decide.

## Gate rule

Any candidate downstream change must answer:

1. What is the claim?
2. What is the evidence?
3. What surface would change?
4. Why is WORK insufficient?
5. What are the risks?
6. What would falsify the claim?
7. What exact human decision is needed?

If those questions cannot be answered, the item remains WORK-only.
