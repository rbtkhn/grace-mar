# Moonshot Source Policy

- **Status:** WORK-only source discipline
- **Record status:** Not Record truth

## Purpose

Moonshot work is vulnerable to hype, speculation, marketing language, and premature capability claims.

This policy classifies sources and claims so that inspiration can be captured without being confused for evidence.

## Source tiers

### Tier 1 — Primary technical / institutional sources

**Examples:**

- technical papers
- official project documentation
- engineering reports
- regulatory filings
- official XPRIZE materials
- project source repositories
- direct institutional announcements
- primary datasets

**Use:** Tier 1 sources may support serious experiment proposals if the claim is directly evidenced and boundary checks pass.

### Tier 2 — Serious analytical synthesis

**Examples:**

- credible scientific analysis
- credible economic analysis
- high-quality technical essays
- serious institutional reports
- well-sourced market or industrial analysis
- expert technical reviews

**Use:** Tier 2 sources may support watch upgrades or experiment proposals when they are specific, sourced, and not merely visionary.

### Tier 3 — Visionary / speculative sources

**Examples:**

- futurist interviews
- abundance framing
- podcasts
- speeches
- strategy conversations
- long-horizon forecasts
- conceptual essays

**Use:** Tier 3 sources may inspire watch items, questions, and contribution maps. They may not independently justify capability claims or execution.

### Tier 4 — Inspiration only

**Examples:**

- viral clips
- hype threads
- marketing claims
- unsourced predictions
- single-source rumors
- promotional decks without evidence
- anonymous claims

**Use:** Tier 4 sources may be logged as inspiration only. They may not justify serious proposals, capability claims, public assertions, or Record updates.

## Claim-strength tiers

### Tier A — Directly evidenced

The claim is supported by strong primary evidence.

### Tier B — Strongly inferred

The claim is not directly proven, but follows from strong evidence and a transparent inference chain.

### Tier C — Plausible but weakly evidenced

The claim may be worth watching, but evidence is incomplete, indirect, or contested.

### Tier D — Speculative / inspirational only

The claim is useful as imagination fuel or a weak signal, but not as evidence.

## Rules

1. Tier 3 and Tier 4 material may inspire a watch item.
2. Tier 3 and Tier 4 material may not justify a capability claim.
3. Tier 3 and Tier 4 material may not justify execution.
4. At least one Tier 1 or strong Tier 2 source is required before any serious experiment proposal.
5. All public-facing claims require stronger evidence than internal watch notes.
6. If source quality is ambiguous, classify downward.
7. If the claim is exciting but weakly sourced, create a hype / contradiction flag.
8. If the claim would alter SELF, SELF-LIBRARY, SKILLS, or EVIDENCE, stage it separately through gate review.
