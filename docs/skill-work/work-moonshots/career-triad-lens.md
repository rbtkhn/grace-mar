# Career triad lens (Founder / Joiner / Investor)

**Status:** WORK-only operator note. **Not** SELF, **not** a canonical **SKILLS** entry, **not** a boundary that forces gate behavior. **Not** CIV-MEM until separately promoted.

## What this is

A **lightweight vocabulary** for stress-testing long-horizon and moonshot-sized choices. It does **not** rank people, does **not** replace PMOS, and does **not** add automation, scores, or mandatory templates.

**Source of the framing (external):** many long-form “future of work / RSI” discussions use a **Founder / Joiner / Investor** triad instead of a classic **ladder** narrative. Cite the episode or article you are actually using; do not treat this file as a transcript.

## The triad (one line each)

- **Founder** — You are creating a new value loop: experiments, product, offers, or narrative you own and can sustain.
- **Joiner** — You are compounding value **inside** an existing, high-velocity org or program (clarity, leverage, and fit matter more than title).
- **Investor** — You are **allocating** attention, capital, or reputation: diligence, time-boxing, saying no, and optionality.

**Hybrid and “n/a” are allowed.** The point is to **name the angle**, not to win a label.

## When to use it

- When filling or reviewing a contribution map from the [map template](../work-strategy/modules/moonshot-orchestration/moonshot-map-template.md) (optional).
- When writing or revising a [moonshot program](moonshot-template.md) or a [mission spec](mission-spec-template.md) and you want a **single** “which mode am I even in?” check.
- When a decision feels like “default employee path” vs “something with leverage”—use the triad as a **lens**, not a verdict.
