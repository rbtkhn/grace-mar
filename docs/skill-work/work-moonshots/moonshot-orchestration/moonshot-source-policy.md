# Moonshot — source and claim policy

**Status:** WORK only. Pairs with [work-strategy/brief-source-registry.md](../../work-strategy/brief-source-registry.md) for **strategy-lane** discipline; this file is **orchestration-specific** and **not** a duplicate of that registry.

## Source tiers (signal quality)

**Tier 1 — Primary technical / institutional sources**

Examples: peer-reviewed or technical **papers**; **official** project or product **documentation**; **engineering** reports; **regulatory** filings; **official** competition materials (e.g. XPRIZE) when primary; **public** **source** repositories (read-only citation).

**Tier 2 — Serious analytical synthesis**

Examples: **credible** scientific or **economic** analysis; **quality** long-form **technical** essays; **serious** **institutional** or **industry** reports; **well-sourced** market or **industrial** analysis.

**Tier 3 — Visionary / speculative sources**

Examples: **futurist** interviews; **abundance** or **narrative** **framing**; **podcasts** and **keynotes**; **strategic** **vision** **documents** without full operational detail.

**Tier 4 — Inspiration only**

Examples: **viral** clips; **hype** **threads**; **marketing** **claims**; **unsourced** or **single-source** **rumors**.

## Claim-strength tiers (what you are willing to assert)

- **Tier A** — **Directly** **evidenced** (traceable to Tier 1 or **strong** Tier 2).  
- **Tier B** — **Strongly** **inferred** from multiple **consistent** **sources** (usually Tier 1–2).  
- **Tier C** — **Plausible** but **weakly** **evidenced** — **internal** and **cautious** use.  
- **Tier D** — **Speculative** / **inspirational** only — **not** a basis for execution claims.

## Rules

- **Tier 3** and **Tier 4** material may **inspire** a **watch** item.  
- **Tier 3** and **Tier 4** material may **not** **justify** a **capability** **claim** (e.g. “we can build X by Y”) without **upgrade** in evidence.  
- **Tier 3** and **Tier 4** material may **not** **justify** **execution** (spend, contracts, public commitments) **on its own**.  
- **At least one** **Tier 1** or **strong** **Tier 2** **anchor** is **required** before a **serious** **experiment** **proposal** (per [lifecycle](moonshot-lifecycle.md) “experiment framing”).  
- All **public-facing** **claims** require **stronger** evidence than **internal** **watch** notes.  
- If **source** **quality** is **ambiguous**, **classify** **downward** (e.g. treat as Tier 3 when unsure between 2 and 3).
