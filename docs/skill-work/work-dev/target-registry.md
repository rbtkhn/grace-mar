# work-dev target registry

Canonical buyer-segment surface for the `work-dev` business layer.

Use this file to decide who the territory is actually for before writing copy or building a pipeline.

---

## Target segments

| Segment | Likely buyer | Pain | Why fit | Lead-source ideas | Status |
|--------|--------------|------|---------|-------------------|--------|
| **AI-native small teams** | Founder; product lead; technical operator | Memory, identity, and workflow behavior are growing ad hoc across tools | They feel the pain early and can move quickly | founder networks, AI builder communities, operator communities | **active_test** |
| **Boutique AI consultancies / implementation shops** | Owner; solutions lead | They can build AI systems but lack a strong portability/governance layer | Good fit for diagnostics, partner work, and downstream implementation | consultant rosters, agency directories, warm intros | **active_test** |
| **Multi-runtime technical teams** | CTO; engineering manager; AI lead | Different runtimes and tools create lock-in, unclear truth sources, and messy handback paths | Strong fit for architecture and portability work | engineering leaders, AI platform communities, runtime-adjacent networks | **researching** |
| **Governance-sensitive operators** | COO; chief of staff; operations lead | They want AI help without surrendering control or auditability | Strong fit where human approval and provenance matter | operator networks, regulated or trust-sensitive service firms | **researching** |

---

## Selection rule

Pick the next segment based on:

1. clearest pain tied to portability, provenance, or human control
2. shortest path to a serious conversation
3. strongest fit with the current offer
