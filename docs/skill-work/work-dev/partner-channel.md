# work-dev partner channel

Partner path for the `work-dev` business layer.

Use this when growth should come through borrowed authority instead of only direct client acquisition.

---

## Good partner types

| Partner type | Why fit | What they get |
|-------------|---------|---------------|
| **Boutique AI consultancies** | Already sell implementation but may lack a strong portability/governance story | A specialist layer for portability, identity, and approval-bound workflows |
| **Implementation agencies** | Have client trust and technical delivery capacity | Extra value for clients without building a whole new architecture practice first |
| **Runtime-adjacent tool vendors** | Need services around their product without owning all service delivery | A design/implementation partner for governed integrations |

---

## Partner pitch shape

Lead with:

- "I can help your clients avoid lock-in and unclear identity/memory architecture"
- "I can do a bounded diagnostic or architecture pass"
- "I am not trying to replace your relationship with the client"

Avoid:

- giant rev-share promises early
- vague "AI expert" positioning
- selling a full company capability that does not exist yet

---

## Guardrail

The partner channel should make the partner look safer and smarter, not create reputational risk for them.
