# Sell Civ-Mem to the Federal Executive Branch

**Purpose:** Outline how to **sell** the civilization-memory / Condition framework (the purpose of history that unifies civilizational perspectives, the seam, one subject many tongues, leaders' wisdom) to the federal executive branch — White House, NSC, policy councils, senior staff. The **product** is the *service* of delivery (briefings, scenario prep, integration); the civ-mem content operates on a different dimension than financial or economic and has no monetary purpose in itself, but its **delivery** and **tailoring** for the executive branch can be contracted. **Human approval required** for any outreach, pricing, or commitment.

**Related:** [argument-purpose-of-history-unifies.md](../../civilization-memory/notes/argument-purpose-of-history-unifies.md), [consulting-charter.md](consulting-charter.md), [offers.md](offers.md), [state-evolved-assistant-brain-heads-of-state.md](state-evolved-assistant-brain-heads-of-state.md) (STATE-evolved product). Compliance: US federal only; no lobbying of appropriations unless separately cleared.

---

## 1. What you're selling

**Not selling:** The civ-mem essays or the "purpose of history" as a proprietary secret. The argument and the framework can remain citable and open.

**Selling:** The **service** of making that dimension available to the federal executive branch:

- **Briefings and scenario prep** — Before high-stakes moments (state dinner, vote on use of force, interfaith or multilateral summit), deliver a short brief that surfaces "the other dimension": the condition, the seam, one subject many tongues, see the face. Not a policy recommendation; an **orienting lens**. "What does the historical/coordination frame say?" so the principal or staff can see the moment through that lens alongside NSC, OMB, and political advice.
- **Purpose-of-history and unified-civilization frame** — A one-time or periodic report (or briefing book) that states the thesis: history has a purpose that unifies civilizational perspectives and identities; that purpose is reassembly via the condition and the seam; the executive can use it as a compass. For transition teams, onboarding, or strategic planning.
- **On-call or subscription** — Monthly or per-event briefs that apply the frame to the calendar: "Next month: X state visit, Y vote, Z speech. Here is the civ-mem lens for each." Low volume, high relevance.
- **Integration support** — Provide the corpus (essays, notes, argument) in a form the executive branch can query or summarize internally (e.g. secure repo, redacted briefs, or API-style access) plus a short method ("when to pull this dimension"). The fee is for curation, access, and method — not for the ideas themselves.

**One-line pitch:** "We provide the executive branch with a dimension of analysis it doesn't have: the purpose of history that unifies civilizational perspectives and identities — the condition, the seam, one subject many tongues — so that leaders can orient high-stakes decisions with that frame alongside policy and politics."

## 2. Value proposition for the buyer

| Buyer need | How civ-mem delivers |
|------------|----------------------|
| **A dimension beyond polling and economics** | Civ-mem operates on a completely different dimension than financial or economic analysis. It answers: What is the purpose of history? How do civilizational perspectives unify without erasing identity? What does "see the face" and "one subject many tongues" mean for this decision? So the principal gets a **wisdom layer** — not instead of NSC/OMB, but in addition. |
| **Preparation for moments that define legacy** | State dinners, votes on war, interfaith gatherings, multilateral summits. Staff already get policy and messaging briefs. They rarely get "what does the historical/coordination frame say?" Civ-mem briefs fill that gap: scenario-based prep (e.g. state dinner with former adversary, Oval Office before a war vote, speech to religious leaders) so the principal can choose to act with the seam visible and one subject many tongues in view. |
| **A unified frame for multitradition engagement** | The executive constantly engages multiple civilizations and traditions. The civ-mem argument: there is one purpose (reassembly, condition, seam) that every tradition can name in its own tongue. So the frame supports **coordination without doctrinal merger** — exactly what the executive needs when dealing with allies, adversaries, and faith communities. |
| **Leaders' wisdom, not more data** | The product is not another dashboard or intel dump. It is **understanding of history** applied to the present: patterns, the seam, the blade, the condition. That supports judgment and orientation, not just information. |

## 3. Who in the federal executive branch

| Target | Why |
|--------|-----|
| **National Security Council (NSC)** | Strategic decisions, state visits, use-of-force context. The "other dimension" (condition, seam, one subject many tongues) can inform how the principal prepares for bilateral/multilateral moments and how war/peace decisions are framed. |
| **Domestic Policy Council (DPC) / Office of Public Engagement** | Interfaith, civil rights, community violence, domestic coordination. Civ-mem frame (one subject many tongues, see the face) applies to domestic moments as well. |
| **Chief of Staff / Director of Presidential Personnel** | Buyer for "leadership development" or "principal preparation" — the purpose-of-history briefing or scenario prep can be positioned as preparation for the role, not just for one meeting. |
| **Speechwriting / Communications** | When the principal speaks to religious leaders, at a memorial, or at a multilateral forum, speechwriters can use the frame (condition, seam, one subject many tongues) to draft with that lens in mind. Sale: briefs or language support for specific speeches. |
| **State Department (Policy Planning, or senior staff)** | Long-term strategic narrative, multilateral engagement, conflict prevention. The "purpose of history that unifies" is a strategic asset for how the US frames its role. |
| **Transition team (incoming administration)** | One-time briefing book: the purpose of history, the condition, the seam, how the executive can use this dimension. Sold as part of transition or onboarding. |

**Entry point:** Often a **staff director, deputy, or senior advisor** who has budget for external briefings or advisory — not the principal directly. Identify 2–3 roles per target office; use referrals (former NSC, DPC, or White House staff in your network) to get an intro.

## 6. Caveats and boundaries

- **Civ-mem has no monetary purpose** — The *content* (essays, argument, purpose of history) is not "sold" as a secret. It can remain open and citable. What is sold is the **service**: delivery, tailoring, scenario application, and method for the federal executive branch. So the "sale" is the labor and the access, not the ideas themselves.
- **No policy authority** — You are not selling "we will tell the president what to do." You are selling "we will add a dimension of analysis so the principal can see the moment through the lens of the condition, the seam, and one subject many tongues." The principal decides.
- **Confidentiality** — If the buyer requires non-disclosure of the engagement, negotiate that separately. Do not disclose who requested briefs or the content of tailored briefs without approval.
- **Conflict with consulting charter** — This is a **new offer surface** (federal executive branch as client). It fits under work-political-consulting (federal US) but is a different buyer (executive branch, not campaign). Update [offers.md](offers.md) if you add this as a formal offer; keep pricing and scope in this doc or a separate SOW template until approved.

## 7. Summary

To sell the civ-mem model to the federal executive branch: (1) **Product** = service of delivery — scenario briefs, purpose-of-history briefing book, subscription briefs, or corpus access + method — not the ideas in secret. (2) **Value** = a dimension of analysis they don't have (purpose of history, condition, seam, one subject many tongues; leaders' wisdom). (3) **Target** = NSC, DPC, chief of staff, speechwriting, State policy planning, or transition team. (4) **Offer** = per-event scenario briefs, one-time briefing book, subscription, pilot (3–5 briefs in 90 days). (5) **Sales path** = capability statement, one-pager, referral-based outreach, sample scenario brief, pilot. (6) **Governance** = advisory only; no policy authority; no lobbying; human approval for all deliverables and commitments. The civ-mem content has no monetary purpose; the **delivery** to the executive branch is what you sell.
