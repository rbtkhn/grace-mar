# Employee Instructions: Simple Condition Public Awareness ($1,000 Budget)

**Purpose:** Step-by-step instructions for an employee to execute the [budget plan](budget-plan-1000.md) and [public awareness campaign design](public-awareness-campaign-design.md). Complete tasks in order; get approval at each gate before spending or publishing.

**Governance:** All spend and all public-facing copy/graphics require **human approval** (operator/principal) before commitment or publication. When the instructions say "Get approval," stop and send the deliverable for sign-off.

---

1. **Read these documents once in full:**

2. **Confirm with the principal:**

   - Who gives approval (name/role) and how (email, Slack, doc comment).

3. **Locate source materials:**  
   - Full letter: `docs/civilization-memory/essays/THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.md`  
   - One-line library (to adapt): `docs/skill-work/work-political-consulting/simple-condition-campaign/one-line-library.md`

## Phase 0: Foundation (Weeks 1–2)

### Step 0.1 — Draft copy for one-pager and letter summary

**Task:** Produce two text-only documents for the designer. No design yet; copy only.

1. **One-pager (public)** — Single page of text (about 300–400 words) that includes:  
   - What the Simple Condition is (one paragraph).  
   - The door is open; we're the ones holding it shut.  
   - Every tradition carries beauty and a blade; the seam must be made visible.  
   - One subject, many tongues — we don't need one creed to coordinate.  
   - No theology required; keep language accessible to secular and religious readers.  
   - Source: pull from THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.md and public-awareness-campaign-design.md §3 (Core messages).

2. **Letter summary (2–3 pages)** — Executive summary for busy religious leaders. Include:  
   - Opening (who the letter is to; why it's written).  
   - The condition (peace as condition, not consequence).  
   - The seam (beauty and blade; two propositions; the ask to make the seam visible).  
   - The children's question ("Does anyone have to die for this to be true?").  
   - The ask: say it plainly, in your own language; one subject, many tongues.  
   - Closing (invitational; no accusation).  
   - Source: condense THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.md; preserve tone and key phrases.

**Deliverable:** Two Word or Google Docs (or markdown files): `one-pager-copy.docx` (or .md), `letter-summary-copy.docx` (or .md).

**Approval gate:** Send both documents to the principal. Do not proceed to design until copy is approved.

---

### Step 0.2 — Create awareness one-line library (no Iran)

**Task:** A short list of 10–15 shareable lines for the *general* awareness campaign (no "demand the vote" or Iran-specific CTAs).

1. Open `one-line-library.md` (Iran campaign).  
2. Create a new document (e.g. `one-line-library-awareness.md` in the same folder).  
3. Copy over only lines that are universal: peace as condition, door is open, beauty and blade, seam, one subject many tongues, children's question. Omit: "demand the vote," "Congress," "see the face" (Iran/conflict-specific), "they're not targets" in a war context.  
4. Suggested lines to include:  
   - The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut.  
   - Peace isn't the reward. Peace is the condition.  
   - Every tradition carries beauty and a blade. Making the seam visible is the solution.  
   - One subject, many tongues.  
   - Does anyone have to die for this to be true? When the answer is no, that's the beauty. When it's yes, that's the blade.  
   - (Add 5–10 more from the existing library that are theology/framework only.)

**Deliverable:** `one-line-library-awareness.md` (or equivalent) in the campaign folder.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves the list. Optional: use this list in the designer brief for quote graphics.

---

### Step 0.3 — Prepare designer brief and hire freelancer (Assets)

**Task:** Write a brief for a freelancer and secure one for the asset budget ($250–350 for one-pager + letter summary + quote graphics).

1. **Designer brief (document):**  
   - Project: Simple Condition public awareness — one 1-page PDF (one-pager), one 2–3 page PDF (letter summary), and 3–5 quote graphics for social and print.  
   - Copy: attach or link the approved one-pager copy and letter-summary copy.  
   - Quote graphics: use these exact lines (or principal-approved subset from Step 0.2):  
     - "The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut."  
     - "Every tradition carries beauty and a blade."  
     - "One subject, many tongues."  
     - (Optional) "Does anyone have to die for this to be true?"  
     - (Optional) "Peace isn't the reward. Peace is the condition."  
   - Style: clean, readable, professional; suitable for both print and social (e.g. 1080×1080 or 1200×628 for social; print-ready PDFs 8.5×11).  
   - Tone: invitational, not partisan or sectarian.  
   - Deliverables: (1) One-pager PDF, (2) Letter summary PDF, (3) 3–5 quote graphic image files (PNG/JPG + source if possible).

2. **Hire:**  
   - Post the job on Fiverr, Upwork, or a design-for-good board. Budget: $150–200 for one-pager + letter summary design; $100–150 for 3–5 quote graphics (or combined $250–350).  
   - Send the brief and approved copy to the chosen freelancer. Set a deadline (e.g. 7–10 days from start).

**Approval gate:** Principal approves the brief and the chosen freelancer (and total spend) before you pay or commit.

---

### Step 0.4 — Receive and approve design deliverables

**Task:** When the freelancer delivers:

1. Check that you received: one-pager PDF, letter summary PDF, 3–5 quote graphics.  
2. Spot-check: no typos, correct quotes, readable on screen and (for PDFs) when printed at 100%.  
3. Save all files in the agreed location (e.g. `simple-condition-campaign/assets/` or shared drive folder) with clear names: `simple-condition-one-pager.pdf`, `simple-condition-letter-summary.pdf`, `quote-door-is-open.png`, etc.  
4. Send all deliverables to the principal for approval.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves final assets. No public use or printing until approved.

### Step 0.5 — Optional: simple video ($50–100)

**Task:** Only if the principal approves using part of the asset budget for video:

- Option A: Use Canva or CapCut to create a 60–90 second video: one quote graphic + text captions + optional royalty-free music. No hire.  
- Option B: Hire a low-cost video editor (Fiverr/Upwork) for a simple edit: stock footage + text overlay + voiceover (operator or volunteer). Cap at $50–100.  
- Script (one idea): "What if peace is the condition, not the consequence? A letter to the world's religious leaders asks them to make the seam visible — the line between beauty and blade. One subject, many tongues." + CTA (e.g. "Read the letter" + link).

**Approval gate:** Principal approves concept and final video before any public post.

---

### Step 0.6 — Build the mail/contact list (Distribution prep)

**Task:** Create the list of 50–75 religious leaders or institutions for the letter mail-out.

1. Research and list:  
   - Denominational offices (e.g. US Catholic Conference, denominational headquarters for Protestant, Jewish, Muslim, Buddhist organizations).  
   - Seminary or religious-studies deans (1–2 per tradition if possible).  
   - Interfaith councils (local or national).  
   - Named clergy or scholars (1–2 per tradition) who are publicly engaged on peace or interfaith issues.  
2. For each entry record: name/title, institution, mailing address (or email if doing email-only). Use a spreadsheet: `simple-condition-mail-list.csv` (columns: Name, Title, Institution, Address, City, State, ZIP, Email, Tradition/Notes).  
3. Prioritize so that the first 50–75 are the most relevant (multifaith, peace/interfaith focus).

**Deliverable:** Spreadsheet with 50–75 rows. No mailing yet.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves the list (and approves whether we are doing physical mail vs. email-only for leaders) before any send.

## Phase 1: Seed (Weeks 3–5)

### Step 1.1 — Print and assemble mail-out (if approved)

**Task:** Only if principal approved physical mail in Step 0.6.

1. **Print:**  
   - Order 100–150 sets: each set = (1) letter summary (2–3 pages), (2) one-pager. Use a print shop or online (e.g. VistaPrint, local FedEx Office). Paper: standard bond or slightly heavier; B&W or limited color to keep cost down. Budget: ~$200–250 for print + envelopes.  
   - If you need to stay under $250, reduce to 75–100 sets.

2. **Assemble:**  
   - Fold and insert letter summary + one-pager into envelopes. Address by hand or label; use first-class postage (estimate ~$1–1.50 per piece for US).  
   - Affix postage and mail in batches (e.g. 25 per day) so you can track.

3. **Record:** Note in the spreadsheet the date sent for each recipient (column: DateSent). Keep a copy of the final one-pager and letter summary in the envelope for your records.

**Approval gate:** Principal has already approved assets and list. Confirm print quantity and postage cost before ordering.

---

### Step 1.2 — Optional: email send to leaders (if not doing mail, or in addition)

**Task:** If the principal prefers email for leaders (or as a second channel):

1. Use Mailchimp (or similar) free tier, or a simple BCC from principal's email.  
2. Draft a short email: subject line e.g. "A letter to the world's religious leaders — the Simple Condition." Body: 2–3 sentences (we're sharing a letter that proposes peace as the condition, not the consequence; it asks religious leaders to make the seam visible; link to full letter and one-pager).  
3. Attach the one-pager and letter summary PDFs, or link to a hosted version.  
4. Send to the approved list (or the subset that has email addresses).  
5. Track: opens/clicks if using Mailchimp; otherwise note send date in spreadsheet.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves email copy and recipient list before send.

---

### Step 1.3 — Order in-person handouts

**Task:** Print 100–200 one-pagers for conferences, church handouts, campus tables.

1. Order from same or another print vendor. Single-sided one-pager; same approved PDF. Budget: $50–75.  
2. Store in a designated box; label "Simple Condition one-pager — for distribution."  
3. Use as needed during Phase 2 (e.g. hand at events, leave at campus ministry offices, send to clergy who request bulk).

**Approval gate:** Principal has approved one-pager. Confirm quantity and cost before ordering.

---

### Step 1.4 — Organic social posts

**Task:** Post the approved quote graphics (and optional video) on the organization's or principal's social channels. No paid boost yet.

1. Schedule or post 3–5 times over 2–3 weeks: use different quote graphics; vary captions (use one-line library awareness).  
2. Include a clear CTA: e.g. "Read the full letter" with link to where the letter is hosted (e.g. website, GitHub Pages, PDF link).  
3. Hashtags (optional): #TheSimpleCondition #OneSubjectManyTongues.  
4. Note which post(s) get the most engagement (saves, shares, comments) for use in Phase 2 paid boost.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves which channels and which posts before publishing.

---

### Step 1.5 — Share pulpit reading and faith-communities kit with clergy

**Task:** Reach out to 10–20 clergy or faith-community contacts (from your network or the mail list).

1. Send a short email or DM: you're sharing a universal pulpit reading and a social kit for faith communities about the Simple Condition (peace as condition, beauty and blade, one subject many tongues). No ask beyond "use if it resonates."  
2. Attach or link: [pulpit-reading-universal.md](pulpit-reading-universal.md), [viral-faith-communities-kit.md](viral-faith-communities-kit.md). If you have PDF versions, send those.  
3. Record in spreadsheet or log: who you contacted, date, any reply (e.g. "will use," "thanks," no response).

**Approval gate:** Principal approves the outreach list and message before sending.

---

## Phase 2: Amplify (Weeks 5–8)

### Step 2.1 — Paid social boost (Meta and/or X)

**Task:** Spend the $150 paid-amplification budget to boost 1–2 posts.

1. **Choose the post to boost:** Use the organic post that performed best in Step 1.4 (or the principal's choice). It must use an approved quote graphic or video and link to the letter/summary.

2. **Meta (Facebook/Instagram):**  
   - In Meta Business Suite or Ads Manager, create a "Boost" for the chosen post.  
   - Budget: $75–100 total. Duration: 7–10 days (e.g. ~$10/day).  
   - Targeting: interests such as religion, peace, interfaith, philosophy, theology. Geography: per principal (e.g. US only, or English-speaking).  
   - CTA button: "Learn more" linking to the letter/summary URL.  
   - Launch only after principal approves targeting and budget.

3. **X (Twitter):**  
   - Option: Promote one tweet with the same quote + link. Budget: $50–75; run 2–3 days.  
   - Or: put full $150 into Meta for one stronger test. Principal decides.

4. **Track:** Note in a short report: platform, post content, spend, duration, and results (impressions, engagements, link clicks, any follows or signups). Share report with principal.

**Approval gate:** Principal approves platform(s), post choice, targeting, and total spend before you run any paid campaign.

## Reserve ($100) — Use only with approval

**When to use:**  
- Translation of one-pager or letter summary into one language (e.g. Spanish, Arabic): commission a qualified translator; ~$50–80 for 2–3 pages. Get principal approval for language and translator before paying.  
- Extra design: e.g. teacher one-pager layout, or a second set of quote graphics.  
- Unexpected print or postage: larger list, reprint.  
- Small fee: e.g. conference submission fee, or small donation to an interfaith org in exchange for sharing the letter (only if principal explicitly approves).

**Process:** Propose the use in writing (email or doc). Principal approves. Then execute and record the spend.
