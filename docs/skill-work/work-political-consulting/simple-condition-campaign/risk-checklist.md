# Risk Checklist — Simple Condition Iran Campaign

Backfire scenarios and mitigations. **Operator sign-off required before launch.** Per governance: human approves all public ship.

---

## Risk 1: "Pro-Iran regime" framing

**Scenario:** Campaign is labelled pro-regime, pro-Iranian government, or sympathetic to Iranian state actors.

**Mitigation:**
- Campaign = anti-**killing**, not pro-any government
- Individuation = Iranian *people* (families, students, medics), not regime
- CTA = Congress vote, ceasefire, no escalation — no endorsement of any state actor
- All copy and assets reviewed for regime-adjacent language before ship

**Check before launch:** [ ] No "support Iran" / "stand with Iran" framing. [ ] All references are to people, not government.

---

## Risk 2: Theological pushback

**Scenario:** Religious audiences reject the Simple Condition as heterodox, blasphemous, or manipulative.

**Mitigation:**
- Offer secular lane (Coordination Hypothesis) alongside religious
- "Regardless of theology, the mechanism holds" — coordination, focal point, individuation
- Letter is invitational ("what if"), not dogmatic
- Avoid claiming authority; "a person who noticed the seam"

**Check before launch:** [ ] Secular kit ready. [ ] Religious copy frames seam as choice, not demand.

---

## Risk 3: Partisan capture

**Scenario:** Campaign becomes "Democrat anti-war" or "Republican isolationist" — loses bipartisan appeal.

**Mitigation:**
- Bipartisan messengers: Massie (R), Khanna (D), Davidson (R)
- Avoid Democrat-only or Republican-only branding
- Lead with "demand the vote," process, Constitution — not party
- Bipartisan kit emphasizes door/peace, not left/right

**Check before launch:** [ ] Messaging tested for neutral tone. [ ] Massie/Davidson visible in assets.

---

## Risk 4: Fatigue / despair

**Scenario:** "Nothing ever changes" — audience disengages or becomes cynical.

**Mitigation:**
- "The door is open" = agency; "we are the ones holding it shut" = responsibility, not hopelessness
- Focal event = concrete moment (vote, anniversary) — something to show up for
- Individuation = "we see them" — collective action, not lone voice
- UGC loop = "add your face," "name one person waiting" — participation, not passive consumption

**Check before launch:** [ ] CTA is actionable. [ ] Tone is invitational, not guilt-heavy.

---

## Risk 5: Co-option by bad actors

**Scenario:** Campaign co-opted by actors with ulterior motives (e.g. pro-regime, anti-US, disinformation).

**Mitigation:**
- Clear CTA: Congress vote, ceasefire, no escalation
- No endorsement of any state actor (US, Iran, Israel, etc.)
- Official campaign channels and sign-on lists curated; reject obvious bad-faith participation
- Governance: human approves all public ship; attribution and channels controlled

**Check before launch:** [ ] CTA is explicit and narrow. [ ] No open-ended "support peace" that could be hijacked.

---

## Risk 6: Legal / compliance (US)

**Scenario:** Campaign triggers FEC, lobbying, or advocacy disclosure requirements.

**Mitigation:**
- Consult counsel before paid promotion or coordinated expenditure
- If partnering with 501(c)(3), ensure no electoral advocacy
- If 501(c)(4) or PAC involved, ensure proper disclosure
- Grassroots "contact your rep" is generally protected; paid ads may have different rules

**Check before launch:** [ ] Counsel consulted if budget > $X or paid promotion. [ ] Disclosure requirements understood.

---

## Risk 7: Backlash to "see the face" content

**Scenario:** Individuation assets (Iranian faces, names, stories) trigger accusations of propaganda, bias, or manipulation.

**Mitigation:**
- Use verified, reputable sources for any names/photos
- Avoid any imagery that could be misread as pro-regime symbolism
- Frame as "humanisation," not "both-sides" — we're anti-killing, not neutral on aggression
- Offer secular + religious framing so no single narrative is mandatory

**Check before launch:** [ ] All see-the-face assets sourced and vetted. [ ] No regime symbols or slogans.
