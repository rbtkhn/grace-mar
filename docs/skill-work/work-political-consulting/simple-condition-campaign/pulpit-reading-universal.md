# Pulpit Reading — Universal (All Traditions)

A short reading any priest, pastor, imam, rabbi, or faith leader can read to their congregation. Generic language; no tradition named. **Human approval required** before distribution. Optional bracketed lines for traditions that use them.

---

## Reading (approx. 90 seconds)

*[You may substitute "the Holy One," "the Divine," "the One we serve," or your tradition's preferred term for "God" where it appears.]*

---

Our traditions teach that every human life is sacred. That the stranger is worthy of care. That peace is not naivety but the condition we are called to meet.

Yet in every tradition there is also a seam: teachings that have been used to justify violence — in defence of faith, in the name of duty, in the name of God. Beauty and a blade, woven together so tightly that to question one can feel like betraying the other.

I am not here to condemn our tradition. I am here to name the seam. To say: we can choose. We can hold the beauty — the prayer, the mercy, the vision of a world made whole — and we can put down the blade. We do not have to abandon our faith to refuse to bless killing.

Right now, people are dying in a war that most of us did not choose. We are told they are enemies, targets, faceless. But they have names. They have families. They have faces. Every tradition we hold says: see the face. Do not reduce a human being to a category.

There is a door. It has been open all along. Peace is not the reward we get after we win. Peace is the condition. We are the ones holding the door shut — or we are the ones who can let it open.

I ask you to do two things. First: see the face. When you hear about this conflict, remember that on every side there are people who did not choose to be targets. Second: if you live in a country whose leaders can vote on war, demand the vote. Let your representatives be on record. Coordination needs a focal point. The vote is ours.

Whatever you call the sacred — whatever name your tradition gives to the one who made us and waits for us — that presence does not need more killing. It needs us to stop. To see the face again. To demand that those who speak in our name do not make war without answering to us.

May we choose the beauty. May we put down the blade. And may we act before one more soul is asked to wait.

Amen. *[Or: So may it be. / Inshallah. / Ken yihi ratzon. / Om. — as appropriate.]*

---

## Notes for clergy

- **Length:** ~280 words; about 90 seconds at a steady pace.
- **Adapt:** Replace "God" with your tradition's term if desired. Change "demand the vote" to "demand accountability" or "demand a vote" in non-US contexts where the mechanism differs.
- **Optional cut:** If your community cannot hear "this war" as a specific conflict, use "in every war" or "in the conflicts of our time."
- **CTA:** Add your own close: e.g. "Contact your representative this week"; "Sign the letter"; "Pray the names."
