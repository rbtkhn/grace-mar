# Audience Kits — Simple Condition Iran Campaign

Four content kits with suggested copy and CTA for each segment. Human approval required before any public ship. Source: Simple Condition Iran campaign plan; [iran-foreign-policy-brief.md](../iran-foreign-policy-brief.md).

---

## Kit 1: Secular / Constitutional

**Audience:** Voters who care about Congress, war powers, and accountability — not necessarily religious.

**Core message:** Congress must vote. Coordination requires a focal moment. Demand the vote.

**Suggested copy**

- **Short:** "Congress must decide war. Your representative needs to be on record. Demand the vote."
- **Medium:** "The Constitution vests the power to declare war in Congress. If American lives are to be risked, that decision must be debated and voted on. Demand the vote on Iran."
- **Long:** "Massie and Khanna forced a vote in March 2026. It failed 212–219. The resolution would have required congressional authorization before more military action. Most Americans in recent polls want the president to get Congress's approval. Demand the vote. Know where your representative stands."

**CTA**

- Contact your representative: [Find your rep] / Call [number]
- Sign the petition: [petition link if applicable]
- Share: #DemandTheVote #EndTheWar

**Format suggestions**

- Short video: 15–30 sec — "Congress must decide. Demand the vote."
- Graphic: One-line + Congress building / vote icon
- Tool: "Find your rep and call" one-tap link

**Political-affiliation note:** Left: emphasize constitutional crisis, executive overreach. Right: emphasize fiscal cost, restraint, Massie-Davidson as GOP dissenters. Independent: emphasize process, accountability, bipartisan ask.

## Kit 2: Religious (Multifaith)

**Audience:** Faith communities — Christian (Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox), Muslim, Jewish, Buddhist, Hindu, Sikh. Denominations and languages vary; offer tradition-specific versions.

**Core message:** Your tradition says every life is sacred. This war violates that. Choose the seam — beauty, not blade.

**Suggested copy**

- **Universal:** "Your faith says every life is sacred. This war says some lives are targets. Choose the seam. Put down the blade."
- **Christian:** "Every life is made in the image of God. Does anyone have to die for that to be true? When the answer is no, that's the beauty. Keep it."
- **Islamic:** "The Quran speaks of a world where justice prevails and oppression ceases. Peace is the condition, not the consequence. Demand the vote."
- **Jewish:** "Beat swords into plowshares. Your tradition already holds the vision. The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut."
- **Multi:** "Every tradition carries beauty and a blade. Make the seam visible. Choose life."

**CTA**

- Sign the clergy letter: [letter sign-on link]
- Share from the pulpit: Sermon talking points (separate doc)
- Share: #ChooseTheSeam #DemandTheVote

**Format suggestions**

- Letter excerpt graphics (1–2 sentences from Simple Condition)
- "Your tradition says X. This war says Y." comparison cards
- Clergy sign-on campaign with public list

**Religious / denomination note:** Offer separate briefs for Sunni, Shi'a, Catholic, Protestant, Orthodox, Reform/Conservative/Orthodox Jewish, etc. Same structure, tradition-specific quotes and framing.

## Kit 3: Individuation (See the Face)

**Audience:** People who respond to stories, faces, names — emotional resonance over process or theology.

**Core message:** Iranian faces. Iranian names. Iranian families. See the face again.

**Suggested copy**

- **Short:** "See the face again. They're not targets. They're people."
- **Medium:** "Every life has a name. Every death has a face. Before you call them enemies, see their children. See their parents."
- **Long:** "The blade cannot survive contact with a specific human face. Individuation reverses dehumanisation. One name. One face. One story. That's how we end this."

**CTA**

- Add your voice: "Name one person waiting" — submit name/story for "we see them" reel
- Share a face: Repost one of the 60-sec story features
- Share: #SeeTheFace #WeSeeThem

**Format suggestions**

- Photo essays: Iranian students, parents, medics — 3–5 images + short captions
- 60-sec video: One person's story — name, face, one sentence
- UGC prompt: "Add your face" or "Name one person waiting" — aggregated reel

**Age note:** Gen Z / young millennials: TikTok/IG, short video, peer-sharing. Gen X / boomers: longer-form, family framing, "what are we leaving our kids?"

## Kit 4: Bipartisan / Exhausted

**Audience:** Voters tired of partisan framing; want peace without picking a side.

**Core message:** The door is open. We keep closing it. Peace as condition — not naivety.

**Suggested copy**

- **Short:** "The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut."
- **Medium:** "Peace isn't naivety. Peace is the condition. The door has been open this entire time. We keep closing it."
- **Long:** "Tired of war? Tired of left vs right? The Simple Condition says: peace is not the reward we get after we win. Peace is the condition. The door is open. We're the ones holding it shut. Demand the vote. End the war."

**CTA**

- Share: One-line meme (door, peace, no partisan label)
- Demand the vote: Same CTA as secular kit — process, not party
- Share: #TheDoorIsOpen #DemandTheVote

**Format suggestions**

- Meme-ready one-liners (no D/R branding)
- Massie (R) + Khanna (D) imagery — "Bipartisan. Demanding the vote."
- Avoid: "anti-war" (can read partisan); prefer "demand the vote," "peace as condition"

**Political / age note:** Cross-cutting — religious conservatives + secular progressives; young activists + older clergy. Same focal point (Congress vote), different entry points.
