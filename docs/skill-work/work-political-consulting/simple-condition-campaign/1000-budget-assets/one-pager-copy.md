## What is the Simple Condition?

The Simple Condition is a letter to the world's religious leaders. It proposes one idea: that peace may be the **condition** for what every tradition says it is waiting for — not the consequence. What if the door has been open all along, and we are the ones holding it shut?

Every major tradition holds a vision of a world in which human beings no longer kill each other. The letter asks: what if that world is not something that arrives after a divine act, but something we must meet before it can arrive? What if we have had the causation reversed?

## Beauty and the blade

The letter names something else. Every tradition carries, in its texts and its history, both a vision of peace and teachings that have been used to justify violence — in defence of faith, in obedience to divine command, in sacred duty. The letter calls this the **seam**: the line between the beauty (the prayers, the compassion, the vision of a world made whole) and the blade (the teaching that under certain conditions, killing is required or honoured).

Making the seam visible is the solution. Not attacking religion. Not imposing one creed. Simply showing where the beauty ends and the blade begins — so that people can choose. You do not have to abandon your faith to put down the blade. You have to see the seam.

## One subject, many tongues

We do not need one language or one theology to coordinate. We need **one subject** — one thing we all point to in our own words. The letter asks religious leaders to say, each in their own language, that killing delays what all of them say they are waiting for. Say it in Hebrew. Say it in Arabic. Say it in the language your people pray in. The subject is the same. When we all say it, we can finally hear each other.

## The children's question

There is one question the letter asks every tradition to teach: *Does anyone have to die for this to be true?* When the answer is no — the psalm, the prayer, the call to mercy — that is the beauty. When the answer is yes — the commanded destruction, the holy war — that is the blade. A generation that can see the seam will not confuse the two again.
