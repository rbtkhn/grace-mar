# Competitor research: Systems for testing leaders' judgment and multi-perspective decision support

**Purpose:** Deep scan of products and research that overlap with the assistant brain / polyphonic cognition offer: systems that surface options (not single recommendations), test or stress-test leader judgment, use multiple perspectives or debate, and/or target executives or government.

**Use:** Positioning, differentiation, and outreach. Keep updated as new competitors or papers appear.

**Related:** [state-evolved-assistant-brain-heads-of-state.md](state-evolved-assistant-brain-heads-of-state.md), [one-pager-assistant-brain-cognitive-polyphony.md](one-pager-assistant-brain-cognitive-polyphony.md), [outreach-draft-post-vivabarneslaw.md](outreach-draft-post-vivabarneslaw.md).

## 1. Direct competitors (multi-perspective / options / debate)

### Crucible (Roundtable Labs)

- **What:** Decision-intelligence platform; up to 12 AI agents debate a question in structured rounds (Research → Claims → Challenges → Convergence). Multiple models (GPT, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, xAI, Qwen).
- **Output:** Decision Brief (one-page recommendation with citations, risks, confidence) + Meeting Minutes (audit trail). **Converges to a recommendation**; disagreement is input, not preserved as final output.
- **Framing:** "Challenges rather than agrees"; founded after a "Yes Man" experience where one LLM validated a plan and another found a critical blocker. Condorcet's Jury Theorem: more agents → better-than-random → convergence.
- **Use cases:** Product strategy, workforce planning, compliance, executive board simulation.
- **Pricing:** Pay-per-session ($5–$15), no subscription; Community Edition for self-hosted.
- **Differentiation vs us:** They **converge** to a recommendation; we **preserve tensions** and leave the decision to the principal. We fix perspectives (A/B/C/D) and never write to record unless explicit; they produce a Decision Brief as the deliverable.

### Suprmind

- **What:** Multi-AI orchestrator; five frontier models (e.g. GPT, Claude, Gemini, Perplexity, Grok) in one conversation. Sequential and other modes.
- **Modes:** Sequential (iterative review), Fusion (simultaneous), Debate, **Red Team** (technical, logical, market, regulatory), Research Symphony, Targeted (@mention specific AIs).
- **Framing:** Disagreements as **signals not noise**; "when Claude contradicts GPT on legal interpretation, that conflict highlights critical analysis points." Converts validated decisions into memos, briefs, executive summaries.
- **Users:** Consultants, research, strategy, investment, legal.
- **Differentiation vs us:** They surface disagreement but still aim at "validated decisions" and deliverables. We don't validate—we present the field so the leader **tests their own judgment**. No fixed perspectives (legitimacy / structure / liability); their Red Team is angle-based but not the same triad. We have explicit "no write unless relay" and sovereign principal.

### Vaisys (Executive AI Partners)

- **What:** Enterprise AI; synthesizes data across finance, ops, sales, HR, IT. Delivers **decision-ready options with pros, cons, constraints**.
- **Output:** Scenario and trade-off analysis; decision-ready briefings with situation summary, options with evidence, pros/cons, and **recommendations** (so they do offer a recommendation layer).
- **Governance:** Audit trails, role-based access, compliance.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Enterprise data integration and internal synthesis; we're orientation layer over a fixed perspective set. They include recommendations; we explicitly don't. Not government/executive-branch focused.

### E-String (Agentic Multi-Stakeholder Analysis)

- **What:** Multiple LLM agents, each primed with a **stakeholder perspective** (e.g. marginalized, regulators, beneficiaries). Multi-round debate; stress-test arguments and proposals.
- **Output:** Thesis teardown, **conflict mapping**, system stability, falsifiers, conditional theses, **unresolvable tradeoffs**. Surfaces competing interests and hidden assumptions.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Closer on "unresolvable tradeoffs" and conflict preservation. Stakeholder-based, not fixed cognitive slots (legitimacy/structure/liability). No "testing leader's judgment" framing; more analytical than principal-facing.

### DeeCee.ai

- **What:** Decision intelligence with frameworks (DACI, SPADE, Helix). Analyzes alternatives and stakeholder perspectives; **options analysis** rather than imposed conclusions.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Framework-driven process; not explicitly "multiple fixed perspectives" or "principal tests judgment." General business decision support.

## 2. Government / policy / civic

### Romania's Ion

- **What:** "World's first" AI government adviser (honorary). Mirror with screen; collects citizen input via ion.gov.ro, social media, caravans; synthesizes into reports for cabinet.
- **Function:** Aggregation of citizen opinion and wishes for government consideration. **Recommends** in the sense of synthesizing and reporting; not multi-perspective option surfacing.
- **Controversy:** Data/GDPR, prioritization of which messages count.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Input aggregation and synthesis for government; we're decision-orientation (options through fixed perspectives, principal decides). No "testing judgment" or polyphonic cognition.

### Meridian (meridian-gov.polsia.app)

- **What:** Autonomous AI advisory for governments. AI readiness assessment, national AI strategy drafting, peer benchmarking, implementation roadmaps, regulation monitoring.
- **Positioning:** Replaces $500K+ consulting; 15 years TBI/Dalberg-style experience. **Strategy and readiness**, not day-to-day decision support.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Strategy and readiness product; we're present-moment orientation and judgment-testing. No multi-perspective fixed slots.

### Deliberation.io (Stanford Digital Economy Lab + MIT Governance Lab)

- **What:** Open-source civic engagement platform. AI-facilitated large-scale deliberation: Socratic dialogue, preference visualization, comment ordering, policy synthesis.
- **Deployment:** Washington, D.C. (July 2025) for AI policy listening; "How much should DC Government be involved in supporting AI innovation?"
- **Framing:** Diverse perspectives, common ground, workable trade-offs; "surfacing diverse perspectives rather than amplifying extremes."
- **Differentiation vs us:** **Public/civic** deliberation and consensus-building; we're **principal-facing** decision orientation (one leader, full field of options). They aggregate many voices; we structure one decision through fixed perspectives.

### PermitAI (PNNL)

- **What:** AI for federal environmental permitting: summarize past reviews, categorize comments, automate routine tasks; expert oversight retained.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Domain-specific (permitting); not general decision-orientation or judgment-testing.

## 3. Executive / strategy tools

### adam.ai

- **What:** Governance and meeting lifecycle; "executive intelligence." Evaluate options, understand consequences, act decisively; decision analysis with implications and trade-offs.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Meeting and governance workflow; we're a dedicated orientation layer with fixed perspectives and no write-by-default. "Evaluate options" is adjacent; we emphasize "test your judgment" and preserved tensions.

### Elate Strategy Advisor (incl. AACOS)

- **What:** For Chiefs of Staff / strategy execution. Plan Analyzer, Reporting Assistant, Risk Navigator. 360° view, automated executive reports, human-in-the-loop.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Strategy execution and reporting; we're pre-call / pre-decision orientation with A/B/C/D perspectives. They optimize execution visibility; we optimize **seeing where your instinct lands** against the full field.

### WorldReplica Navigator

- **What:** AI strategy companion for senior management. Digital twin, 360 risk/opportunity (e.g. PESTEL), scenario impact; 24/7 monitoring.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Risk and opportunity assessment and monitoring; we're structured option slots and judgment-testing, not digital twin or continuous monitoring.

## 4. Research / academic

### PRISM (Perspective Reasoning for Integrated Synthesis and Mediation)

- **What:** Multi-perspective framework for AI alignment. Seven "basis worldviews" (survival, emotion, norms, rational, pluralistic, narrative, nondual). **Pareto-inspired** optimization; reconcile competing values without single metric; mediate conflicts iteratively.
- **Domains:** Public health, workplace automation, education. Bounds "interpretable human vantage points."
- **Differentiation vs us:** Alignment and value reconciliation; we're decision support with fixed slots (legitimacy/structure/liability) and **no synthesis**—tensions preserved. PRISM mediates; we don't.

### Prescriptive AI for auditing human judgment (arXiv 2512.04480)

- **What:** **Audit** human decision-making in high-stakes environments; stress-test. Separates epistemic justification from outcomes; exposes status quo bias, outcome dependency. Not predictive mimicry.
- **Example:** Elite soccer management; performance collapse after salient positive events that experts overlook (outcome bias).
- **Differentiation vs us:** Same philosophy—**audit/test judgment, don't replace**. They're diagnostic (expose bias); we're **orientation** (surface options so the principal can test their read). Complementary; we could cite as research neighbor.

### RedDebate, Sphinx, etc. (red team / devil's advocate)

- **What:** Multi-agent red teaming; challenge beliefs; safer responses; falsification. "AI Sphinx" as devil's advocate for strategy.
- **Differentiation vs us:** Red team stresses **one** kind of perspective (adversarial). We have **three (or four) fixed** perspectives, none dominant; liability is one slot, not the only challenger.

## 5. Positioning summary

| Dimension | Us (assistant brain + polyphonic cognition) | Typical competitor |
|-----------|---------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Output** | Structured options; tensions **preserved** | Recommendation or consensus, or "validated" decision |
| **Leader's role** | **Test** their judgment against the field; decide | Receive recommendation or synthesis |
| **Perspectives** | **Fixed** slots (A/B/C/D); same every time | Variable agents or stakeholders; or single model |
| **Write to record** | **Never** unless explicit relay | Often produces briefs, memos, logs by default |
| **Government** | Federal executive target; pilot path | Often enterprise or civic; some gov (Ion, Meridian, D.C. deliberation) |
| **Framing** | "System for testing leader's own judgment and instincts" | "Better decisions," "stress-test," "multi-AI debate," "decision intelligence" |

**White space:** Products that **converge** (Crucible, Suprmind) or **recommend** (Vaisys, many strategy tools). Research that **mediates** (PRISM) or **audits** (prescriptive AI). Few combine: (1) fixed perspectives, (2) preserved tensions, (3) no write unless relay, (4) explicit "test your judgment" framing, (5) government/executive-branch orientation.

## 6. Brainstorm: Ideas to further differentiate

Below are ideas to deepen differentiation. Pick and combine; not all need to ship at once.

### Product / UX

- **Instinct-as-input:** Before showing A/B/C, ask: "What's your read?" or "Where are you leaning?" Then show the three perspectives and **map the leader's instinct** onto the field — "Your lean sits here (closer to B); A and C would push back by…" Makes "testing your judgment" literal; no competitor does this as a first-class flow.
- **Reversibility as a first-class dimension:** Make "irreversibility" or "what becomes irreversible" an explicit tag or row in the output (Barnes cares; prescriptive-AI research cares). Many tools mention risk but don't name reversibility.
- **Contradiction badge:** When A and C (or any pair) disagree, label it explicitly: "Tension: A and C conflict here. We do not resolve." Reinforces "we never converge" as a visible guarantee.
- **Same three angles, every time:** In UI and copy, always show the same labels (e.g. Legitimacy / Structure / Liability) so the principal can compare across decisions. "You always get the same three; no relabeling, no hidden lens."
- **Bounded session:** Position as **pre-decision only** — "before the call, before the decision." Not 24/7 monitoring or continuous risk dashboards. Ritualized, scoped; reduces creep and clarifies use case.
- **Lexile / plain-language option:** If the principal prefers simple language (e.g. 600L), offer an output tier. "Options in plain language" is a UX differentiator; most executive tools assume high literacy.

### Positioning / language

- **"We never give you one answer":** Make it a tagline or guarantee. Competitors converge or recommend; we don't. Put it on the one-pager and capability statement.
- **"Mirror, not recommender":** Already in the draft post; double down. The system reflects the field; the principal sees themselves (their instinct) in relation to it.
- **Sovereign session:** "Your session. No silent logging. No training on your data. No retention unless you say 'record'." Privacy and sovereignty as a named feature, not fine print.
- **Structure over models:** Don't lead with "powered by GPT/Claude." Lead with "three perspectives, tensions preserved, you decide." The value is the **method**, not the underlying LLM. Contrast with Suprmind ("five frontier models") and Crucible ("12 agents").
- **One principal, one decision:** We're not a team deliberation or civic platform. The unit of use is **one principal** (or principal + aide) in **one decision moment**. Clear buyer and scope.

### Governance / trust

- **No write unless relay (explicit guarantee):** Contractual or stated guarantee: "The system never writes to any record, log, or institutional memory unless you explicitly invoke relay." Differentiates from tools that auto-log or "improve from your usage."
- **Perspective provenance:** Each option is traceable to a named lens (A = Legitimacy, B = Structure, C = Liability). Not "Agent 7 said…" but "Through the liability lens: …" Auditable, comparable across sessions.
- **Methodology certification:** Name the method — e.g. "Polyphonic cognition protocol" or "Sovereign decision protocol" — and offer a short, teachable training so the buyer gets **method + tool**. Staff can run it; the principal owns the judgment call.

### Content / perspectives

- **Civ-mem lens as optional fourth:** The "purpose of history / condition / seam / one subject many tongues" lens is **unique**. No competitor has a named philosophical lens that's optional. For buyers who care about coherence with a worldview, D is the differentiator.
- **Catalyst reframe (C then A′, B′):** When the liability view (C) is salient, offer "A reframed in light of C" and "B reframed in light of C." Shows the system can sharpen other perspectives using the catalyst; no one else does this as a defined behavior.
- **Duty of competence in copy:** Explicitly promise: "Every run includes at least one option at the accommodation/reversal end (e.g. don't escalate, delay, reframe)." So the principal always sees the full range, not a filtered "best" set.

### Evidence / proof

- **Cite prescriptive AI research:** The arXiv paper on auditing human judgment (stress-test, don't replace) is a **research neighbor**. Cite it in capability statement or one-pager: "Aligned with prescriptive AI research: test judgment, don't replace it."
- **Before/after artifact (anonymized):** If you have a safe example: "One run: principal's instinct was X; the field showed A/B/C; principal chose Y and later said the liability view changed their read." Proof of "testing judgment" in practice.
- **Contrast table in sales:** Use the positioning table (§5) in outreach: "Others converge to a recommendation; we preserve tensions. Others write briefs by default; we never write unless you say so."

### Deployment / model

- **On-prem / air-gapped option:** For federal or sensitive contexts, offer deployment where no data leaves the boundary. "Your data never leaves your environment" is a trust and compliance differentiator.
- **No model branding in the product:** The UI and outputs don't mention which LLM produced which line. The user sees "A / B / C" and the content, not "Claude said…" Reduces vendor lock-in and focuses attention on structure.

### Conceptual (civ-mem as moat)

- **Purpose of history as optional depth:** For institutions that want it, the civ-mem corpus (condition, seam, one subject many tongues) is a **content moat**. The system that applies it is the product; the philosophy is documented and teachable. Competitors don't have an equivalent named worldview.
- **One subject, many tongues in the room:** Frame the three perspectives as "one decision, many tongues" — same reference (the decision), multiple grammars (legitimacy, structure, liability). Connects product to civ-mem's conceptual framework without requiring the buyer to adopt the full corpus.
