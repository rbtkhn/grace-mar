# WAP target registry

Canonical target-segment surface for `work-political-consulting`.

Use this file to decide who outreach is for before building lists or writing copy.

Start narrow:

- choose one segment at a time
- note why the segment fits
- record what pain seems most likely
- only then gather actual leads

---

## Target segments

| Segment | Likely buyer | Why fit | Likely pain points | Lead-source ideas | Quality notes | Status |
|--------|--------------|---------|--------------------|-------------------|---------------|--------|
| **Small campaign operators** | Campaign manager; principal; chief of staff | Need useful briefing and message support without adding a large team | Fragmented weekly prep, reactive research, dropped follow-ups, unclear next actions | Candidate sites, local campaign ecosystems, state party ecosystems, campaign vendor adjacency | Strong fit for the diagnostic offer; trust-sensitive and likely bandwidth-constrained | **active_test** |
| **Boutique political consultants** | Consultant owner; account lead | Handle multiple principals and often need more operator visibility or repeatable prep | Too much context switching, inconsistent briefing, uneven content prep across clients | Consultant rosters, political vendor directories, referral chains, issue/political conferences | Attractive because one workflow may generalize across multiple accounts | **researching** |
| **Issue-advocacy or media operators** | Communications lead; research lead; founder | Need structured source triage and message rhythm around one issue area | Ad hoc briefing, no clean queue, narrative drift, weak handoff between research and messaging | Advocacy org ecosystems, issue newsletters, regional media/policy operators | Good fit when the need is message operations, not full campaign strategy | **researching** |

## Partner segments

These are not end buyers. They are trusted intermediaries who may introduce WAP into warmer client relationships.

| Partner type | Who they already serve | Why fit | What they gain by introducing WAP | Lead-source ideas | Quality notes | Status |
|-------------|------------------------|---------|-----------------------------------|-------------------|---------------|--------|
| **Boutique political consultants** | Campaigns and principals | Already deal with clients who have briefing and message workflow pain | Another way to help clients without building a new internal workflow offering from scratch | Consultant rosters, political vendor directories, warm introductions | Highest-fit first partner type because the client problems are already adjacent | **active_test** |
| **Campaign-adjacent communications or media operators** | Campaigns, advocacy groups, or issue-driven teams | Often see message prep, source triage, and follow-up pain up close | Can offer a workflow clarity diagnostic without owning implementation themselves | Agency sites, issue-media ecosystems, referrals | Good when the client pain is around prep and coordination rather than core campaign strategy | **researching** |
| **Issue-advocacy operators with advisory relationships** | Advocacy clients or aligned operators | May have trusted relationships with groups that need cleaner message operations | Can bring in a bounded specialist instead of trying to solve workflow design themselves | Advocacy ecosystems, conferences, referral chains | Useful if the partner has real trust and the client work is campaign-adjacent rather than purely policy research | **researching** |

---

## Segment selection rule

Pick the next segment based on:

1. strongest fit with the current offer
2. easiest path to a trustworthy conversation
3. clearest operational pain, not the largest market size
4. for partner-led outreach, whether the intermediary already has real buyer trust

---

## Guardrail

This file tracks targeting logic, not mass lead lists. Keep it strategic and human-readable whether the path is direct or partner-led.
