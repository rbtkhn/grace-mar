# Principal profile — Ro Khanna

**Role:** U.S. Representative, California’s 17th Congressional District (CA-17).  
**Party:** Democrat (progressive / tech-district populist).  
**In office:** Since January 2017.

| Field | Detail |

## Key issues (documented for briefs)

| Issue | Principal’s documented stance / context |
|-------|----------------------------------------|
| **War powers / Iran** | Vocal critic of launching or continuing the Iran war without constitutional authorization; co-led a bipartisan War Powers push with Rep. Thomas Massie to require Congress to vote. Argues gas prices are up, the regime is still in power, and U.S. actions helped “create a threat” that was not imminent before. |
| **Wealth tax / tech tension** | Supports a California wealth tax (e.g. one-time 5% on billionaires) and federal wealth-tax efforts; this sparked a tech-backed primary challenge from Agarwal and high-profile Silicon Valley investors. Frames it as asking the wealthiest to contribute fairly to workers and public goods. |
| **Epstein transparency** | Worked on an Epstein Files Transparency effort (with Massie and others) to compel release of documents related to the Epstein case; framed as a transparency and anti-impunity issue rather than a partisan weapon. |
| **General** | Progressive Democrat representing a tech-district: talks about industrial policy, worker power, antitrust, and digital rights while keeping a “pro-innovation but not captured by billionaires” identity. |
