# Vance First Hours in Office — Script

**Premise:** President Trump resigns. Vice President Vance succeeds under Article II and the 25th Amendment. This document scripts the first 36–48 hours so the first word of the administration is the Simple Condition (peace) and the second is the ethics pledge for the federal workforce.

**Use:** Operational timeline and talking points for transition staff, advance, and the new President. All referenced documents exist in this repo.

---

## H‑hour: Resignation and succession

- **President Trump** submits a written resignation to the Secretary of State (or other lawful recipient per precedent). Resignation is effective as specified (e.g. noon, or upon delivery).
- **Vice President Vance** is notified. He is now President-elect until he takes the oath.
- **Oath of office.** The new President takes the oath as required by Article II, Section 1 — typically administered by the Chief Justice (or another authorized officer if the Chief Justice is unavailable). Venue: White House, Capitol, or other agreed site. Keep the ceremony brief and dignified; no long remarks before the oath so that the first *public* message is the speech.
- **Immediate logistics.** Press is notified that the new President will address the nation and the world within [X] hours. No policy announcements, no personnel moves, before the speech. The first act is the speech.

---

## Hour 0–2: First act — speech to the nation and the world

- **Single priority:** Televised and streamed address. No other “first act.”
- **Content:** Use [speech-day-one-simple-condition.md](speech-day-one-simple-condition.md). Core message: **I am here to offer peace.** Peace as the *condition*, not the reward. We will meet the condition; we will put down the blade; we will see the face, not the category. One subject, many tongues. Be fruitful and multiply. The door is open.
- **Audience:** American people and the world. No partisan framing; no attack on the previous administration. The speech stands on its own.
- **Delivery:** From the Oval Office or another dignified setting. No audience in frame if possible — the camera on the President and the words. Length: ~5–6 minutes.
- **After the speech:** No Q&A. Release the written statement immediately. Use [statement-after-day-one-speech.md](statement-after-day-one-speech.md): reinforces the speech; announces Day Two ethics pledge; invites Elon Musk to design a shadow federal government (full blueprint, same functions at a fraction of the cost; Congress votes whether to implement; side effect: deficit solution). Let the speech and the statement stand.

---

## Hour 2–24: First day — no competing messages

- **Discipline:** No major policy rollouts, no Cabinet shuffles, no tweets or statements that undercut the speech. The first day is defined by one thing: the offer of peace and the commitment to the condition.
- **Internal:** The new President may meet with senior staff and key Cabinet officials (as available) to align on the sequence: Day One = speech; Day Two = ethics pledge. No leaks about “what’s next” beyond that.
- **Calls (optional):** If the new President chooses to call allies or key partners, the message is consistent: “My first word to the world was peace as the condition. We will hold ourselves to it. I wanted you to hear it from me.” No negotiation, no asks — just the same message.

---

## Day Two: Ethics pledge for the federal workforce

- **Second act:** Issue the ethics pledge for all executive branch employees.
- **Mechanism:** Executive order (or presidential memorandum) requiring all current executive branch employees to sign the pledge within [e.g. 90] days, and all new hires to sign as a condition of employment. The order (or memo) attaches the pledge text.
- **Pledge text:** Use the “Federal Ethics Pledge” in [federal-ethics-pledge-proposal.md](federal-ethics-pledge-proposal.md). It already covers: no use of office for private gain; no misuse of nonpublic information or government property/time; outside activities and conflicts/recusal/gifts; appearance and public trust; face not category; post-employment and lobbying restrictions.
- **Rollout:** The President (or designee) holds a short public event: “Yesterday I said we would meet the condition. Today we are asking every federal employee to put that in writing. This pledge is the same standard — face not category, public interest first — applied to the conduct of the government.” Sign the order or memo. Then the President (and optionally senior staff) sign the pledge on camera. No long speech; the gesture and the document carry the message.
- **OGE and agencies:** Direct the Office of Government Ethics and agency ethics officials to implement: distribution of the pledge, collection of signed copies, and incorporation into onboarding. Reference existing 5 CFR Part 2635; the pledge does not replace it but makes the commitments explicit and personal.
