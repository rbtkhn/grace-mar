# Federal Ethics Pledge Proposal

**Purpose:** Address the most common federal ethics violations since 2000 with new ethics rules and a single, signable pledge for federal employees. Aligned with the Condition framework (face not category, seam visible, condition-first). Suitable for Day Two of an administration that has committed to the Simple Condition on Day One.

**Scope:** Executive branch employees. Legislative and judicial branches have separate ethics regimes.

---

## 1. Research summary: most common federal ethics violations since 2000

**Sources:** U.S. Office of Government Ethics (OGE) annual disciplinary and prosecution surveys; 5 CFR Part 2635 (Standards of Ethical Conduct for Employees of the Executive Branch); recent OIG and OSC reports (2020–2024).

**Findings:** OGE data consistently shows **misuse of position (Subpart G)** as the dominant violation type—e.g., 2018: 623 of 1,077 disciplinary actions (40 agencies); 2023: 270 of 609. This category covers use of public office for private gain (self or others), use of nonpublic information for personal benefit, and unauthorized use of government property or official time. **Outside activities (Subpart H)** are the second most cited (e.g., 85 violations in 2018, 65 in 2023): conflicting outside employment, unreported or unauthorized outside work, and teaching/speaking/writing and fundraising rules. **Conflicting financial interests (Subpart C)** and **impartiality (Subpart E)** account for significant violations: participating in particular matters where the employee has a financial interest or a covered personal or business relationship, and failure to recuse. **Gifts (Subparts A and B)**—acceptance from prohibited sources or gifts that could affect impartiality—and **general provisions** (basic obligation, appearance of impropriety) round out the main categories. Recent high-profile cases (e.g., DOI Deputy Secretary Beaudreau—financial conflicts and failure to divest; U.S. Attorney Rollins—misuse of nonpublic information and Hatch Act violations; Navy Secretary Del Toro—political activity in official capacity; FBI official—steering benefits to spouse) reinforce that misuse of position, nonpublic information, and conflict/recusal failures are where enforcement and prevention should focus. The existing ethics pledge under Executive Order 13770 (2017) emphasized revolving-door and lobbying restrictions (5-year agency lobbying ban, 2-year broader restriction, permanent foreign-agent ban, gift bans from lobbyists, recusal from former employer/client matters) but did **not** explicitly address misuse of position, use of nonpublic information, use of official time or property, or day-to-day impartiality—the areas where most violations actually occur.

## 2. Proposed ethics rules (to address gaps)

The following rules target the most common violation types and are consistent with 5 CFR Part 2635. They are framed for inclusion in a pledge and are aligned with Condition-derived ethics principles (condition-first, seam visibility, face not category, no sacred footnote).

| Violation type | Proposed rule (pledge-ready) | Condition alignment |
|----------------|------------------------------|---------------------|
| **Misuse of position** | I will not use my public office, title, or authority for private gain—my own or others'—or to endorse any product, service, or cause. | Face not category: the office is a trust, not an instrument for private benefit. |
| **Nonpublic information** | I will not use nonpublic information acquired in my official capacity for personal benefit or to advantage any person or entity. | Seam visibility: information is held for the public purpose, not repurposed. |
| **Government property and time** | I will not use government property or official time for unauthorized purposes. | Condition-first: official resources serve the condition of public service. |
| **Outside activities** | I will not engage in outside employment or activities that conflict with my official duties, and I will seek required approvals and disclose as required. | Seam visible: conflict between outside interest and duty must be disclosed and resolved. |
| **Financial conflicts and impartiality** | I will not participate in any particular matter in which I have a financial interest or a covered personal or business relationship, and I will recuse or obtain a waiver as required. | No sacred footnote: exceptions (waivers) must be explicit, narrow, and accountable. |
| **Gifts** | I will not solicit or accept gifts from prohibited sources or gifts that could reasonably be seen as affecting my impartiality. | Same. |
| **Appearance and public trust** | I will avoid conduct that would create a reasonable appearance of violating these standards, and I will act in a way that upholds public trust. | Plain disclosure / seam visible: appearance of impropriety is part of the seam. |

**Rationale (brief):**

- **Misuse of position:** Single largest source of disciplinary actions; pledge makes the prohibition explicit and personal.
- **Nonpublic information:** Recurring in high-impact cases (e.g., leaks, insider advantage); reinforces that information is held for the public.
- **Government property and time:** Directly addresses unauthorized use that shows up in OGE data and reinforces stewardship of public resources.
- **Outside activities:** Second most common violation category; pledge ties to existing approval and disclosure requirements.
- **Financial conflicts and impartiality:** Ensures recusal and waivers are the exception, not the default; aligns with "no sacred footnote."
- **Gifts:** Clear, standard prohibition that supports impartiality and public trust.
- **Appearance and public trust:** Captures the basic obligation (§2635.101) and the "reasonable appearance" standard; supports seam visibility.

**Revolving door:** Post-employment and lobbying restrictions (e.g., 5-year agency lobbying ban, 2-year broader restriction, permanent foreign-agent ban, gift bans from lobbyists) may be retained or strengthened in a separate executive order or policy. The pledge below references "applicable post-employment and lobbying restrictions" so that one document can cover both conduct-in-office and post-service commitments without restating every detail.

## 3. Pledge text (for all federal employees)

The following pledge may be used as the basis for an executive order, agency policy, or onboarding form. It incorporates the proposed rules above and an optional one-sentence reference to the Condition (treating persons as persons). The Condition sentence may be omitted for a purely secular version; the accompanying proposal memo can still note alignment with Condition-derived principles.

---

### Federal Ethics Pledge

I solemnly commit to uphold the Constitution and the laws of the United States and to place the public interest above private gain in the performance of my official duties.

I will treat my public office as a trust. I will not use my position, title, or authority for private gain—my own or others'—or to endorse any product, service, or cause. I will not use nonpublic information acquired in my official capacity for personal benefit or to advantage any person or entity. I will not use government property or official time for unauthorized purposes.

I will not engage in outside employment or activities that conflict with my official duties, and I will seek required approvals and disclose outside activities as required. I will not participate in any particular matter in which I have a financial interest or a covered personal or business relationship, and I will recuse or obtain a waiver as required by law and regulation. I will not solicit or accept gifts from prohibited sources or gifts that could reasonably be seen as affecting my impartiality.

I will avoid conduct that would create a reasonable appearance of violating these standards, and I will act in a way that upholds public trust. In my dealings with the public and with colleagues, I will treat persons as persons—not as categories or instruments—and I will not use my position in a way that replaces the face with the category.

I will comply with all applicable post-employment and lobbying restrictions during and after my federal service.

I understand that violation of these commitments may result in disciplinary action, up to and including removal, and may subject me to civil or criminal penalties where applicable.

**Signature:** _________________________  
**Date:** _________________________
