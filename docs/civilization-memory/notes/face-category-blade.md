# Face and category — the blade's mechanism and its expulsion

**Source:** Session reflection. Connects to [THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION](../essays/THE-SIMPLE-CONDITION.md) and the "see the face" thread.

---

## Face and fingerprint

Face and fingerprint are among the things that make humans *different* from each other — but they are surface-level variations on an **identical structure**. Same person, different face. The variation does not justify treating the other as a different kind of thing.

---

## The blade's mechanism

**What is the manifestation of the foreign thing to expel?**

The moment you **stop seeing a face** and **start seeing a category**.

The blade operates by replacing the specific, irreducible human face with a label: "enemy combatant," "military-age male," "member of that group." The face disappears; the category holds. Killing is then justified *within* the category. The blade is that replacement — face → category.

Certainty that survives only while the face is hidden is not moral clarity. It is the blade. If your certainty dissolves when you see the actual person, what you were feeling was the blade.

---

## The test

**Find the face inside the category.** If your certainty dissolves when you see the actual person, it was the blade.

---

## The expulsion

**See the face again.** Let the category dissolve. Let the specific, irreducible person reappear.

No new rule, no new theology. The expulsion is the reversal of the replacement: category → face. Individuation is the expulsion of the blade.
