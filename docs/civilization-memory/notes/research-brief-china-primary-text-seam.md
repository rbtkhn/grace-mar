# Research brief: China primary-text seam mapping

**Purpose:** Deep-dive the Chinese canon (Analects, Mencius, Xunzi, Han Feizi, Shangjun shu) to **map the seam** — passages and concepts where the same tradition carries both beauty (face, form, restraint) and blade (category, punishment, erasure). Output supports Applied Theology Act II (The Chinese record) and provides evidence for MEM–CHINA entries.

**Related:** [APPLIED-THEOLOGY-MANUSCRIPT](../book/APPLIED-THEOLOGY-MANUSCRIPT.md) (Kongzi, Mencius, Legalism, tributary, Three Kingdoms, Deng); [face-category-blade](face-category-blade.md); [scripture-as-test-concept-and-literature](scripture-as-test-concept-and-literature.md).

---

## Research questions

1. **Analects (論語):** Where do 仁 *ren*, 禮 *li*, 君子 *junzi*, 正名 *zhengming* appear in passages that support (a) extending face and form vs (b) enforcing hierarchy or masking coercion? Produce a **seam map** (theme-by-theme: beauty reading / blade reading / seam).
2. **Mencius (孟子):** Same for 仁政 *renzheng*, 民心 *minxin*, 王道 *wangdao*, 暴君 *baojun*. Where does "people's heart" extend face vs become a category ("enemy of the people")?
3. **Xunzi (荀子):** Ritual and law as repair after disorder; 化 *hua* (transformation) vs 刑 *xing* (punishment). Where does Xunzi sharpen the seam (ritual as form that limits blade) vs lean toward the blade?
4. **Han Feizi / Shangjun shu:** Exact passages on 法 *fa*, 勢 *shi*, 術 *shu*; where "when ritual fails" turns into "erase the other"; how to teach the **two registers** (Confucian vs Legalist) without flattening the texts.

---

## Key sources

**Primary (translations + Chinese where possible):**
- *Analects* — Legge, Slingerland, or Lau; consider 論語 *Lunyu* concordance or digitized text for keyword search.
- *Mencius* — Van Norden, Lau, or Legge.
- *Xunzi* — Knoblock & Riegel, Hutton; chapters on 禮 *li*, 性惡 "nature is bad," 王制 "king's model."
- *Han Feizi* — Watson, Liao; chapters on 五蠹 "five vermin," 顯學 "prominent doctrines," 定法 "establishing standards."
- *Shangjun shu* (Book of Lord Shang) — Duyvendak, Pines; chapters on 農戰 "agriculture and war," 賞刑 "rewards and punishments."

**Secondary (seam / two registers / Confucian–Legalist):**
- Creel, *The Origins of Statecraft in China* (Legalist vs Confucian).
- Pines, *The Book of Lord Shang* (intro and notes on ideology).
- Schwartz, *The World of Thought in Ancient China* (chapters on Confucius, Mencius, Xunzi, Legalists).
- Slingerland, *Effortless Action* (Analects and *wu wei*; ritual as form).
- Recent articles on "Confucian constitutionalism" or "ritual and law" that distinguish beauty vs blade readings.

## Method / outline

1. **Keyword pass:** For each text, list every occurrence of the core terms (e.g. 仁, 禮, 君子 in Analects; 仁政, 民心 in Mencius; 法, 勢, 術 in Han Feizi). Tag each with: (a) beauty (extends face/form/restraint), (b) blade (category/punishment/erasure), (c) ambiguous (seam).
2. **Thematic seam summary:** For each text, write 1–2 pages: (i) beauty reading (what the tradition names that supports the condition); (ii) blade reading (same vocabulary used to justify harm or category); (iii) seam (where one passage or concept can be read both ways).
3. **Two-register comparison:** One short section: Kongzi + Mencius (+ Xunzi) as "ritual/virtue register" vs Han Feizi + Shangjun shu as "punishment/statecraft register." When does the text itself say "when ritual fails, use law"? Document exact citations.
4. **Applied Theology hook:** For each text, draft 2–4 sentences in the book's voice: "MEM–CHINA–LIT–[TEXT] encodes … The beauty reading … The blade … Make the seam visible."
