## 1. Theological resonances (support for the condition)

| **Sunni Islam** | *Maqasid* (preservation of life), priority of mercy and opening of Quran support condition; greater jihad (self) vs lesser — condition fits when "what must hold for the world we claim to want." |

## 2. Scripture as test — existing precedents

- **Jewish:** Akedah as test; "pass" = angel staying Abraham's hand (refusal of the sacrifice). So "win by refusing the violent script" is not new.
- **Hindu:** Gita as inner battle; Gandhi's *Anasaktiyoga* = refuse outer war, fight inner; scripture as test already alive.
- **Buddhist:** Precepts are clear; the "test" is whether we follow the first precept.
- **Catholic:** Development of doctrine, priority of Gospel of life; conquest narratives and imprecatory psalms already read as test by many exegetes — book makes the move explicit and cross-tradition.
- **Islam:** Abrogation, context, priority of mercy; "this passage is a test; we refuse to perform it" = strong but teachable hermeneutic.
- **Orthodox:** Allegorical and moral senses; not bound to literal enactment of every narrative.
- **Shi'a:** Long tradition of interpretation that limits and contextualises violent passages.
- **Africa / Bishop:** After colonialism and war, reading texts critically; book gives shared language: "we are not betraying the text when we refuse the blade; we are passing the test."

**Use:** Add a sentence or footnote: "The move to read violent or commanding passages as test has precedent within Judaism (Akedah), Hinduism (Gita as inner struggle), Islam (abrogation and mercy), and Christian exegesis (development of doctrine, moral sense). The book extends that move as a shared hermeneutic across traditions."

## 3. Heaven as what we build — qualifications

- **Catholic:** "Heaven as what we build" can sound Pelagian; qualify: we build the *conditions* (peace, face recognised) that our tradition says heaven fulfils; grace works through our freedom.
- **Orthodox:** Qualify by priority of divine action; we build in liturgy, charity, peace — conduct matters but condition is met *in* the Church, *through* the mysteries.
- **Jewish:** "We build heaven" must be "we build the conditions; God brings the fullness" — not achievement by our efforts alone.
- **Buddhist:** We do not "await" a single divine return; we "walk the path" and build cessation of suffering — formulation is partly theistic; small correction, not rejection.
- **Islam:** Building what we await fits responsibility in history and accountability; Babel as one subject many tongues fits Quran on diversity of peoples and languages.
- **Africa / East Asia:** Building the Kingdom here is already familiar; "we are co-workers."

**Use:** In the "heaven as what we build" sentence(s), add a qualifier: e.g. "We build the conditions that our traditions say heaven fulfils" or "we build what we await in the sense that our conduct creates the conditions — the full coming remains in God's hands / in the path."

## 4. Objections and limits (what the book should acknowledge or qualify)

| Source | Objection / limit |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Cardinal** | Condition not independent of revelation; we meet it in response to grace and in the light of the Gospel. Do not flatten doctrinal difference: "reference is the same but full truth is not identical." |
| **Imam** | Classical fiqh has a place for justified force (defence, resistance). Do not repudiate in toto; default = beauty, seam visible; "we coordinate on the condition; we do not coordinate on every ruling." |
| **Imam** | **Content gap:** Say more about the victims of violence — the dead, the displaced. Their faces are the reason we must stop; they are not only "category." |
| **Rabbi** | Do not imply we achieve the messianic age by our efforts alone. Religious Zionism / prophecy-fulfillment as blade is a painful internal conversation. |
| **Metropolitan** | Condition cannot be fully met outside the Church; we meet it *in* the Church, *through* the mysteries. One subject many tongues when we speak to the world; inside we have the fullness. |
| **Dr. Farsi** | Tradition of justified resistance; do not renounce in the abstract; default = face, seam visible, so "resistance" does not erase the other. |
| **Dr. Farsi** | **Framing:** The book can sound as if traditions have not been doing this work. Many have been making the seam visible from within. The book is a mirror and a shared frame, not a discovery no one had made. |

**Use:** (1) Add one sentence on justified force: e.g. "The condition is a moral and spiritual priority and a shared reference for coordination; it does not require every tradition to renounce in advance every doctrine of justified defence or resistance, but it does require that the default be the face and that the seam remain visible." (2) Add a short paragraph or footnote on victims: the dead and displaced, their faces as the reason we must stop. (3) Add one sentence acknowledging that many traditions and scholars have long been making the seam visible; the book offers a shared frame to say it across traditions.

## 5. Content gaps (what to add)

- **Structural and economic violence (Bishop, Granthi):** The blade is not only war. It is the structure that erases the face of the poor, the worker, the migrant, the oppressed. Making the seam visible must include economic and structural violence; "category" applies there too.
- **Victims (Imam):** The dead and displaced — their faces, their memory — as the reason we must stop; they are not only "category" but the ones whose absence demands the condition.
- **Who is addressed (Rev. Chen):** "Religious leaders" / "theological leaders" can sound institutional and male. Include lay theologians, women, and traditions without a single hierarchy; the condition and the seam are for everyone to see and to teach.

**Use:** (1) In Illustrations or in a short "Scope" note: extend "blade" / "category" to structural and economic violence (one paragraph or a few sentences). (2) In the Letter or Preface: one sentence on the dead and displaced — we stop for their sake; their faces are the reason. (3) In the Letter’s salutation or in a footnote: "theological leaders" includes all who teach and guard the sacred, including lay theologians and traditions without a single hierarchy.

## 6. Framing suggestions

- **Book as mirror, not discovery (Dr. Farsi):** Acknowledge that the book reflects and names what many traditions and scholars already do; it offers a shared frame so we can say to each other, across traditions, what we are already saying at home.
- **One subject many tongues ≠ syncretism (Rev. Chen):** Explicitly state: one subject many tongues is not syncretism; it is the possibility of a shared moral reference. (The book may already imply this; making it explicit helps.)
- **Buddhist "awaiting" (Venerable Thich Minh):** The formulation "what the traditions await" is partly theistic. Buddhists "walk the path" and build cessation of suffering rather than "await" a single return. A small in-text qualifier ("what the traditions await or seek") could include that.

## 7. Suggested one-paragraph addition (Preface or "Responses")

*Suggested text the book could add (e.g. after the Preface or as a short "Responses and limits" note):*

> Readers and responders have noted that the condition does not require any tradition to renounce every doctrine of justified defence or resistance; it asks that the default be the face and that the seam remain visible, and that we coordinate on the condition without claiming to coordinate on every ruling. They have also noted that the "blade" and "category" apply beyond armed violence — to structural and economic violence that erase the face of the poor, the worker, and the displaced — and that the dead and displaced are not only "category" but the reason we must stop: their faces demand the condition. Many traditions and scholars have long been making the seam visible from within; this book offers a shared frame so that we can say to each other, across traditions, what we are already saying at home. The formulation "heaven as what we build" is here understood as building the *conditions* that our traditions say heaven fulfils; the full coming remains in God's hands or in the path. The condition and the seam are for everyone to see and to teach — those who hold institutional authority and those who do not.
