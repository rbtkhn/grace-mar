## 2. Result: No Explicit Contradiction

**Finding:** No MEM–PERSIA file or CIV–CORE–PERSIA states that peace is impossible, that the condition cannot be met, or that killing is required regardless of circumstance.

- Grep and read pass for phrases such as "peace is impossible," "condition cannot be met," "restraint fails," "must fight," "no peace," "anarchy overrides" across the PERSIA folder found **no hits**.
- The corpus is descriptive and analytical (legitimacy, endurance, sovereignty, siege, conquest, parity). It encodes **why** conflict and siege persist and **how** Persian memory interprets them; it does not assert that coordination or the condition is impossible.

## 3. Structural Tension (Polyphonic — Acknowledged in Book)

**CIV–CORE–PERSIA** encodes axioms that describe a world in which restraint is structurally secondary and siege is the default frame:

- **Axiom 1:** Sovereignty as sacred; autonomy absolute; "Dependency equals suffocation."
- **Axiom 4:** Structural suspicion; "Trust is never default; deterrence precedes diplomacy."
- **Axiom 7:** Shi'a memory engine: "Martyrdom → endurance → legitimacy → sovereignty recursion."
- **Axiom 8:** "Survival as primary legitimacy metric."
- **Continuity engine:** Colonial Trauma → 1953 Coup → Iran–Iraq War → Sanctions Era; "Each era increases siege literacy and distrust of external guarantees."
- **Exit Primacy Law (EPL-1):** "Exit dominates reform once survival outside incumbent rails is proven."

**Assessment:** This is the same kind of **advisory realist / structural voice** the book already acknowledges in "Civ-mem and the peace thesis": it explains conflict in terms of structure and incentives ("the logic of anarchy overrode the logic of restraint") and offers a competing explanation for why states and regimes fight or harden. The book's position holds: the corpus design treats this as **polyphonic tension**; that voice sharpens and does not replace; resolution is the user's task. MEM-Persia does not contradict the belief that applied theology is a solution for world peace; it includes a perspective that emphasises structural obstacles, which the book can acknowledge as what has often overridden restraint in history, without conceding that it must always do so.

## 7. Verdict

**Applied theology is not contradicted by the MEM-Persia corpus.**

- The corpus supports the thesis where it encodes face, recognition, continuity through compression (reassembly), parity without mutual destruction, law as form, and one subject many tongues.
- It makes the seam visible where it encodes conquest, humiliation, siege, martyrdom-as-category, and structural suspicion.

**Recommendation:** No change to the applied-theology thesis or to the "Civ-mem and the peace thesis" paragraph is required. The MEM-Persia test reinforces that the civilizational-memory record can be read consistently with the condition, the seam, and the method (make the seam visible; choose the beauty).
