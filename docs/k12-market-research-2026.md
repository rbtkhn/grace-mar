# K-12 market, competitor, and pedagogy research — Colorado companion-self niche

**Purpose:** Inform **Companion-self for Schools / Colorado-first** offer ([k12-schools-colorado.md](k12-schools-colorado.md), [k12-schools-pilot-playbook.md](k12-schools-pilot-playbook.md)). Not legal advice. **Last updated:** March 2026.

---

## 1. Market research (portable learner identity)

| Signal | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| **K-12 interoperability** (Project Unicorn, Ed-Fi, LEA exchange) | Districts optimize SIS/LMS pipes, not child-owned **narrative identity**. Gap: long-horizon, family/school-approved profile outside a single LMS. |
| **CLR 2.0 / Open Badges 3.0 (1EdTech, 2025)** | Sector moving toward **verifiable portable credentials**. Record + evidence can **align later** with CLR-style exports without becoming a full credential platform on day one. |
| **Learner Information Framework** | Cross-system learner data location — this product is the **human-gated story + curiosity + personality** layer many stacks omit. |
| **AI schools + adaptive apps** (Alpha, Khan, IXL) | Teaching layer crowded; **identity + consent + export** comparatively weak ([market-research-alpha-khan.md](market-research-alpha-khan.md)). **Supplemental** positioning: they teach; we **record**. |

**Colorado procurement:** Charters often use **BuyQ** (GPO), **CSP-style** budgets; microschools emphasize **competency + individual learner profiles** — fit for Record + evidence story. Large district RFPs = later wave.

## 2. Competitor research (direct and adjacent)

| Player | Positioning | vs. companion-self offer |
|--------|-------------|---------------------------|
| **Seesaw** | Elementary LMS + digital portfolio + family; teacher-captured evidence | Institution-centric; not **sovereign merge** on identity claims; not **Voice from approved Record only**. |
| **FreshGrade** | PreK–12 portfolio, standards, parent engagement | Same class: school-owned portfolio, not **auditable fork + merge ritual**. |
| **SIS / LMS (e.g. PowerSchool)** | Grades, attendance, compliance | System of record for **transactions**, not developmental **IX-style** mind + narrative. |
| **TwinMind / second brain** ([competitive-analysis.md](competitive-analysis.md)) | Adult productivity memory | Not child-safe K-12 positioning; no gate story. |
| **Tutor AIs (Khanmigo-class)** | Teaching | No **durable parent-approved Record** as core product. |
| **Ed-Fi / CLR tooling** | Standards, exchange, credentials | Interoperability — not **Lexile-bounded Voice + tricameral** product. |

**White space:** Human-approved **identity Record** + **queryable Voice (knowledge boundary)** + **export for handoffs** — **supplemental** to Seesaw/LMS.

## 3. Pedagogical and related research

| Theme | Direction | Product tie-in |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| **Student agency in assessment** | Student-led conferences, portfolios (ASCD line); self-assessment design | **RECURSION-GATE** = visible consent to what counts as “known” — aligns with **family/student voice** in what enters the Record. |
| **ePortfolios as process** | Reflection, goal-setting, self-evaluation when scaffolded | **EVIDENCE + artifacts** = portfolio process; **SELF IX** = stable substrate for Voice. |
| **Developmentally appropriate AI** | Boundaries, abstention | **Knowledge boundary + Lexile** = responsible Voice vs. open tutor. |
| **Microschool / competency** | Individual learner profiles, mixed age | One Record per child + exports supports handoffs without claiming SpEd compliance. |

**Risk:** Portfolios without scaffolding → busywork. Features should **reduce** parent/educator burden (staging, templates, “we did X”).
