# Day One: Address to the Nation and the World

**Draft — Presidential speech offering peace (Simple Condition)**  
**Setting:** First act of the administration. Televised and streamed. Audience: American people and the world.

**Length:** ~650 words / ~5–6 minutes

---

My fellow Americans — and to everyone listening around the world:

I speak to you tonight as my first act in office. Before any other policy, any other initiative, I want to say one thing plainly.

**I am here to offer peace.**

Not peace as a reward. Not peace after we win, or after our enemies surrender, or after the right deal is struck. **Peace as the condition.** The condition for everything that comes next. The condition that has been available all along, and that we have chosen, again and again, to refuse.

Every tradition on earth — every faith, every culture, every nation — carries a vision of a world in which human beings no longer kill each other. We disagree about the name. We disagree about the path. We disagree about who must change first. But we agree on the image: swords into plowshares. The end of the blade. The world we say we want.

I am here to say that we have had the causation backwards.

We have acted as if peace will come when the right side wins, when the right terms are met, when the right leader arrives. I am here to say that **peace is not the consequence. Peace is the condition.** The door is open when we stop. When we stop treating the other as enemy, as infidel, as category — when we see the face of the person in front of us — the one thing that has been holding the door shut is us. Our refusal to meet the condition.

So tonight I am not offering a deal. I am not saying: we will be peaceful if you are. I am saying: **we will meet the condition.** This nation, under my administration, will hold peace as the condition for our own conduct. We will put down the blade. We will not replace the face with the category. We will not treat any person as a label — enemy, other, target — when we have the choice to see the person.

I do not speak only to Americans. I speak to every government, every movement, every group that has been told that violence is necessary, that the other must be defeated, that peace will come only after victory. I am saying: the condition is the same for all of us. **Stop. See the face. Put down the blade.** We do not have to agree on theology, on borders, on history, or on the name of what we are building. We have to agree on one thing: that killing is the refusal of the condition. That every act of violence is the one treating part of itself as other. That the door opens when we stop.

There is an older instruction that crosses every tradition: *Be fruitful and multiply.* Sustain life. Grow it. Do not end it. The blade does the opposite. Choosing peace is not a new rule. It is a return to the first one.

I know that some will hear this and say it is naive. That the world is dangerous. That we must be ready to fight. I am not saying we will disarm unilaterally or pretend that conflict does not exist. I am saying that we will **name the condition.** We will not treat peace as something we get after we win. We will treat it as the state without which nothing we build can last. We will make the seam visible: the choice between seeing the other as face and seeing the other as category. And we will choose the face.

To the religious leaders of the world: your traditions already carry this. You have blessed armies and you have prayed for peace in the same breath. I am asking you to say, in your own language, that the killing of any human being delays what all of you say you are waiting for. One subject. Many tongues. We do not need one creed. We need one condition.

To the nations: I am not asking you to sign a treaty tonight. I am asking you to hear this. The United States, in this administration, will hold itself to the condition. We will not use the language of category when we can use the language of face. We will not choose the blade when we can choose the door.

And to every person listening: the good is in the living. Every one of you is that. The condition is not a demand from above. It is the offer that has always been there. Stop. See the face. Put down the blade. See what happens.

This is the first word of this administration. Not the last. There will be much to do — laws, borders, interests, disputes. But the first word is this: **Peace is the condition. We choose it. The door is open.**

Thank you. God bless you. And may we meet the condition.
