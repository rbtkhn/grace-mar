### MS-001 — `christianity-islam-civilizational-bridge`

- **Goal (one line):** Advance a **geopolitical vision** where Christian-majority and Muslim-majority civilizations move toward **peaceful unity** by growing a **shared moral and spiritual vocabulary** in public life—alongside **steady coexistence**, **fair dealing**, and **dignity**—**not** erasing faith or history.
- **Why (link to values / IX alignment):** Strategy work already orients toward **long horizons** and **cross-civilizational** judgment; this moonshot names a **north star** for that work—**restraint over permanent enmity**, **institutions that outlast flare-ups**, and **truth-telling** about harm and hope on all sides.
- **Articulation (operator choices):**
  - **What “unify” means first:** **Shared moral and spiritual vocabulary** among elites and publics (treaties and money can follow; they are not the first definition).
  - **First geographic lens:** **West Asia / Eastern Mediterranean** (not the only region forever).
  - **Held tension (left open on purpose):** **Realist power** versus **moral aspiration**.
  - **Failure mode to refuse:** **Utopian sloganeering** with **no contact with real constraints**.
  - **Primary bridge domain:** **Sacred sites, pilgrimage, and symbolic recognition** (status and dignity).
  - **Second bridge domain:** **TBD** (pick one later—e.g. education and memory, arts and translation, diplomacy).
  - **Voice:** **Cold-water realist** with **moral guardrails** (plain facts first; avoid preachy heat).
  - **Success shape (12–24 months):** At least one **policy-adjacent** **brief or memo** aimed at people who **steer institutional attention** (not only private notes).
  - **Tie to strategy work:** Use as **fuel for expert threads**; apply **tri-frame** only when you **explicitly** call for it (not the default on every strategy pass).
- **Success metrics (staging):**
  - **Policy-adjacent output:** **One** brief or memo per **quarter** that could sit in front of an attention-holding reader (format can be simple; clarity matters).
  - **Constraints contact:** Each artifact lists **who has power**, **what they want**, and **what could break** if the vision ignores friction.
  - **Humility:** Name **limits**—what you cannot do alone, and where **locals and experts** must lead.
  - **Anti-failure guard:** If a draft reads like **cheap hope** with no **trade-offs**, **stop** and revise.
- **Next experiment / step:** Draft **one short, scoped** piece (outline or one page) on **sacred sites, pilgrimage, or symbolic dignity** in the **Eastern Mediterranean / West Asia** lens—**cite** sources where claims are heavy. Use it to test the bridge domain before a wider sweep.
