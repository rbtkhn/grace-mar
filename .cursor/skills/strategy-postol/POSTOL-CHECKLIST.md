# Postol-style technical pass — checklist

Use when stress-testing **missiles, missile defense, drones, nuclear latency**, or **urban effects** claims in **operator WORK** copy — whether the source is Postol or someone echoing similar arguments. **Corpus:** [analyst-corpus INDEX](../../../research/external/work-strategy/analyst-corpus/INDEX.md) (`theodore-postol` rows) + per-file **Perceiver** / hooks in `research/external/work-strategy/transcripts/theodore-postol-*.md`.

---

## Delivery and accuracy

- [ ] Separate **guidance** (knowing position) from **control** (stable body attitude) for **terminal phase** claims.
- [ ] If citing **accuracy** (e.g. CEP, km vs hundreds of meters): what **missile family**, what **event** (e.g. dated strike), what **method** (impact points, telemetry, or assertion only)?
- [ ] Older **tumbling / breakup** vs **stable re-entry + terminal motor** — do not merge generations without labeling.

## Missile defense (BMD)

- [ ] **Intercept rate** claims: treat as **hypothesis** until **primary** or **independent BDA** supports them; note **survivorship** in open video (absence of intercept flash ≠ proof of zero intercepts globally).
- [ ] **System vs role:** **Iron Dome** vs **Patriot** vs **THAAD** vs **Arrow** — which threat class each is **designed** for; avoid “one score” for all.
- [ ] **Cost exchange:** **drone** vs **interceptor** — flag **contested** unit costs; cite **who** published **which** figure.

## Sensor chain and warning

- [ ] Draw a **one-paragraph chain:** **early warning → cueing → tracking → fire control**. If **radars** are degraded, which **link** breaks first?
- [ ] **Satellite launch detection** vs **terminal tracking** — do not treat **plume warning** as **precision track**.
- [ ] **Warning time** (e.g. ~2 min vs none): tie to **which radar class** and **geometry**, not generic “Israel.”

## Drones and commercial stack

- [ ] **GNSS precision** (e.g. BeiDou vs GPS civilian): distinguish **technical capability** from **receiver availability**, **jamming**, and **policy**.
- [ ] **Starlink / commercial C2:** distinguish **what is physically possible** from **operator policy, ToS, sanctions, and reported enforcement** — verify current **open** reporting before asserting **ubiquitous** adversary use.
- [ ] **Low RCS / bird clutter:** note **radar mode** and **signal processing** limits as **contested** terrain, not settled by analogy alone.

## Tunnels and underground posture

- [ ] **Entrance strike ≠ facility killed** — **networked tunnels**, **decoys**, **re-sealing** after blast.
- [ ] **Launch from tunnel** vs **production depth** — same checklist, different verification targets.

## Named studies

- [ ] If citing **RUSI** or any table (e.g. **interceptor depletion** dates): open **primary** publication, confirm **date**, **assumptions**, and **that the row matches** the speaker’s excerpt.

## Nuclear (when adjacent)

- [ ] **Material + latency** vs **deployed arsenal** — do not collapse **breakout** claims into **deterrent** without **delivery + command** discussion (see separate Postol nuclear transcripts in corpus).

## Scenario vs forecast

- [ ] Label **illustrative** **effects** or **targeting narratives** as **scenario**, not **prediction**.
- [ ] **Retaliation / deterrence** logic: separate **physics** from **doctrine** and **political decision**.

## ASR / quote hygiene

- [ ] Normalize **Theodore Postol** (not “Apostol”); **BeiDou** (not “BYU”) in formal cites when quoting from captions.
